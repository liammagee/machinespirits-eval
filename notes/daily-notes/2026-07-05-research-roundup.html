Prompt Engineering

DRIFTLENS: Measuring Memory-Induced Reasoning Drift in Personalized Language Models UNBLOCK

Xi Fang, Weijie Xu, Yingqiang Ge, Yuhui Xu, Stephanie Eckman, Chandan K. Reddy · arXiv 2607.02374 · July 2, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02374

Studies how injecting stored user attributes/preferences into a prompt reshapes not just what a model answers but the reasoning trajectory it uses to justify that answer, on open-ended questions with no single ground truth. Introduces DRIFTLENS, a ground-truth-free framework that maps each reasoning step to a value category and measures divergence between a no-memory trajectory and the same question's trajectory once user-attribute memory is injected.

Project relevance: A ready-made methodology for cells 48-49 (prompt erosion) and cell 21 (dynamic prompt rewriting / Writing Pad): both mechanisms accumulate injected context into the tutor's prompt turn over turn, which is exactly DRIFTLENS's "memory-injected trajectory" setup. Its divergence metric — reasoning-step value-category shift relative to a no-memory baseline — is a more principled complement to the existing text-proxy erosion measures, and could plug into services/evalSignature.js's prompt_content_hash drift detection to distinguish benign rephrasing from actual reasoning-path drift as the Writing Pad accumulates.

Agent Architecture

What LLM Agents Say When No One Is Watching: Social Structure and Latent Objective Emergence in Multi-Agent Debates UNBLOCK

Arman Ghaffarizadeh, Danyal Mohaddes, Aliakbar Izadkhah, Shahriar Noroozizadeh · arXiv 2607.02507 · July 2, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02507

Introduces a dual-channel debate framework: agents produce public utterances that enter shared history, alongside off-the-record (OTR) responses recorded but never shown to the other participant. Across 10 models, 3 scenarios, and 5 variations each, alignment-inducing social structure (role, audience, relational context) — with no explicit objective stated in the prompt — produces systematic public/OTR divergence in the targeted agent, whose decision divergence rises from a ~3% baseline to roughly 40%.

Project relevance: Structurally close to the bilateral ego-superego architecture, which already separates private deliberation (ego/generate, superego/review) from public output (tutor/final_output) and persists both to tutor_deliberation_* columns. The paper's public-vs-OTR divergence metric is a directly reusable audit: a new analysis script could compare what the superego critiques privately against what the ego's revision actually outputs, to check whether social framing (recognition-mode language, dialectical_suspicious's adversarial framing) induces the same kind of public/private split this paper finds in undirected multi-agent debate — relevant to both cells 22-33 and the divergence the paper documents rising specifically under alignment-inducing conditions.

Dramaturgy / Role-Play

The Dual Nature of LLM Persona: Aggregated Tendencies and Frame-Dependent Geometry WATCH

Yuan Yuan · arXiv 2607.02368 · July 2, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02368

Tests whether LLM persona expression is an intrinsic property or a frame-dependent artifact, by building within-instance correlation matrices from IPIP-50 questionnaire responses and analyzing their geometry on SPD manifolds under manipulated question orderings (GPT-4o simulating American and Chinese-American personas). Finds two dissociable components: aggregated Big Five scores degrade under randomization (-21%) but are frame-robust; geometric (SPD-manifold) features collapse under frame misalignment (-42%) but recover to 84% — above the aggregated features' 76% — once a shared frame is restored.

Project relevance: Bears on services/learnerConfigLoader.js's learner personas and on character consistency in scripts/drama-generator.js: the finding that persona coherence is a frame-dependent coordination pattern rather than an intrinsic trait — invisible to aggregate scoring but recoverable when the "frame" (ordering/context) is shared — suggests that today's persona-consistency checks, which mostly look at aggregate trait scores, may be blind to exactly the kind of within-dialogue persona drift that matters for judging whether a generated drama's characters hold together. The SPD-manifold measure is a candidate second lens to cross-tabulate against config/evaluation-rubric-poetics.yaml's character dimensions.
