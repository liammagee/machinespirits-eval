Agent Architecture & Memory

PerspectiveGap: A Benchmark for Multi-Agent Orchestration Prompting UNBLOCK

(Authors not confirmed in search results) · arXiv 2606.08878 · June 7, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.08878

PerspectiveGap tests 27 commercial models on 110 orchestration-prompting scenarios spanning 10 multi-agent topologies; average pass rate is only 14.9% (GPT-5.5: 62%, Claude Opus 4.7 notably weak despite strong coding scores). The core finding: writing prompts that correctly assign perspective (what each sub-agent needs to know, vs. what it must not see) is a distinct capability under-proxied by general benchmarks.

Project relevance: The "Prompt Economy" principle (loop-centred orchestrations that minimise role/engineering overhead) is a direct frame for auditing the ego-superego cells: does the superego prompt contain only what the superego needs, or is there bleed-through of ego-level context? The benchmark's leakage metric — GPT-5.5 49.1% vs. average 246.5% — is a concrete diagnostic the project could apply to prompts/tutor-superego*.md to test whether superego prompts inadvertently expose ego-level reasoning. Could unblock the superego ablation work (cells 93–100).

Agent Memory: Characterization and System Implications of Stateful Long-Horizon Workloads WATCH

Yasmine Omri, Ziyu Gan, Zachary Broveak, Robin Geens, Zexue He, Alex Pentland, Marian Verhelst, Tsachy Weissman, Thierry Tambe · arXiv 2606.06448 · June 4, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06448

The first systems-level taxonomy of agent memory, classifying existing architectures along four axes: storage medium (parametric / external KV / vector / structured), retrieval mechanism (flat / LLM-mediated / graph traversal / agentic control), update strategy, and scope (session / cross-session / world model). Identifies "stateful long-horizon workloads" as a distinct systems problem requiring new profiling methodology.

Project relevance: Maps cleanly onto the adaptive runner's state schema (cells 110–113): the stateSchema.js design straddles "flat retrieval" and "agentic control" in the paper's taxonomy, which may explain the latency and cost behaviour seen in smoke runs. The update-strategy axis is directly applicable to the otherEgoProfiling mechanism (cells 54–59): the paper provides vocabulary to distinguish append-only profiling from summarization-with-loss.

Prompt Engineering & Context Architecture

From Prompts to Context: An Ontology-Driven Framework for Human-Generative AI Collaboration UNBLOCK

Ngọc Luyện Lê, Marie-Hélène Abel, Bertrand Laforge · arXiv 2605.29675 · May 28, 2026 · also in Information Systems Frontiers (Springer) · https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.29675

Introduces the Contextual Collaboration AI Ontology (CCAI), an OWL vocabulary that models tasks, agent roles, resources, and constraints as machine-interpretable entities. Populated CCAI instances are combined with SPARQL-based retrieval to turn otherwise ephemeral prompt-response pairs into structured, queryable collaboration traces. Addresses the provenance gap: after a dialogue, it is usually impossible to reconstruct who did what, with which resource, under which constraint.

Project relevance: This is a near-exact formal treatment of what services/evalSignature.js does informally via config_hash / dialogue_content_hash / prompt_content_hash. CCAI could serve as a reference ontology for formalising the eval pipeline's provenance columns — and as a vocabulary for describing the ego/superego role split in Paper 2.0's architecture section. The SPARQL-query model also suggests a path toward querying the evaluation DB with ontology-grounded filters rather than raw SQL (relevant to /ms-query-db).

Influencing LLM Multi-Agent Dialogue via Policy-Parameterized Prompts WATCH

Hongbo Bo, Jingyu Hu, Weiru Liu (University of Bristol) · arXiv 2603.09890 · March 10, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09890

Treats prompts as first-class actions in a state-action policy: a five-component prompt constructor (role, goal, context, constraints, output format) reads the current dialogue state and dynamically instantiates the next prompt without any model fine-tuning. Dialogue quality is evaluated on five behavioural indicators: responsiveness, rebuttal, evidence usage, non-repetition, and stance shift.

Project relevance: The five-indicator set (responsiveness, rebuttal, evidence use, non-repetition, stance shift) is more behaviourally specific than the dialogue quality rubric's current dimensions and could extend config/evaluation-rubric-dialogue.yaml with process-level metrics. The state-action prompt policy is structurally equivalent to the adaptive runner's policyActions.js — reading this paper is useful background before the next iteration of the A13 cells (111–113).

Ontology / Knowledge Representation

Toward Pre-Deployment Assurance for Enterprise AI Agents: Ontology-Grounded Simulation and Trust Certification WATCH

Thanh Luong Tuan (Golden Gate University), Abhijit Sanyal (Novartis) · arXiv 2606.04037 · June 3, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.04037

Proposes an Agent Operational Envelope (AOE) ontology formalising certification space across permissions, domain constraints, safety properties, governance rules, and autonomy levels. An automated pipeline derives regulatory, operational, and adversarial test scenarios from the AOE; a resulting Trust Certificate carries machine-verifiable graduated verdicts (Approved / Conditional / Rejected).

Project relevance: The AOE's five certification dimensions map structurally onto the poetics rubric (config/evaluation-rubric-poetics.yaml): each of the six Aristotle-derived dimensions could be formalised as an ontology class with associated SHACL shapes, enabling automated rubric validation and provenance-checked certification of tutor quality. The "adversarial scenario generation" component is also directly applicable to the trap-scenario suite (cells 110–125), where scenario design is currently manual.

Rhetoric & Persuasion

Political Persuasion and Endorsement in Large Language Models WATCH

Alessia Antelmi, Alessia Galdeman, Lucio La Cava, Arianna Pera, Giovanni Da San Martino · arXiv 2606.05961 · June 5, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05961

Tests six LLMs (diverse geographic origins) on real-world media content annotated with persuasion techniques, measuring endorsement via five-point Likert scale. Without political conditioning, LLMs generally do not endorse persuasive messages — but partisan persona prompting significantly modulates endorsement, with large model-level variance.

Project relevance: The recognition-theoretic tutor prompts are, in effect, persona prompts that may bias the tutor toward endorsing certain learning moves. This paper provides a controlled methodology for testing whether the recognition framing inflates positive assessments in the ego's self-evaluation — a confound not currently controlled for in the rubric v2.2 design. Also relevant to the placebo cells (15–18): the placebo condition removes Hegelian theory but retains "best-practice" framing that may itself be a subtle persona prompt.

Pedagogy & Tutoring Agents

Identifying High-Confidence Social Biases in LLMs for Trustworthy Conversational Tutoring Agents WATCH

Aitor Arronte Alvarez, Naiyi Xie Fincham (University of Hawaii at Manoa) · arXiv 2606.01584 · June 1, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.01584

Evaluates LLMs in authentic student–tutor language-learning conversations, finding that bias detection is substantially harder in conversational tutoring contexts than in standard benchmarks, and that current SOTA models are overconfident in incorrect bias assessments — they fail to flag stereotypical bias while expressing high confidence.

Project relevance: The judge model in the evaluation pipeline (Claude Sonnet / Opus) faces the same overconfidence risk: when the tutor response contains subtle recognition-theory cues, the judge may be systematically over-generous. The paper's bias-detection methodology (regenerating dialogues while preserving linguistic structure, then testing judge responses) is a direct template for a new eval-cli.js calibrate sub-command that stress-tests the judge against bias-injected transcripts.
