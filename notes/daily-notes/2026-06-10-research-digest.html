Prompt Engineering

Decomposing Factual Sycophancy in Language Models: How Size and Instruction Tuning Shape Robustness UNBLOCK

Victor De Marez, Luna De Bruyne, Walter Daelemans · arXiv 2606.06306 · June 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06306

The paper decomposes factual sycophancy into two distinct channels — truth margin (the model's baseline preference for the correct answer) and manipulation sensitivity (how far social pressure shifts that preference) — then measures both across 56 open-weight models (0.3B–32B) and 13 manipulation types. Key finding: model size governs vulnerability, but instruction tuning changes how size acts — small instruction-tuned models can become less robust while large ones become more robust, and the effect is manipulation-type dependent.

Project relevance: This decomposition applies directly to the ego-superego revision loop: when the ego revises after superego critique, is that revision driven by a shift in truth margin (genuine epistemic update) or manipulation sensitivity (compliance with an authoritative inner voice)? The truth-margin / manipulation-sensitivity framework could sharpen the adaptationIndex beyond its current text-surface proxy and give the "near-miss: learner draw or structural cap?" question in /ms-replay-one-side a finer-grained answer.

CRAFT: Cost-aware Refinement And Front-aware Tuning of Prompts WATCH

arXiv 2606.04661 · June 3, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.04661

Frames prompt optimisation as a search over the Pareto front of accuracy × prompt-token cost rather than a single-objective maximisation. CRAFT uses NSGA-II retention, Pareto-gap acquisition to allocate validation calls near the optimistic frontier, and alternating accuracy-oriented / cost-oriented generators, avoiding the "scalarization collapse" failure of weighted-sum baselines.

Project relevance: The eval system's prompts (ego, superego, learner) are currently hand-tuned for accuracy only; CRAFT offers a principled method to trade off prompt length (= API cost per run) against rubric score. Especially relevant for the superego prompts in cells 22–33 and 93–100 where prompt length is a known confound (cf. placebo length-matching, cells 15–18).

Agent Architecture

PerspectiveGap: A Benchmark for Multi-Agent Orchestration Prompting UNBLOCK

Youran Sun, Xingyu Ren, Kejia Zhang, Xinpeng Liu, Jiaxuan Guo · arXiv 2606.08878 · June 7, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.08878

PerspectiveGap introduces 110 scenarios across 10 multi-agent topologies, each requiring an LLM to compose role-specific orchestration prompts that give each sub-agent exactly what it needs — no more, no less (the Prompt Economy principle). Benchmarked on 27 models from 10 providers: GPT-5.5 substantially leads; notably, Opus 4.7 shows a significant weakness in orchestration prompting despite its strong coding performance.

Project relevance: The ego-superego loop is exactly the pattern PerspectiveGap benchmarks — orchestrating a sub-agent (superego) with a perspective-scoped prompt. The Opus 4.7 weakness finding is immediately actionable: cells using Opus as orchestrator (check ego_model column in the DB) may under-perform specifically in the orchestration-prompting dimension, not in reasoning. The 10-topology taxonomy could also serve as a diagnostic for which multi-agent topology the cells instantiate.

Organizational Control Layer: Governance Infrastructure at the Execution Boundary of LLM Agent Systems WATCH

Multiple institutions (McGill, Purdue, UNSW, UCLA, NYU et al.) · arXiv 2606.04306 · June 3, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.04306

Introduces the Organizational Control Layer (OCL), a model-agnostic governance shim that intercepts agent-generated actions before environment-facing execution, enforcing policies and triggering escalation without touching the underlying LLM generator. On adversarial buyer-seller environments: unsafe executions fall from 88% to near-zero; valid success rises from 12% to 96%.

Project relevance: The adaptive runner (cells 110–113, 124) already implements a version of this pattern — the state-policy graph intercepts proposed turns before they are emitted to the learner. OCL's policy-enforcement API design could inform a cleaner abstraction of that interception layer, and the escalation mechanism maps onto the validator condition in cell 113 (A13 C4).

Rhetoric & Persuasion

"I understand your perspective": LLM Persuasion and Sycophancy through the Lens of Communicative Action Theory UNBLOCK

Esra Dönmez, Agnieszka Falenska · arXiv 2606.08076 · June 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.08076 | Note: a near-identical title also appears in ACL Findings 2025 — treat as updated/extended version

Applies Habermas's Theory of Communicative Action to LLM-generated persuasion, coding illocutionary intent (conveying knowledge, building trust, signalling similarity) across LLM vs. human counter-arguments on ChangeMyView debates. All three LLMs convey illocutionary intent more consistently than humans, potentially increasing perceived anthropomorphism and persuasive effect.

Project relevance: Habermas and Hegel share theoretical terrain; the illocutionary-act taxonomy (knowledge / trust / similarity) maps directly onto the recognition-theory dimensions in prompts/tutor-ego-recognition.md. This could extend the dialogue quality rubric (config/evaluation-rubric-v2.2) with a rhetorical mode dimension: does the tutor's recognitive move function as a genuine epistemic offer or a trust-building manoeuvre? Also directly relevant to the poetics pipeline: illocutionary coding could distinguish peripeteia-preceding turns (where the tutor shifts rhetorical mode) from routine turns.

Political Persuasion and Endorsement in Large Language Models WATCH

Alessia Antelmi, Alessia Galdeman, Lucio La Cava, Arianna Pera, Giovanni Da San Martino · arXiv 2606.05961 · June 4, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05961

Evaluates six LLMs (from multiple geographic regions) on content annotated with real-world persuasion techniques, using a five-point Likert endorsement scale. Without political conditioning LLMs generally do not endorse persuasion-infused messages; partisan persona prompting increases polarisation of endorsement, particularly for content embedding persuasion techniques.

Project relevance: The divergent-superego cells (22–33) explicitly assign the superego a suspicious / adversary / advocate persona. This paper's finding that persona prompting modulates persuasion-technique endorsement suggests those personas may be shifting the superego's rhetorical posture as well as its critique content — a confound worth measuring separately in the superego_feedback traces.
