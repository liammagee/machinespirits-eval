Prompt Engineering

PerspectiveGap: A Benchmark for Multi-Agent Orchestration Prompting UNBLOCK

Youran Sun, Xingyu Ren, Kejia Zhang, Xinpeng Liu, Jiaxuan Guo · arXiv 2606.08878 · June 7, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.08878

PerspectiveGap evaluates LLMs' ability to write the orchestration prompt that each sub-agent in a multi-agent pipeline receives — the "what does this role need to know" problem. 110 scenarios across 10 real-world topologies, tested on 27 commercial models from 10 companies; GPT-5.5 dominates, while Opus 4.7 shows a surprising weakness despite strong coding scores. The benchmark formalises the Prompt Economy principle: loop-centred orchestrations that maximise utility with minimal role and engineering overhead.

Project relevance: The orchestration-prompting framing maps directly onto how cells 3–4, 7–8, and 22–33 pass context from the ego turn to the superego turn (and back). PerspectiveGap's failure modes — agents receiving too much or too little shared context — are precisely the failure modes the project has observed in cells with high tutor_deliberation_* latency. The benchmark is a ready-made evaluation harness for stress-testing the ego→superego handoff prompt; running it on current cells before the next multi-agent prompt revision would be a concrete unblocking step.

Reasoning Structure of Large Language Models WATCH

Frédéric Berdoz, Luca A. Lanzendörfer, Fabian Farestam, Roger Wattenhofer (ETH Zurich) · arXiv 2606.03883 · June 2, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03883

Introduces a pipeline that converts unstructured chain-of-thought traces into verifiable reasoning graphs — directed graphs of claims and dependencies — and a benchmark of logic puzzles designed so graph structure can be checked mechanically. The key finding: models with identical final-answer accuracy and token counts can have fundamentally different reasoning structures; accuracy alone is an unreliable proxy for reasoning quality.

Project relevance: The dialogue-trace analyser (services/dialogueTraceAnalyzer.js) and analyze-mechanism-traces.js both operate on text-surface signals (RevΔ, EgoSpec). Converting ego/superego deliberation turns into claim-dependency graphs would give a structural complement to these surface metrics — the graph topology could distinguish genuine pedagogical reasoning from verbose but shallow responses, a known gap in the current rubric v2.2 scoring.

Agent Architecture

A Motivational Architecture for Conversational AGI UNBLOCK

Anna Mikeda (Glass Umbrella), Ben Goertzel (SingularityNet) · arXiv 2606.05411 · June 3, 2026 · Accepted at AGI-26 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05411

Recasts motivational homeostasis in dialogue-native terms: instead of regulating bodily needs, a conversational agent regulates competence, uncertainty reduction, affiliation, affinity, legitimacy, nurturing, and aesthetic coherence. Built on the OpenPsi motivational lineage + MetaMo scaffold, the paper proposes a ten-stage motivational processing pipeline separating cognitive modulation from situational appraisal, and a dual decision strategy blending urgency-driven fast response with deliberative multi-goal optimisation. Specialised to CompanionAgent and ResearchAgent examples.

Project relevance: The seven dialogue-native motivational dimensions are a near-exact vocabulary match for the Hegelian recognition dimensions in the project's prompts (legitimacy ≈ recognition of autonomous subject; affiliation ≈ intersubjective bond; nurturing ≈ pedagogical care). The ten-stage pipeline is structurally analogous to the ego→superego→revision loop. This paper offers a formal motivational grounding for the current recognition-enhanced prompts that could sharpen the theoretical framing in Paper 2.0 §3–4, and provides a ready vocabulary for describing what the superego is actually optimising.

Dramaturgy / Theory of Mind

GroupToM-Bench: Benchmarking Group Theory of Mind and Nonlinear Social Emergence in MLLMs UNBLOCK

Weidong Tang, Jierui Li, Yueling Hou, Zihan Mei, Can Zhang, Xinyan Wan, Zhiyuan Liang, Pengfei Zhou, Yang You, Wangbo Zhao (Xidian U / NUS / UESTC / USTC) · arXiv 2606.04184 · June 2, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.04184

The first multimodal benchmark for group-level Theory of Mind. Built around a three-level causal chain: micro (BDI states — belief, desire, intention), meso (group tension and structural constraints), and macro (outcome prediction and mechanistic attribution). Key finding: collective behaviour emerges non-linearly from social tensions and conformity dynamics; it cannot be recovered by summing individual-level ToM scores. Current frontier MLLMs fail substantially at group-level tasks despite near-human individual-level ToM.

Project relevance: Cells 115–123 are the project's bilateral-ToM / P2.1–P2.2 ablation suite. GroupToM-Bench's three-level causal chain (BDI → group tension → outcome) is a principled scaffold for the drama-machine's slot model: the peripeteia moment in poetics rubric maps onto the "group tension" meso level, and anagnorisis onto mechanistic attribution at the macro level. The non-linearity finding also explains why cell 114 (dialogue-engine baseline) underperforms cells 110–113 on trap scenarios — the standard runner lacks the group-dynamics layer entirely. Adapting GroupToM's micro/meso/macro decomposition as an auxiliary scoring dimension in config/evaluation-rubric-poetics.yaml is a concrete next step.

Rhetoric & Argumentation

Rhetorical Questions in LLM Representations: A Linear Probing Study WATCH

Louie Hong Yao, Vishesh Anand, Yuan Zhuang, Tianyu Jiang · arXiv 2604.14128 · April 15, 2026 (v2: April 21) · https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14128

Linear probing study showing that rhetorical questions (asked to persuade or signal stance, not to seek information) are linearly separable from genuine information-seeking questions within LLM representation space, with cross-dataset AUROC 0.7–0.8. The rhetorical signal emerges early in the network and is most stably captured by last-token representations. However, probes trained on different data distributions produce substantially different rankings (overlap among top-ranked instances <0.2), implying heterogeneous rhetorical strategies rather than a single latent dimension.

Project relevance: The project's rhetoric workstream (notes/rhetoric/) and the analyze-recognition-lexicon.js script currently work at token surface level. This paper suggests that the tutor's Hegelian "recognition moves" — which are typically rhetorical (acknowledging the learner as subject, not requesting factual answers) — may be detectable via last-token probes at much lower cost than full rubric scoring. The heterogeneous-strategies finding (low probe overlap) is also a warning: a single "recognition score" may be conflating distinct rhetorical modes, which aligns with the multi-dimensional design of rubric v2.2.
