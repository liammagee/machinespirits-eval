LLM Pedagogy & Tutoring

Measuring Whether LLM Tutors Teach or Solve: A Diagnostic for Educational Impact UNBLOCK

Junyi Yao, Zihao Zheng, Baichuan Li (Washington University in St. Louis / Southern Methodist University) · arXiv 2606.16206 · June 15, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16206

The paper shows that task-solving ability and learning-supportive behavior are only weakly correlated (r = 0.421 across 8 models on MathTutorBench), with several models shifting meaningfully in rank when evaluation switches from the solving composite to the pedagogy composite. The proposed diagnostic is a lightweight gap measure between the two composites, applicable to any tutoring benchmark without re-collecting data.

Project relevance: This is the cleanest operationalisation yet of what the v2.2 rubric is trying to separate across its eight dimensions — the paper makes the solving/teaching split explicit and measurable. The diagnostic gap metric could be computed directly from existing evaluation_results rows (compare tutor_first_turn_score on answer-production scenarios vs. guided-discovery scenarios), and would immediately test whether cells with richer superego mechanisms (e.g. cells 22–33) score higher on the pedagogy composite without gaining on solving. This could sharpen the paper's Finding 9 / §6.5 argument.

LectūraAgents: A Multi-Agent Framework for Adaptive Personalized AI-Assisted Learning and Embodied Teaching WATCH

Jaward Sesay, Yue Yu, Siwei Dong, Yemin Shi, Guangyao Chen, Börje F. Karlsson (Beijing Institute of Technology / Peking University / Cornell / BAAI) · arXiv 2606.16428 · June 15, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16428

LectūraAgents is a hierarchical multi-agent architecture in which a ProfessorAgent coordinates a team of specialised subordinate agents (research, planning, review, delivery) and dynamically adapts lecture content and embodied delivery actions (handwriting, highlighting) to the learner's inferred needs. The framework connects personalised content generation with adaptive embodied delivery end-to-end.

Project relevance: The ProfessorAgent-over-subagents pattern is a vertical decomposition of what this project achieves horizontally across ego / superego turns. The review subordinate agent's role — checking draft lecture content before delivery — is structurally identical to the superego function, but is modelled as a distinct agent rather than an inner voice; comparing performance on the same scenarios could help separate the architectural contribution from the prompt-content contribution. Also relevant as a comparison point for cells 80–92 (messages-mode, multi-turn).

Prompt Engineering

Environment-Grounded Automated Prompt Optimization for LLM Game Agents WATCH

Rean Clive Fernandes, Lukas Fehring, Theresa Eimer, Marius Lindauer, Matthias Feurer · arXiv 2606.17838 · June 16, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17838

The paper proposes an automated prompt-optimisation loop for LLM agents in interactive environments, decomposing the observation-to-action pipeline into a goal-conditioned descriptor agent and an action selection agent, then iteratively refining each module's prompt via an LLM-driven evolutionary loop guided by environment returns. A behavior analyzer attributes episode outcomes to specific prompt components; a mutator proposes targeted revisions validated through rollouts.

Project relevance: This is a production-grade implementation of what npm run prompt-lab -- autotune approximates manually. The behavior-analyzer → mutator → rollout pipeline maps onto the eval loop: the evaluation rubric provides the return signal, each cell's ego/superego prompts are the modules, and the eval runner provides the rollout. Directly applicable as a blueprint for automating prompt evolution across the 125-cell factorial without hand-writing variants. The descriptor/action decomposition also echoes the ego/superego split.

Agent Architecture & Deliberation

LLM-as-an-Investigator: Evidence-First Reasoning for Robust Interactive Problem Diagnosis WATCH

Fabrizio Marozzo (University of Calabria) · Pietro Liò (University of Cambridge) · arXiv 2606.13220 · June 11, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13220

The paper introduces a Solution Investigator Agent that responds to incomplete or hypothesis-laden user descriptions by estimating problem ambiguity, generating candidate hypotheses, asking targeted clarification questions, and updating hypothesis probabilities after each answer — explicitly countering user-driven sycophancy (premature commitment to the user's frame before evidence is gathered).

Project relevance: The anti-sycophancy framing directly addresses the failure mode identified in 2606.00820 (prior roundup): whether superego critique produces genuine reasoning-induced persuasion or mere social compliance. The Bayesian hypothesis-update model is a formalisation of what the superego is supposed to do — maintain epistemic independence from the ego's initial framing. Applicable to superego prompt redesign for cells with configured superego (cells 3–4, 7–8, 22–33, 82–83, 86–89) and to the A13 validator cell (cell 113).

Philosophy / Recognition Theory

Prompt, negate, repeat: a Hegelian meditation on AI WATCH

Dwayne Woods · AI & Society, Vol. 41, pp. 3143–3157 (2026) · Springer Nature · https://doi.org/10.1007/s00146-025-02802-z
(Note: DOI path suggests accepted 2025, published in print 2026; not an arxiv preprint.)

Drawing on Hegel's Science of Logic, the paper argues that LLMs instantiate dialectical thinking in the Hegelian sense: each prompt is an abstract determination; the model's generation negates (particularises and complicates) that abstraction; iteration produces progressive concretion. The paper frames the recursiveness, contradiction resolution, and convergence of LLM outputs as structural analogues of Hegel's Aufhebung.

Project relevance: Provides explicit philosophical grounding for the ego-superego architecture: if the ego's initial generation is the abstract determination and the superego's critique is the negation, the revised output is the Aufhebung — preserved, cancelled, and elevated. This framing could strengthen §3 (Theoretical Framework) of docs/research/paper-full-2.0.md and sharpen the distinction between dialectical cells (28–33) and non-dialectical ones. Also relevant to the poetics arc's anagnorisis: the moment of recognition may be precisely the point at which the learner's initial abstract self-understanding is negated and concretised.
