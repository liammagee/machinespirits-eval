Pedagogy & Tutoring

Hey Chat, Can You Teach Me? Structuring Socratic Dialogue for Human Learning in the Wild UNBLOCK

Sidney Tio et al. · arXiv 2606.11744 · June 10, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11744

Addresses the gap between "chat with an LLM" and structured tutoring by building a prerequisite knowledge graph (subtopics as nodes, dependencies as edges) and framing tutoring as a graph-traversal planning problem — deciding which node to teach next and how many Socratic dialogue turns to budget per node before moving on. Frontier LLMs fail badly at this when prompted naively; the structured approach recovers meaningful curriculum sequencing and student-state inference over extended sessions.

Project relevance: This is the closest published analogue to the project's guided-discovery dialogue engine. The prerequisite-graph framing maps directly onto the suggestion-scenarios.yaml structure (each scenario = a node; the learner's impasse = an unexplored dependency). The student-state inference mechanism is also a structural match for the otherEgoProfiling cells (54–59): the "infer knowledge state from dialogue" loop is exactly what bidirectional profiling is trying to operationalise. The paper's failure analysis of unstructured LLM tutoring is directly relevant to the human-learner pilot (§A1 of TODO.md) — recommend reading before finalising the pilot protocol.

Prompt Engineering & Reasoning

The Periodic Table of LLM Reasoning: A Structured Survey of Reasoning Paradigms, Methods, and Failure Modes WATCH

Avinash Anand et al. (10 authors) · arXiv 2606.11470 · June 9, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11470

A structured taxonomy of 300+ reasoning papers organised like a periodic table: groups cover chain-of-thought, multi-hop, mathematical, common-sense, visual, code, retrieval-augmented, tool-augmented/agentic, and RL-based reasoning — each cell indexed by paradigm × method × failure mode. Key finding: agentic and tool-augmented reasoning share failure modes (planning horizon collapse, tool-noise sensitivity) that are distinct from pure CoT failures.

Project relevance: The taxonomy's "agentic reasoning" section (tool-augmented + RL) maps onto the adaptive runner (cells 110–113); the failure-mode catalogue is a candidate diagnostic checklist for the analyze-strategy-shift.js post-hoc analysis. The "retrieval-augmented" section may surface prompt strategies for the recognition prompts not yet tried in the ablation suite (cells 9–21). Low-effort read; high payoff as a reference frame.

Agent Architecture

Agent Planning Benchmark: A Diagnostic Framework for Planning Capabilities in LLM Agents WATCH

(Authors not confirmed in search results) · arXiv 2606.04874 · early June 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.04874

4,209 multimodal cases across 22 domains stress-test five planning settings: holistic planning, feedback-conditioned step-wise planning, tool-noise robustness, calibrated refusal (the agent should admit infeasibility), and inference-time refinement. Systematic weaknesses emerge in long-horizon planning and tool-noise conditions across all tested frontier models.

Project relevance: The adaptive runner (cells 110–113) is an LLM-based planner operating under the same long-horizon + tool-noise failure modes identified here. The "calibrated refusal" metric — does the agent know when the task is infeasible? — is directly relevant to the trap-scenario suite (cells 110–125), where the ideal tutor should recognise an unanswerable student impasse rather than confabulating. The benchmark design (22 domains × 5 settings) could also inform the cross-suite design (§6.8.7, cells 124–125).

Dramaturgy / Roleplay

RPGAgent: Driving Coherent Story-to-Play Generation with an LLM-Based Multi-Agent System WATCH

(CHI 2026) · ACM DL 10.1145/3772318.3790326 · CHI 2026 (spring 2026) · https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3772318.3790326

Specialised LLM agents (narrative, scene, mechanic) collaborate under the Elemental Tetrad framework to turn a designer's story outline into coherent playable game content — exchanging structured data to enforce consistency across narrative, visual, and gameplay layers. A within-subjects study (N=18) shows RPGAgent outperforms GPT-assisted solo generation on both user experience and creative satisfaction.

Project relevance: The specialised-agent-per-narrative-layer architecture is structurally analogous to the /ms-drama-machine slot model (tutor / learner / audience / judge roles). RPGAgent's consistency enforcement mechanism — agents exchange structured data rather than free text — is a candidate technique for tightening the adaptation-recognition loop, where the critic currently receives only free-form transcript prose. Also useful as a citation anchor for the "multi-agent dramaturgy" framing in Paper 2.0 §.

Narrative Theory-Driven LLM Methods for Automatic Story Generation and Understanding: A Survey WATCH

David Y. Liu, Aditya Joshi, Paul Dawson (University of New South Wales) · arXiv 2602.15851 · January 23, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.15851

Maps classical narrative theories (Propp, Freytag, Labov, Genette, schema theory) onto NLP pipeline components — dataset construction, prompting, fine-tuning, evaluation — and surveys how LLMs engage each. Key finding: LLMs make cross-disciplinary narrative integration easy but lack unified benchmarks; critic-level evaluation (does the generated story conform to narrative theory?) is the most open problem.

Project relevance: The "critic-level evaluation" gap is exactly the poetics arc's problem: config/evaluation-rubric-poetics.yaml operationalises Aristotle's peripeteia/anagnorisis as a scoring instrument, and this survey provides the scholarly grounding for why that instrument is novel. The Freytag/Labov narrative-arc taxonomies also offer alternative structural scaffolds for the adaptation-recognition loop's iteration taxonomy. Directly citable in Paper 2.0 §2 (related work) and §6.9 (poetics arc).

Ontology / Knowledge Representation

Automatic Ontology Construction Using LLMs as an External Layer of Memory, Verification, and Planning WATCH

(Authors not confirmed in search results) · arXiv 2604.20795 · April 22, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20795

Uses an LLM trio — one for memory (maintaining a structured RDF/OWL knowledge graph), one for verification (checking consistency of new assertions against existing axioms), one for planning (deciding when to extend vs. revise the ontology) — to construct and maintain a living domain ontology from unstructured text. Demonstrated on engineering / safety-critical domains.

Project relevance: The memory/verification/planning triad is structurally isomorphic to the ego/superego/revision loop: Ego ≈ memory (model of the learner), Superego ≈ verification (check against pedagogical axioms), revision ≈ planning (decide whether to extend or revise the approach). This framing offers formal vocabulary for cells 54–59 (otherEgoProfiling) and could anchor a more precise description of the bilateral-ToM cells (115–123) in Paper 2.0 §6.8.5.
