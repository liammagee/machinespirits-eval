Pedagogy / Tutoring Evaluation

From Solver to Tutor: Evaluating the Pedagogical Intelligence of LLMs with KMP-Bench UNBLOCK

(Authors: see PDF) · arXiv 2603.02775 · March 3, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.02775

KMP-Bench (Knowledge-Mediated Pedagogy Benchmark) assesses LLM tutoring quality by inserting models into truncated human dialogue snippets and scoring responses against six core pedagogical principles: Challenge, Explanation, Modelling, Practice, Questioning, and Feedback. The framing explicitly distinguishes solver competence (correct answers) from tutor competence (contextually appropriate pedagogical moves).

Project relevance: The six KMP dimensions could be mapped against the v2.2 tutor rubric's eight dimensions as an external validity check (both decompose pedagogical quality, but from different theoretical traditions). The "truncated dialogue" probing design is particularly relevant to the counterfactual replay infrastructure in /ms-replay-one-side and cell 110's LangGraph adaptive runner.

Experiential Reflective Learning for Self-Improving LLM Agents WATCH

(Authors: see PDF) · arXiv 2603.24639 · March 2026 · ICLR 2026 MemAgents Workshop · https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.24639

ERL agents generate generalised heuristics by reflecting on past experience across episodes, storing them in a retrievable memory, and revising them when subsequent experience contradicts them. Benchmarks show consistent gains over single-episode reflection (ReAct, Reflexion) on multi-step tasks.

Project relevance: The heuristic-generation loop resembles the adaptive runner's policy-update mechanism (cells 110–113). ERL's cross-episode memory is the missing piece for tutors that improve across learner interactions rather than within a single session — a natural next step after the within-session adaptationIndex.

Agent Architecture / Multi-Agent Persuasion

Strategic Persuasion with Trait-Conditioned Multi-Agent Systems for Iterative Legal Argumentation WATCH

(Authors: see PDF) · arXiv 2604.07028 · April 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07028

Prosecution and defence are modelled as heterogeneous teams of LLM agents; each team collectively generates arguments conditioned on shared strategic traits (e.g. aggressive, conciliatory) over multiple debate rounds. The trait-conditioning mechanism enables controlled variation in rhetorical style without fine-tuning.

Project relevance: The trait-conditioning approach is structurally close to the divergent superego family (cells 22–27: suspicious / adversary / advocate variants). The paper's evaluation of how trait-conditioned agents shift their argument style across rounds could inform a more principled design of the dialectical cells (28–33) and sharpen the hypothesis behind the dialectical_suspicious mechanism.

Rhetoric & Persuasion

Political Persuasion and Endorsement in Large Language Models WATCH

Labruna et al. · arXiv 2606.05961 · June 2026 · Findings of ACL: EACL 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05961

Systematically tests whether major LLMs endorse political positions and whether their outputs constitute persuasion under controlled conditions. Finds significant cross-model variation in both endorsement tendency and persuasive efficacy; smaller models are disproportionately persuasive in certain political domains.

Project relevance: The persuasion-efficacy measurement design is adjacent to the learnerGrowthIndex: both try to detect belief/stance change from LLM output. The cross-model variation finding is relevant for multi-judge IRR (the project's Opus vs. GPT rejudge infrastructure) — judge models may also vary in their persuasion susceptibility as scorers, not just their evaluation calibration.

Rhetorical Questions in LLM Representations: A Linear Probing Study WATCH

(Authors: see PDF) · arXiv 2604.14128 · April 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14128

Uses linear probes on intermediate LLM layer activations to test whether rhetorical questions (asked to persuade or signal stance, not to seek information) are represented distinctly from sincere questions. Finds that by mid-to-late layers, rhetorical and sincere questions are linearly separable, suggesting models internally track pragmatic intent before decoding.

Project relevance: Tutors using guided-discovery often pivot between sincere questions ("what do you notice?") and rhetorical prods ("isn't it the case that…?"). The probe methodology could be applied to tutor turns to measure the ratio of sincere-to-rhetorical questioning as a new process measure alongside adaptationIndex — and offers an eventual empirical hook for the Socratic stance assumed by the recognition-theory prompts.

Ontology / Knowledge Representation

Automatic Ontology Construction Using LLMs as an External Layer of Memory, Verification, and Planning WATCH

(Authors: see PDF) · arXiv 2604.20795 · April 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20795

Proposes a hybrid architecture in which LLMs are coupled with an external RDF/OWL ontology acting as a persistent, verifiable knowledge store. The LLM reads from and writes to the ontology via a structured query interface; a validation layer enforces ontological consistency before each write. Demonstrated on biomedical and enterprise knowledge construction tasks.

Project relevance: The poetics pipeline currently lacks a formal representation of the dramaturgical ontology (peripeteia, anagnorisis, hamartia, etc.) — rubric dimensions are defined in YAML but not as a machine-traversable graph. This architecture offers a concrete pattern for externalising config/evaluation-rubric-poetics.yaml into a queryable OWL ontology, enabling consistency checking across critic models and potential multi-hop reasoning over dramatic form.
