Agent Architecture

Who Broke the System? Failure Localization in LLM-Based Multi-Agent Systems UNBLOCK

Yufei Xia, Anjun Gao, Yueyang Quan, Zhuqing Liu, Minghong Fang · arXiv 2607.07989 · July 8, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07989

Introduces AgentLocate, a framework for attributing a multi-agent LLM system's failure to both a specific responsible agent and the earliest decisive step in its trajectory, rather than just flagging that a failure occurred somewhere in the pipeline. Outperforms existing failure-localization methods on identifying both the responsible agent and the failure step.

Project relevance: Directly applicable to the bilateral ego-superego architecture, which already separates private deliberation steps (ego/generate → superego/review → ego/revision) with symmetric labels on both tutor and learner sides and persists them to tutor_deliberation_* / learner_deliberation_* and deliberation_rounds. There is currently no automated pass that attributes a low-scoring turn to a specific step in that chain; AgentLocate's agent+step attribution method is a ready-made complement to analyze-mechanism-traces.js, and would be especially useful on cells 22-33 (divergent superego variants) and the adaptive trap suite (110-125), where the counterfactual-replay machinery in services/adaptiveTutor/ already isolates individual turns but doesn't yet attribute failure within a turn's deliberation chain.

Operational Reframing and Approval-Framed Delegation in Multi-Agent LLM Safety WATCH

Lifei Liu, Haoran Yu, Xiaochong Jiang, Su Wang, Pin Qian, Yihang Chen · arXiv 2607.07097 · July 8, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07097

Argues that safety evaluations of multi-agent LLM systems conflate three distinct mechanisms: harmful intent reframed as plausible operational work, a planner that refuses or transforms the request, and an executor that acts under delegation prompts implying prior approval. A five-condition controlled-contrast design across 30 synthetic harmful scenarios plus an external validation set (four agent-safety benchmarks, LLM-judged compliance) finds operational reframing is the most portable risk signal — raising compliance for GPT, Gemini, and DeepSeek, while Claude is comparatively resistant.

Project relevance: Structurally close to how the dialectical_suspicious/adversary/advocate superego variants (cells 22-33) and the id-director charisma family (101-109) work: a persona/framing layer changes what the downstream ego treats as "already approved." The paper's five-condition contrast design is a directly reusable template for testing whether recognition-mode or dialectical framing induces similar approval-implying compliance shifts in the ego's revision step, and — given the standing directive that nemotron/kimi nulls are stack-bounded — would be worth running cross-model (nemotron/kimi vs. the mandated codex.gpt-5.5/Sonnet 5 stack) to see whether the compliance-inflation effect the paper finds in GPT/Gemini/DeepSeek also shows up as a stack-specific artifact here.

Prompt Engineering

Prismata: Confining Cross-Site Prompt Injection in Web Agents WATCH

Corban Villa, Alp Eren Ozdarendeli, Sijun Tan, Raluca Ada Popa (UC Berkeley) · arXiv 2607.08147 · July 9, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08147

Addresses prompt injection in web agents by enforcing contextual least privilege: a dynamic trust-derivation step produces permission labels for page content with structural confinement guarantees, and a mechanical-confinement layer enforces those labels by redacting content and restricting agent capabilities — with no developer annotations required, so it scales to the long tail of websites.

Project relevance: Tangential — this project has no web-agent attack surface — but the underlying pattern (label untrusted injected content with derived trust/permission scope rather than trusting it uniformly) is conceptually adjacent to how cell 21's dynamic prompt rewriting (Writing Pad) and cells 48-49 (prompt erosion) accumulate injected context into the tutor's prompt turn over turn. Prismata's "no annotations required" trust-derivation could inform a lighter-weight provenance tag on Writing Pad injections, distinguishing tutor-authored context from learner-sourced content, for services/evalSignature.js's existing prompt_content_hash drift detection. Flagged WATCH rather than UNBLOCK since the fit requires real adaptation, not direct reuse.

Rhetoric

Adversarial Social Epistemology for Assemblies of Humans and Large Language Models UNBLOCK

Mihnea C. Moldoveanu, Joel A.C. Baum · arXiv 2607.07760 · July 8, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07760

Outlines an adversarial social epistemology for communicative landscapes where public assertions are scaffolded by chains of testimony, inference, institutional certification, and tacit trust. In such landscapes, agents have incentives and affordances to distort, color, omit, fabricate, or strategically under-specify information for private, reputational, rhetorical, or material gain — phenomena the authors argue existing accounts of epistemic bubbles, echo chambers, or misinformation diffusion don't adequately capture. Provides vocabulary for analyzing how agents exploit the commitments that normally make scaffolded assertions trustworthy, plus machinery for auditing and redressing trust breaches.

Project relevance: A close theoretical match for the suspicious/adversary/advocate superego variants (cells 22-27, 28-33) and the dialectical_suspicious mechanism (cells 93-100), whose entire design premise is a superego agent that must catch an ego distorting, omitting, or strategically under-specifying pedagogical claims. This paper's distort/color/omit/fabricate/under-specify taxonomy is a more precise vocabulary than the project currently uses to describe what those superego variants are meant to detect, and its trust-breach auditing machinery is a candidate lens for the poetics rubric (config/evaluation-rubric-poetics.yaml) — dramatic recognition (anagnorisis) is, in this paper's terms, the moment a scaffolded assertion's trust breach is finally exposed, which could sharpen how the poetics pipeline scores whether a dialogue's peripeteia genuinely surfaces a prior distortion versus merely gesturing at one.
