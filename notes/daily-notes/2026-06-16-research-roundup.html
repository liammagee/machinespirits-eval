Agent Architecture & Multi-Agent Deliberation

ARMOR-MAD: Adaptive Routing for Heterogeneous Multi-Agent Debate in Large Language Model Reasoning WATCH

Fuqiang Niu, Bowen Zhang · arXiv 2606.13197 · 11 Jun 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13197v1

ARMOR-MAD combines three routing mechanisms: Pre-debate Agreement Routing (PAR) decides whether a Round-0 multi-agent agreement already renders debate unnecessary; Early Agreement Stopping Evaluator (EASE) halts ongoing debate the moment agents converge; and Semantic Outlier Detection (SOD) down-weights abnormal final answers during aggregation. Fixed debate pipelines are replaced by a dynamic topology that skips deliberation when it adds no value.

Project relevance: The PAR + EASE logic maps directly onto the superego-bypass question: cells with a configured superego currently run a full ego↔superego exchange every turn regardless of convergence. ARMOR-MAD's pre-debate agreement check is a concrete, publishable mechanism for skipping that exchange when the ego's Round-0 output already satisfies the superego's criteria — reducing token cost for cells 3–4, 7–8, 22–33, 82–83, 86–89. Also relevant to the adaptive runner's state-policy graph (cells 110–113), which currently has no early-exit condition on debate convergence.

Multi-Agent Reasoning with Adaptive Worker Allocation for Stance Detection WATCH

Meysam Sabbaghan, Arman Zareian Jahromi, Doina Caragea · arXiv 2606.11609 · 10 Jun 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11609

Applied to stance detection (identifying an author's implicit or rhetorically framed position toward a target), the paper shifts multi-agent aggregation from label-level voting to reasoning-level synthesis: workers generate structured rationales, and a coordinator synthesises those rationales rather than simply tallying predicted labels. Dynamic worker allocation concentrates compute on ambiguous or high-complexity cases.

Project relevance: Reasoning-level synthesis over rationales — rather than score averaging — is a direct design pattern for the multi-critic poetics panel (config/evaluation-rubric-poetics.yaml). The project's critic disagreement finding (weighted κ ≈ 0.04) might be partly explained by label-level aggregation; this paper's approach of synthesising the underlying reasoning could be tested as an alternative aggregation step for poetics:audit.

Ontology & Knowledge Model Evolution

EvoArena: Tracking Memory Evolution for Robust LLM Agents in Dynamic Environments WATCH

Jundong Xu, Qingchuan Li, Jiaying Wu, Yihuai Lan, Shuyue Stella Li, Huichi Zhou, Bowen Jiang, Lei Wang, Jun Wang, Anh Tuan Luu, Caiming Xiong, Hae Won Park, Bryan Hooi, Zhiyuan Hu · arXiv 2606.13681 · 11 Jun 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13681v1 | GitHub: EvoArena

EvoArena benchmarks LLM agents against progressive environment updates across terminal, software, and social domains. The companion contribution, EvoMem, is a patch-based memory paradigm that records memory evolution as structured update histories rather than snapshots, enabling agents to reason about environmental change through the delta between memory states.

Project relevance: EvoMem's patch-based memory is a concrete alternative to the flat per-turn adaptationIndex: rather than computing a scalar delta between consecutive ego outputs, the agent maintains a structured history of changes to its learner model. This could sharpen the otherEgoProfiling mechanism (cells 54–59) and the bilateral transformation indices, and provides a benchmark suite to test whether ego-superego deliberation genuinely produces durable profile updates versus transient adjustments that revert by the next turn.

Near-Misses (June 9 — borderline window)

  • Decoupling Thought from Speech: Knowledge-Grounded Counterfactual Reasoning for Resilient Multi-Agent Argumentation (arXiv 2606.10475, Jakub Masłowski & Jarosław A. Chudziak, Warsaw University of Technology, submitted 9 Jun 2026) — Introduces KG-CFR, a dual-stage architecture that enforces a strict separation between a private, retrieval-augmented planning buffer and a public execution layer to prevent identity drift, argument repetition, and role collapse under sustained perturbations. Project relevance: The private buffer / public speech split is an explicit engineering realisation of the ego-superego boundary — and a solution to the problem the project has flagged as "insight-action gap" (Finding 11, analyze-insight-action-gap.js). The counterfactual reasoning component also maps onto the adaptive runner's counterfactual replay in cells 110–113. arxiv.org/abs/2606.10475
  • "I understand your perspective": LLM Persuasion and Sycophancy through the Lens of Communicative Action Theory (arXiv 2606.08076, Esra Dönmez & Agnieszka Falenska, ACL 2025 Findings — arxiv preprint June 2026) — Maps LLM-generated counter-arguments from the persuasive subreddit ChangeMyView onto Habermas' illocutionary intent dimensions (conveying knowledge / building trust / signalling similarity) and finds that LLMs convey illocutionary intent more strongly than humans, with sycophantic alignment closely tracking opinion change. Project relevance: The illocutionary intent taxonomy is the missing rhetorical dimension in the dialogue quality rubric (v2.2 currently lacks a rhetoric axis); Habermasian communicative action is a philosophically coherent complement to the existing Hegelian recognition frame. Worth integrating into a future rubric v2.3. arxiv.org/abs/2606.08076 | ACL Anthology
