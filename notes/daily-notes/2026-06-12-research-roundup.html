Agent Architecture & Deliberation

The Self-Correction Illusion: LLMs Correct Others but Not Themselves UNBLOCK

Kuan-Yen Chen, Fang-Yi Su, Jung-Hsien Chiang · arXiv 2606.05976 · June 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05976

Keeping an erroneous claim byte-identical (SHA-256 verified), the authors vary only its chat-template role label — the agent's own <thought>, a user message, a tool response, or a system <memory> block — and find that relabeling from <thought> to any external role lifts explicit correction rates by 23–93 percentage points (10 of 13 cells statistically significant, across seven model families and three domains). Self-correction failure is a role-label artifact, not a capability deficit.

Project relevance: This provides direct empirical grounding for the ego-superego loop's core design choice: the superego sees ego output as external content, which is exactly the framing shown here to unlock correction. Conversely, it warns that any merge of ego and superego into a single context (e.g. a naive "self-reflect" prompt) will suppress correction to near-zero. The 13-cell coverage also offers a replication scaffold for cells 3–4, 7–8, 22–33 — measure correction rate vs. role-label to validate the architecture rather than just assuming it works. Additionally, the CLAUDE.md symmetry requirement (tutor/learner trace agent labels must not default to a generic user) gains new empirical motivation: wrong role labels would suppress correction on the tutor side.

The Confident Liar: Diagnosing Multi-Agent Debate with Log-Probabilities and LLM-as-Judge UNBLOCK

Ali Keramati, Justin Cheok, Jacob Horne, Mark Warschauer (University of California, Irvine) · arXiv 2606.10296 · June 9, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10296

In a two-agent Constructor/Auditor debate framework, the paper maps the relationship between token-level log-probability distributions, LLM-as-judge rubric scores, and ground-truth task accuracy across rubric scoring, mathematical reasoning, and factual QA. It exposes a "confident liar" failure mode: high-probability generation that receives high judge scores yet corresponds to wrong answers — internal confidence and external evaluation can systematically co-misalign with correctness.

Project relevance: The Constructor/Auditor pairing is structurally identical to the ego/superego pairing. The confident-liar finding directly interrogates the project's evaluate pipeline: if the judge scores high-fluency, high-confidence ego outputs independently of whether the pedagogical content is correct, then tutor_first_turn_score may be measuring confident generation, not genuine recognition. The log-probability instrument is cheap to add to any eval run (no extra API calls) and could serve as a calibration signal alongside the existing rubric scores. Mark Warschauer is a prominent educational technology researcher (UCI); the rubric-scoring domain in this paper maps closely to the v2.2 rubric evaluation workflow.

Early-Token Confidence Predicts Reasoning Quality in Multi-Agent LLM Debate WATCH

Ali Keramati, Justin Cheok, Jacob Horne, Mark Warschauer (University of California, Irvine) · arXiv 2606.10307 · June 9, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10307

Using the same debate-based essay-scoring framework as 2606.10296 (above), this companion paper shows that early-token log-probability (first few tokens of the generated response) is the strongest single predictor of downstream reasoning quality, outperforming full-sequence statistics. A systematic asymmetry emerges: confidence-quality alignment is substantially stronger for the supportive (Constructor) agent role than for the adversarial (Auditor) role.

Project relevance: The supportive/adversarial asymmetry mirrors the ego (constructive) vs. superego (critical) split. If early-token confidence is a quality proxy for the ego but not the superego, it suggests the superego's critique quality is harder to predict from its own generation signal alone — which would explain why tutor_deliberation_* metrics have historically shown weaker correlations with outcome scores than the tutor's own output metrics. The education-specific framing (Warschauer's lab) grounds this in the essay-scoring task, making it a plausible replication target for the project's dialogue-quality rubric.

Evaluation & Trajectory Analysis

TRACE: Trajectory Reasoning through Adaptive Cross-Step Evidence Aggregation for LLM Agents WATCH

Vijitha Mittapalli, Shreyaa Jayant Dani et al. (University of Massachusetts Amherst / Adobe Research / Dolby Labs / University of Oregon / Cisco) · arXiv 2606.07054 · June 5, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.07054

TRACE monitors long-horizon agent trajectories for hidden malicious objectives through a TIJ (Triage-Inspect-Judge) loop: it triages each step for signal, performs targeted inspection while accumulating evidence across temporally distant actions, and synthesises a trajectory-level verdict. Achieves aggregate F1 = 0.713 and recall = 0.844, with the largest gains on tasks requiring long-range evidence linking across many steps.

Project relevance: The TIJ loop is an architectural parallel to the adaptive evaluator in the project: Triage ≈ analyze-learning-stagnation.js (identifying low-signal turns), Inspect ≈ analyze-trajectory-curves.js (targeted per-dimension drill-down), Judge ≈ tutor_holistic_* scoring (whole-transcript verdict). The cross-step evidence aggregation design — carrying forward evidence rather than windowing — could improve the bilateral_transformation_index computation, which currently aggregates turn deltas independently without long-range linking.

Pedagogical Reasoning & Guided Discovery

Counterexample Guided Learning in the Large using Reasoning Agents WATCH

(Authors from submission 2606.11521) · arXiv 2606.11521 · June 9, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11521

An LLM agent proposes candidate solutions (regular expressions) while a verifier returns minimal counterexamples that falsify each candidate; the agent refines iteratively. Verifier feedback improves sample efficiency dramatically — success rates lift from 3.2% to 38.1% and from 38.9% to 74.1% on two regex domains — outperforming self-reflection alone.

Project relevance: The verifier-as-counterexample-provider is a formal instantiation of Socratic guided discovery: the tutor withholds the answer and instead supplies the minimal disconfirming evidence that forces the learner to revise. This is precisely the mechanism the poetics rubric measures in the peripeteia dimension (a sudden reversal triggered by new information). The quantitative efficiency gains — comparing self-reflection baseline vs. external verifier feedback — offer a clean experimental template for the recognition vs. placebo cells (e.g. cells 5–6 vs. 15–18).
