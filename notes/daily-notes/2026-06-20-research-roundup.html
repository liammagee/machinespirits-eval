Prompt Engineering

Learning to Prompt: Improving Student Engagement with Adaptive LLM-based High-School Tutoring UNBLOCK

Po-Chin Chang, Nicholas Hogan, Aske Plaat, Michiel T. van der Meer · Conference on Games 2026 (vision paper) · arXiv 2606.20138 · ~19–20 Jun 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20138

An adaptive prompt-engineering framework for LLM-based high-school tutors that iterates over prompt strategies in response to student engagement signals detected mid-session. Rather than fixing a prompt at session start, the system continuously adjusts the tutor prompt based on learner behavioral signals, showing measurable pedagogical gains across varied learner profiles.

Project relevance: Directly addresses the prompt-engineering backbone of LLM tutoring. The adaptive strategy parallels the project's prompts/ versioning and resolveEvalProfile dispatch logic; the engagement-signal framing maps onto the learner rubric's engagement dimension (v2.2). The evaluation methodology — iterating prompts against live learner signals — is a candidate paradigm for the npm run prompt-lab -- autotune workflow, replacing manual ablation (cells 9–20) with a closed-loop engagement-driven search.

Agent Architecture

Contagion Networks: Evaluator Bias Propagation in Multi-Agent LLM Systems UNBLOCK

Zewen Liu · arXiv 2606.20493 · ~19–20 Jun 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20493

Formalizes how evaluator bias propagates through multi-agent LLM systems via "contagion": one agent's biased judgment contaminates downstream agents that consume it as context. Develops a network model of bias spread across agent chains and proposes mitigations including independent sampling and adversarial critic insertion.

Project relevance: A direct warning for the project's LLM-as-judge pipeline. The inter-rater reliability methodology (analyze-judge-reliability.js, paired rejudge runs) assumes independent judges; contagion contamination is a live threat if superego-critique context leaks into judge prompts. Also structural: in the Ego→Superego→Ego revision loop, a biased superego critique compounds across revision rounds and across cells, precisely the systematic drift §5.4 tries to detect. The adversarial-critic-insertion mitigation maps directly onto the dialectical_suspicious superego cells 22–33.

Ontology & Formal Reasoning

DeFAb: A Verifiable Benchmark for Defeasible Abduction in Foundation Models WATCH

Patrick Cooper, Alvaro Velasquez · NASA Formal Methods Symposium (NFM 2026) · arXiv 2606.18557 · ~18–19 Jun 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18557

A 2,880-instance benchmark testing whether foundation models can perform defeasible abduction — drawing revisable conclusions from incomplete evidence and retracting them when contradicting evidence arrives. Performance degrades systematically with rising abductive complexity even in frontier models, and the degradation is measurable and reproducible across configurations.

Project relevance: Defeasible reasoning is structurally isomorphic to the Hegelian recognition process: the tutor and learner must revise beliefs about the Other in light of new evidence, retracting prior conclusions when challenged. DeFAb could calibrate the poetics critic — if the judge model fails defeasible abduction at depth 3 it may mis-classify recognition events requiring revision of an earlier premise. The NFM framing (formal, verifiable ground truth) also parallels the project's passes_required / passes_forbidden validation columns.

Dramaturgy & Role-Play

When Roleplaying, Do Models Believe What They Say? UNBLOCK

Benjamin Sturgeon, David Africa, Sid Black · arXiv 2606.11502 · ~9 Jun 2026 (coverage gap — not in prior roundups) · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11502

Linear truth probes applied to LLMs roleplaying historical personas (Aristotle, Newton, etc.) whose stated beliefs diverge from modern consensus. Key finding: persona induction suppresses the internal false-belief probe less than equally-false non-persona claims — the model continues to classify its own in-character assertion as false while voicing it. The suppression pattern persists across prompting, in-context learning, and supervised fine-tuning.

Project relevance: Directly challenges the epistemic foundations of the drama-machine learner. If an LLM-learner playing a "confused student" is internally probing the correct answer as true throughout, the apparent anagnorisis is a surface output shift rather than a genuine belief update — precisely the gap between dramatic form and epistemic substance that §6.13 opens. Suggests the poetics rubric needs a "depth of belief change" sub-dimension, or that the anagnorisis detector should consult an auxiliary truth probe alongside surface language. High priority for the adaptation-recognition loop redesign.

Rhetoric & Persuasion

Using Cognitive Models to Improve Language Model Simulation of Human Persuasion Games UNBLOCK

Zirui Cheng, Zeyu Shen, Thomas L. Griffiths, Peter Henderson · Princeton University · arXiv 2606.17657 · ~16 Jun 2026 (coverage gap) · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17657

"Equation-to-Behavior Prompting" encodes cognitive models — Bayesian updating, affine probability distortion, motivated updating — directly into LLM prompts and tests whether models reproduce the corresponding human persuasion behaviors in legal decision-making scenarios. Large models approximate equation-based specifications more faithfully than smaller models, covering a broader behavioral range than baseline LLM simulation without any fine-tuning.

Project relevance: A precise methodological twin to the project's recognition-theory prompting: both encode a theoretical specification (cognitive equations / Hegelian recognition) as an explicit prompt constraint. Princeton's results suggest that making the Hegelian recognition dynamics formally explicit in the ego prompt — equations or structural rules, not prose description alone — may sharpen inter-cell contrasts on the §6.3 suggestion-scenario suite. A concrete experiment to pilot under npm run prompt-lab -- autotune, especially for cells 5–8 and 84–90 (recognition-mode variants).
