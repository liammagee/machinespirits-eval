Agent Architecture & Deliberation

Not All Flips Are Conformity: Decomposing Stance Convergence in Multi-Agent LLM Debate UNBLOCK

Xiqi Hao, Zengqing Wu, Yu-Xuan Qiu, Chuan Xiao, Ruiqi Xu, Shuyuan Zheng, Jianbin Qin · arXiv 2606.00820 · June 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.00820

The paper isolates three distinct mechanisms behind agent answer-flips in multi-agent debate: spontaneous instability (baseline volatility under self-reflection alone, ~37% of observations), stance-induced conformity (~29%, predominantly harmful), and reasoning-induced persuasion (the only "good" flip). The decomposition uses controlled counterfactual conditions.

Project relevance: This directly operationalises the question the ego-superego loop currently cannot answer: when the ego revises after superego critique, is that genuine pedagogical adaptation or social compliance with an authoritative inner voice? The 3-source framework (instability / conformity / persuasion) could be adapted as a post-hoc classifier on ego revision turns, sharpening the adaptationIndex and potentially resolving the "near-miss is learner draw or structural cap" problem in /ms-replay-one-side.

Dynamic Trust-Aware Sparse Communication Topology for LLM-Based Multi-Agent Consensus WATCH

Wanshuang Gou, Zihan Liu · arXiv 2606.01828 · June 1, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.01828

DySCo replaces fully-connected broadcasting in multi-agent deliberation with a sparse communication graph selected each round by weighting edges on agent reliability × answer divergence × task relevance. Token costs and latency scale linearly with agents rather than quadratically, while cross-validation quality is preserved.

Project relevance: Multi-turn cells with superego (cells 3–4, 7–8, 22–33, 82–83, 86–89) pay a full ego↔superego exchange each turn. DySCo's trust-weighted edge selection is a direct candidate for cells where the superego has already converged — skipping the exchange when divergence is low. Also relevant to the adaptive runner's state-policy graph (cells 110–113).

Dramaturgy / Roleplay

ArcANE: Do Role-Playing Language Agents Stay in Character at the Right Time? UNBLOCK

Woojung Song, Nalim Kim, Sangjun Song, Chaewon Heo, Jongwon Lim, Yohan Jo · arXiv 2606.05553 · June 4, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05553 | HuggingFace page

ArcANE (Arc-Aware Narrative Evaluation) automatically segments 17 novels into character arc phases along a psychological axis, then probes the same scenario across phases — including situations the source text never depicts. Conditioning on the Character Arc outperforms all six other context strategies (chapter text, persona card, etc.) across six models; the gap is largest for out-of-text scenarios where retrieval fails.

Project relevance: The "character arc phase" construct maps almost exactly onto the poetics pipeline's peripeteia/anagnorisis trajectory. ArcANE's phase-segmentation methodology — narrative phases × psychological axis × phase-conditioned probing — could be adapted as a scoring scaffold for config/evaluation-rubric-poetics.yaml, replacing per-dimension scores with phase-transition scores that track the tutor's (or learner's) psychological arc across the dialogue. This is potentially the most direct methodological import for the phase-2 poetics arc.

Rhetoric & Argumentation

A Model of Multi-turn Human Persuadability Using Probabilistic Belief Tracing UNBLOCK

Jared Moore, Noah Goodman, Nick Haber, Max Kleiman-Weiner (Stanford / UWashington) · arXiv 2606.05330 · June 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05330 | GitHub: persuasiontrace

PersuasionTrace is a web-based experimental platform that records belief reports at every turn of a human–LLM conversation, annotates each persuader turn with logos / pathos / ethos dimensions, and clusters human participants into distinct belief-update trajectories. LLMs prove persuasive across generic and personalised topics, text and audio modalities, and multi-turn interaction; human targets split into two clusters of belief dynamics.

Project relevance: The logos/pathos/ethos trichotomy could extend the dialogue quality rubric with a rhetorical mode dimension (currently absent from v2.2). More urgently, the probabilistic belief-tracing architecture is a near-exact analogue of bilateral transformation measurement — the learnerGrowthIndex is currently inferred from surface text; PersuasionTrace's turn-level belief reports offer a direct validation blueprint for the human-learner pilot (§A1 of TODO.md).

Argument Collapse: LLMs Flatten Long-Form Public Debate WATCH

Yekyung Kim, Yapei Chang, Chau Minh Pham, Mohit Iyyer (University of Maryland) · arXiv 2606.01736 · June 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.01736

Studying NYT opinion debates, the paper shows that different LLMs independently converge to nearly identical argument sets: human essays share unique main arguments 65.3% of the time; LLM essays only 3.4%. Prompting for diversity recovers some variation but remains outside human argument space.

Project relevance: If tutors collapse their explanatory repertoire the same way LLMs collapse arguments, this would suppress the bilateral transformation the project measures. The argument-uniqueness metric could be adapted as a diagnostic for tutor response diversity across runs — a complement to the existing adaptationIndex — and connects to Finding 11 / analyze-insight-action-gap.js.

Ontology / Knowledge Representation

Agentic Generation and Evolution of Knowledge Models WATCH

Man Zhang, Tao Yue (Beihang University); Nazareno M. Aguirre, Diego Garbervetsky, Sebastian Uchitel (CONICET / Universidad de Buenos Aires / Imperial College London) · arXiv 2606.03662 · June 2, 2026 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03662

TrustModel proposes a three-subsystem architecture for living knowledge models: Modeling (construct and update the knowledge model), Conformance (assess alignment between model and observed system behaviour), and Evolution (generate guidance to keep models synchronised with change). The paper is positioned as a vision/position piece for dynamic, safety-critical systems.

Project relevance: The Modeling/Conformance/Evolution triad maps structurally onto the ego-superego loop: Ego ≈ Modeling (generating a response model of the learner), Superego ≈ Conformance (checking that model against pedagogical criteria), revision ≈ Evolution (updating the approach). The paper may offer formal vocabulary for describing the learner-model updates that underpin the otherEgoProfiling mechanism (cells 54–59).
