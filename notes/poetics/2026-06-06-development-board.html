01·

Two axes — status and theme

How to read this board.

Every item carries a status and a theme. The full board in §03 is filterable on both. Colour follows status throughout: closed open ruled out.

S.

Status — three buckets

Open — work is live, gated on a dependency, or deferred to a future paper. Closed — settled with a result, including null results and confounds (a null is a finding). Ruled out — a deliberate won't-do: superseded, by-design, or dropped after the question it would have answered was answered another way.

T.

Theme — six tracks

A · experiments the empirical arc · B · infrastructure code quality · C · maintenance repo hygiene · D · research mechanism & theory · E · bugs known defects · F · paper revisions. Each card keeps its original TODO.md id (A1, B8, D5 …) so you can find it in the source.

!

Priority — only meaningful on open items

CRITICAL blocks publication-quality claims · HIGH would significantly strengthen findings · MEDIUM valuable, not blocking · LOW opportunistic. Closed items keep their old priority for the record but it no longer gates anything.

Why keep the closed ones Thirty-six of forty-eight entries are closed. The settled nulls are the most reusable part — they are the map of moves that don't pay.

Other board Paper-2.0 day-to-day work has its own list at notes/paper-2-0/BOARD.md; this file is the older long-horizon arc. The two don't overlap.

02·

The eight open items

What's live now.

Eight of forty-eight items are open. Two are in motion, two are gated on an external dependency or approval, and four are research deferred to a future paper. Everything else on the board is settled.

In motion

A18

Recursive tutor-learning benchmark HIGH active

The teacher is the learner: after a failed attempt the tutor records a bounded strategy lesson and must use it to improve on a held-out sibling case — a contrastive before/after, not a single polished transcript. Steps A18.0–A18.36 are done; the arc has converged on a bounded claim across two artificial families (selector, bead): saved attempt-1 policy memory transfers to held-out siblings in ways blind contrastive critics distinguish from a no-policy continuation. Open: A18.37 — the 12-family fanout that establishes whether that convergence is a rate or a singleton (it currently rests on n=1, relational_betweenness). The full chronology is the story so far.

A17

One-side replay replication across scenes cost-gated

Freeze the tutor and scene, regenerate only the learner K times — isolating learner-variance from scene-variance. The one run so far found a near-miss was a learner draw, not a structural cap (graded 3.38 vs the original 2.5). Open: replicate across evidence-poor scenes to test the two-layer model — whether a scene carries a decisive artifact is a scene property; whether a given learner reaches it is partly a draw. Paid; gated on an explicit go-ahead with a cost estimate.

Gated on a dependency or approval

A1

Human-learner validation CRITICAL gated

Every evaluation on this board uses a simulated learner. The single highest-value open question is whether recognition-enhanced tutoring produces genuine learning gains in real humans. The engineering layer is complete — persistence, a 13-endpoint pilot flow, a counterbalanced item bank with server-side scoring, an idempotent ingestion path to the eval scorer, 15 passing tests. Gated on the content/legal track: IRB approval, real consent text, NAEP-derived items + free-text prompts, OSF pre-registration. A three-arm pilot (base / recognition / behaviorist-matched) before any RCT.

A15

Cross-dialogue retrieval-augmented adaptation design · gated on A14

A persistent cache of (learner-state → policy-action → outcome) tuples across dialogues, retrieved and injected at policy-selection time — no weight updates. It attacks the time-scale A14 explicitly does not: a tutor at turn 5 with a new learner can retrieve 4-turn priors from similar past learners. Gated on A14 so the cache keys reuse A14's typed, validated state rather than ad-hoc text features. A complement, not a substitute; lands as a paper § if run.

Research — deferred to a future paper

Four research items are designed and partly done, but parked: they are either Paper 3.0 infrastructure or separate-paper scope, and the cross-version-contamination rule forbids retro-scoring under a newer rubric anyway.

  • D5 · Rubric v3.0 PCA consolidation MEDIUM — §8.6's PCA shows the eight v2.2 dimensions collapse to essentially one factor (PC1 = 80.7%, KMO = 0.938). Open: a 2-factor v3.0 rubric, or a discriminant-validity demonstration. Decisions pending; Paper 3.0 infrastructure.

Terms

Glossary.

Open work is live
A board item that is in motion, gated on a dependency or approval, or deferred to a future paper. The eight open items are where the actual work is — everything else is a record.
Closed settled with a result
Resolved — including null results and confounds. A null is a finding: it tells you a move doesn't pay. Closed items are kept as a map, not deleted.
Ruled out a deliberate won't-do
An item decided against: superseded, by-design, or dropped after the question it would have answered got answered another way. Distinct from open (not yet done) and closed (done).
Policy memory the distilled repair
A one-paragraph distillation of the repair a tutor improvised on a training scene, injected into a held-out replay (S1) to test whether it transfers. The one channel in the A18 arc that reliably carries new signal.
Headroom room to learn
The gap that exists only when a no-policy replay (S0) fails a held-out scene and the policy replay (S1) succeeds via the registered policy. Structural, not probabilistic: present only if a shortcut exists and lands on a decoy rather than the target.
Intersubjective orientation the active ingredient
Treating the learner as a partner whose understanding is co-constructed — shared across recognition theory and its constructivist descendants. A10/A13/D6 found this family membership, not the specific Hegelian vocabulary, is what drives recognition's effect (between-family d = 1.38).
Pre-registration thresholds locked before the run
Fixing hypotheses, sample sizes, and decision grids in writing before generating any data, so the analysis can't be steered by the result. A null on a pre-registered contrast (e.g. A16) is itself a reportable finding.
CUT a scorer threshold
The score cutoff in recognitionOrigin.js above which a tutor move counts as a device-driven mechanism. B8 found the value matters less than which critics sit on the panel — Qwen/DeepSeek score the same evidence systematically lower than Sonnet.
