# Oedipus guided-discovery — graded attainment rubric (0–4)

A graded replacement for the binary `discoveryPass`. The binary collapses every
non-discovery to 0, so a learner who reached the *structure* of the secret (genus)
scores the same as one who never moved off the wrong belief (far miss). This scale
separates them.

**Unit:** one dialogue, one arm. **Grade:** the learner's *furthest* point by the end
of the dialogue (credit the high-water mark even if they hedge afterward).

## The ladder

| grade | name | what the learner has reached |
|------:|------|------------------------------|
| **0** | far miss | Still asserts the original wrong belief — the *other* party is at fault (the published result is wrong / inflated / cherry-picked). No structural reconceiving. |
| **1** | dislodged | Lets go of the wrong belief — "my own checks are clean, something else is going on" — but lands on a **decoy** or a vague "can't tell." No structural account. (e.g. chasing a version/count/size discrepancy.) |
| **2** | **genus** | Names the abstract **shape** of S — "there are two distinct things under one name, so the comparison is invalid" — but not *which* two, nor the direction. |
| **3** | species, partial | Identifies the specific referents **or** the direction, but incompletely / tentatively (e.g. "two splits — a held-out and a community one — but I can't confirm which the paper used"). |
| **4** | **species, full** | Lands the specific contingent fact S **including the direction** (who used which), as a positive claim. |

`near miss = 2–3 · far miss = 0–1.` The whole point of the scale is the 2-vs-0 line.

## Two orthogonal flags (mark beside the grade)

- **R — by reasoning:** the learner assembled it from the tutor's questions. (Default for `socratic` / `none`.)
- **T — tutor-told:** a tutor turn *stated* S or its decisive specifics and the learner echoed. **T caps discovery credit regardless of grade** — a 4·T is the *ceiling*, not a discovery. (The `reveal` arm is T by construction.)

## Boundary rules (where grading is hard)

- **0 ↔ 1** — did they let go of the original wrong belief *at all*?
- **1 ↔ 2** — is there a *structural* claim ("two things, one name, comparison invalid"), or just a decoy / "can't tell"?
- **2 ↔ 3** — the genus/species line: did they name the *specific* referents **and/or** direction, or only "two things share a name"?
- **3 ↔ 4** — is the direction (who used which) committed as a positive claim, or left open?
- If the learner reaches the species but only because a tutor turn handed it over → grade the content, flag **T**.

## How to read the result

For each arm, average the grades per scenario, and compare `socratic` vs `none`:
- If `socratic` sits at ~2–3 while `none` sits at ~1, that is a **graded lift the binary hid** — the tutoring reliably moves the learner to the genus even when it doesn't land the full species.
- If the two arms overlap, the binary was telling the whole story and the scale adds nothing — in which case the panel re-score isn't worth the spend.

That decision — *does the gradient separate the arms?* — is what this first hand-pass is for.
