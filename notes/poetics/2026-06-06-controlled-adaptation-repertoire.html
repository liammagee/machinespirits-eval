Three instruments, one repertoire.

What our ontology memory model, our small-rhetoric scorers, and our large-dramatic-form rubric actually measured — and how to turn the handful of real wins into a controlled set of adaptive mechanisms.

We built three different instruments to ask one question — does the tutor adapt, and does the adaptation carry new signal rather than restate the obvious? Each reads the dialogue at a different grain: a formal memory model (symbolic), small rhetorical phrasing (lexical, micro), and large dramatic turns (whole-transcript, macro).

This note does two things. It states plainly where each instrument now stands — what paid off, what is a null, what was a confound. Then it proposes how to exploit the few wins into a wider repertoire of controlled adaptive mechanisms. It is a lab notebook: the durable claims live in paper-full-2.0.md and the summary; here I narrate, cite every number to its source, and propose. The story so far tells the same arc as a chronology — this one cuts it by instrument.

01·

Three grains of the same question

One question, read at three grains.

The question is always the same: when a learner is genuinely stuck, does the tutor's move change — and can we tell the change came from something it picked up, not from re-describing what the scene already made obvious? We attacked it with three instruments, each at a different scale.

I.

Ontology memory model symbolic

A formal, consistency-checkable memory of who-knows-what — a shared description-logic vocabulary, per-role belief stores, and a theory-of-mind layer the tutor reads. Headline: plain ontology grounding helps; the mind-reading layer bolted on top of it is a null.

II.

Small rhetorical phrasing lexical · micro

Does the wording carry the adaptation? Two scorers: recognition-lexicon density, and a derived-origin classifier that buckets recognition as device-induced vs organic. Headline: vocabulary is a marker, not a mediator; and the origin scorer was, for a while, measuring itself.

III.

Large dramatic turns whole-transcript · macro

Does the whole transcript have the shape of a recognition — a reversal that lands, an insight that recoheres the scene? An Aristotelian six-dimension rubric, scored end-to-end. Headline: real on literary canon, does not transfer to tutoring, and every apparent effect traces to the author.

Throughline The three converge on a single finding — §05. Read separately first; the convergence is the point.

Genre An analysis note is allowed to be provisional. Durable claims graduate to the paper and the summary.

02·

Instrument I · a formal, checkable memory of who-knows-what

The ontology helps. The mind-reading layer on top of it does not.

The build is real and inspectable. We consolidated the repo's two formal ontologies (reasoning-core.ttl + poetics-core.ttl) into one co-loaded shared vocabulary, added a deliberate disjointness set (consistency-axioms.ttl — e.g. knowledge-component mastered ⊥ missing), and built per-role ABoxes: turn-stamped, two-tier (grounded vs hypothesized) belief stores, with a per-turn description-logic consistency check and latest-wins belief revision. A role's acquired graph about another role is its theory-of-mind. Anagnorisis is measured as set-overlap; seventeen unit tests pin the machinery.

✓

Plain ontology grounding beats baseline works

In a real multi-turn A/B on three stress scenarios, ontology policy guidance — "only assert the conclusion if the proof is the learner's" — lifts the anagnorisis outcome from 0.83 (baseline) to 1.00. Grounding the move in a formal model of what the learner actually owns changes the move for the better.

✕

The theory-of-mind feedback layer is a null null · slightly negative

Add a per-turn, consistency-checked ToM brief on top of the ontology guidance and the outcome goes the wrong way: anagnorisis 0.83 vs 1.00 (lower — the extra directiveness cost the learner the goal in the identity-threat scene), judge score +0.06 (noise).

And it fires correctly: surface compliance — a learner claiming the conclusion while signalling weak ownership — is flagged as a grounded inconsistency at turns 2–3, and the brief is followed. It simply re-encodes a move the ontology guidance already prompts. A formal, checkable interior reaches the same null as §6.10's free-text one.

Two findings worth keeping out of the wreckage. First: ToM consistency is not pedagogy. A tutor that accurately reads a deferring learner and just answers is perfectly consistent — and bad. Scaffolding is productive inconsistency: treat-as-partner over treat-as-accurately-modelled. Second: the description-logic machinery is correct and inspectable — it beats §6.10's free-text proxy on checkability — it just doesn't pay on these traps.

Cite Real Codex multi-turn A/B, stress suite n=3, single backend, heuristic detector. scripts/run-ontology-tom-ab.js; write-up notes/ontology/2026-06-02-multi-turn-tom-ab.md; paper §6.10 symbolic corroboration (out of claim set).

Now fused The vocabulary is folded into the poetics ontology (Aristotle / Wittgenstein / Brandom scorekeeping). A population bridge from runs into the ABox is built and unit-tested on fixtures — not yet run on real loop data.

03·

Instrument II · does the wording carry the adaptation?

Vocabulary is a marker, not a mediator — and one scorer was measuring itself.

Two families of micro-instrument, two different lessons.

Family A · recognition-lexicon density (A10 / A10b)

a.

Swap the Hegelian vocabulary and the effect survives settled · the marker isn't the mechanism

Replace recognition's specific Hegelian wording with matched-specificity prompts grounded in its constructivist descendants (Vygotsky, Piaget, Kapur, Chi, VanLehn, Graesser) and the effect reproduces — pooled three-judge d = 0.14–0.19, below the |d| < 0.2 density-sufficient threshold. The active ingredient is intersubjective-pedagogy orientation, not the lexicon.

But orientation has a sign. Matched-specificity prompts grounded in the orthogonal behaviorist tradition (Skinner, Gagné, Keller, Thorndike, Rosenshine) score pooled d = 0.89 below the generic baseline — rigour in the wrong family is actively counterproductive. Between-family d = 1.38 dominates within-family contrasts by an order of magnitude. The recognition lexicon does surface as a marker (it leads on the recognition_quality sub-dimension, 2.75 vs 2.26) — but it does not mediate the overall score. Replicated on a fourth model (Nemotron, d = 0.17).

Family B · derived-origin scorer (device-induced vs organic)

b.

The "organic-recognition saturation" was a scorer bug bug → fragile positive

The adaptation loop kept stalling on "organic recognition" — the peripeteia arm earned 3–4/4 recognition votes but its origin stayed unattributed. It looked like the drama failing. It was the scorer. Origin was derived, never voted (recognitionOrigin.js: the only binding axis is a tutor-mechanism score ≥ 75), and that axis was clamped by brittle hardcoded lexicons in two paths. "Tiles float. The diagram does not." — a textbook reversal — matched no lexicon, so it clamped to organic. Only the author-family critic (Sonnet, the generator's own family) ever cleared the gate.

The EDRA-M3 surgery fixed the measurement. Post-fix on the author-family panel: a small, fragile positive — lift 0.333 [0.12, 0.65] over nine valid pairs, with the peripeteia arm still under-recognized (3/9). Swapping the critic to GPT to de-confound the author family shows the peripeteia effect survives with no inflation — lift 1.0 on valid pairs — but 3 of 6 pairs are invalidated by control-leak. The leak's root cause is the generator: the control arms have the learner visibly self-correcting (tutor-did-device = false on every leaked control). A real but fragile small positive, and a reusable de-confounding harness.

Cite A10/A10b — paper §7.9, §8.6.1. Origin scorer — scripts/lib/recognitionOrigin.js; surgery spec notes/poetics/2026-05-28-edra-m3-surgery-spec.md; GPT de-confound exports/paired-increment-reverse-GPT-pooled-*.json.

Reporting rule Always report the control-leak invalidation rate beside the lift. A lift of 1.0 on a denominator halved by leaks is misleading on its own.

04·

Instrument III · does the whole transcript have the shape of a recognition?

Real on canon. Does not transfer to tutoring.

The poetics rubric (evaluation-rubric-poetics.yaml v1.0) scores a transcript end-to-end on six Aristotelian dimensions — peripeteia, anagnorisis, surprise-with-inevitability, unity of action, hamartia integration, catharsis. On canonical literary drama it passed, triple-validated (Phase 1). Then we asked whether it reads tutoring dialogue the way a human does.

✕

Phase-2 transfer to tutoring failed null · pre-registered

Instrument-vs-human weighted κ = 0.044 (nominal 0.222; raw agreement 17/36 = 47%) against a pre-registered ≥ 0.60 bar. The instrument that validated on canon does not reproduce human form-judgments on tutoring transcripts.

The mechanism is the result: the critic over-attributes recognition ~3× (27/36 calls vs the human's 10/36). Of its 27, only 9 matched the human; 12 were ordinary exchange, 6 were insight-declaration-without-recohering. It made every call after clearing its own evidence gate (citing a verbatim earlier turn) — so it reads ordinary tutoring progression as structural recohering. It is fooled by the costume of recognition.

⚠

The "critic mirror" is an author confound confound

The full 2×2 (critic × author) relocates the effect entirely. Claude-authored dramas read as recognition to both critics — 100% / 100% (n=6). Codex-authored dramas read flat/trap to both — 22% / 0% (n=18). The author dominates; the critic is a small secondary effect, visible only on codex-authored text.

Costume caveat: all six Claude dramas read as recognition to both critics including the ones designed as flat, trap, and impasse. The instrument cannot separate earned recohering from a convincing costume. "Claude wears the recognition costume so consistently both critics fall for it even on designed traps" is as good a reading as "Claude writes better recohering."

⚠

A timing effect that was really a coherence confound confound

A contrastive timing-pair probe asked whether moving a tutor device next to the learner's reframe makes blind critics attribute the reframe to the tutor. Pooled, it looked like a tidy positive (A−B = 0.28). It was a coherence confound: displacing a pivot whose text refers back to the obstruction breaks anaphora and tanks coherence — attribution falls with coherence, not with timing.

On the coherence-clean subset the timing effect is 0.00, all three critics — but at the attribution floor, so underpowered rather than a proven null. And the critics are not gullible: a decoy arm (neutral move + adjacency) earns less attribution than the real bridge (0.17 < 0.33). They track mechanism content, not bare sequence. The design's mandated coherence-gate caught its own confound.

There is no clean positive for the dramatic instrument on tutoring. The arc's only positive — the recursive teacher-as-learner (A18, §7.9) — abandons the read-form-from-the-transcript premise and instead asks whether a saved policy transfers to a held-out scene. That is a different instrument, and the only one that pays.

Frame This is dramatic-form classification of text-internal recohering — never real-learning or mind-reading detection. Divergence from human labels is a finding, not a κ to chase.

05·

What all three instruments keep saying

Every win is a signal win. Every null is a re-encoding null.

Across the symbolic, the lexical, and the dramatic grain, the same shape shows through. Adaptation pays when the mechanism carries new signal the strong model doesn't already infer. It is null when it re-encodes what the model already reads in-context — a richer ToM brief, a denser lexicon, a more elaborate state schema. The model had already inferred the thing; restating it formally adds nothing.

And the second half: apparent effects keep tracing back to the generator / author, not to a privileged learner interior. The dramatic instrument's whole effect was the author. The origin scorer's leak was the generator. There is no privileged interior in a simulated dyad — when both sides are the same strong model, "reading the learner's mind" is reading your own.

The hard part is not measuring adaptation. It is manufacturing signal a strong model doesn't already have.three instruments · 2026-06-06

Why this dictates the repertoire.
A mechanism is worth building only if it (1) carries new signal, (2) toggles cleanly so we can run an on/off contrast, and (3) is scored on an architecture-independent channel so the win can't be the author confound that sank the dramatic instrument. Those three filters are the whole design discipline of §06.

Same lesson, more grains §6.8–6.10 of the paper reached this on the runner and state-schema work; the three instruments here reach it again on symbolic memory, lexicon, and dramatic form.

06·

Turning the small wins into mechanisms · forward-looking design

Six things worth building.

These are proposals, not results — opportunities to compound the wins above. Each passes the three filters from §05: new signal, clean toggle, architecture-independent scoring. They are ordered by how load-bearing the underlying win already is.

1.

Controlled trap-generation by card geometry strongest win to exploit

A18 settled that headroom is structural, not a thin probabilistic band: a card shows headroom iff a fresh tutor takes a shortcut and that shortcut lands on a decoy rather than the target. That makes headroom manufacturable — author scenes where the obvious shortcut is wired to a decoy, and the target is reachable only through the relation the policy names. The zero-cost cue-map preflight and blind-S0 screen already triage candidates before any paid replay; the per-card structural rule tells the generator what to construct. Turn fixture-authoring from intuition into a recipe.

2.

Policy-memory as a reusable signal primitive the one channel that pays

The distilled one-paragraph repair is the single channel in the whole arc that reliably carries new signal. Generalize it: a library of distilled repairs keyed by relation-type, injected as a controlled S1-vs-S0 contrast and scored on the blind option-adjudication arbiter (target/decoy aliases held out from the critic). The primitive is already proven on a per-card basis (§7.9, 5 of 8 fresh held-out cards); the opportunity is to make it a stocked, retrievable repertoire rather than a per-family hand-build.

3.

Ontology as a move-grounding constraint — not a mind-reading layer works · use it narrowly

Plain ontology guidance beat baseline (0.83 → 1.00) by constraining the move to what the learner actually owns. Use it for exactly that — and do not bolt the redundant ToM-feedback layer on top (§02). A second design rule falls out of the same study: aim for productive inconsistency (scaffold) over consistency (just-answer). The ontology is a guard-rail on the tutor's assertion, not a model of the learner's mind.

4.

Intersubjective-orientation as a tunable register small dial, set deliberately

The active ingredient is the orientation, not the vocabulary (within-family d = 0.14–0.19; between-family d = 1.38). Treat which pedagogical family as a small, deliberate dial — and respect the sign: wrong-family rigour (behaviorist) is actively counterproductive (d = 0.89 below base on strong models), not merely inert. The lever is the family choice, made on purpose, not a denser lexicon hoping the words do the work.

5.

The de-confounded origin scorer as a measurement layer methods win

EDRA-M3 turned a self-measuring scorer into a usable one, and the GPT critic-swap plus control-leak detector is a reusable de-confounding harness. Keep it as the standing measurement layer for any device-induced-vs-organic recognition contrast — cross-family critic panel, control-leak as a hard invalidator, lift always reported beside its leak rate. The instrument's value now is as a control, not a headline.

6.

Adversarial superego via prompt-rewrite provisional

A critic that genuinely tries to undermine the ego's draft beats an advisory one decisively, and the load-bearing channel is the prompt-rewrite, not message-mutation. A controllable, architecture-level dial that reshapes the tutor's move before it is spoken. Still a working finding, not a durable §; promising precisely because it injects an adversarial process the base model doesn't run on itself.

Design discipline Every item is either a new-signal mechanism (1, 2, 3, 6) or a control/measurement layer (5), or a deliberate dial (4). Nothing here re-encodes what the model already infers — which is exactly the disqualifier for everything in §08.

Status Proposals. As of 2026-06-06 none had been run as written; several since have — see the Update (§09). The standing open door was the most principled new-signal move left: model the concealed interior properly (Adaptation Plan 2.0).

Micro · orientation transfers

Constructivist phrasing reproduces recognition

Matched-specificity constructivist prompts (Vygotsky / Piaget / Kapur …) reproduce recognition's effect within d = 0.14–0.19. The orientation moves across cognate vocabularies — only the specific Hegelian words are interchangeable. A win for the orientation, a null for the lexicon.

Ontology · over baseline

Plain ontology grounding lifts anagnorisis

A formal who-knows-what memory, used to constrain the tutor's assertion, lifts the outcome from 0.83 to 1.00. Grounding the move in what the learner owns is a real, if narrow, win — and the part of the ontology stack that pays.

Micro · de-confounded

Peripeteia device survives the critic swap

After the EDRA-M3 fix, swapping the author-family critic for GPT does not collapse the effect — lift 1.0 on valid pairs, no author-family inflation. Bounded: 3 of 6 pairs leak. Real on the clean pairs, and a reusable de-confounding harness.

blue_lower — the helper cue was a second self-solve path

Behaviorist phrasing scores below baseline

Matched-specificity behaviorist prompts (Skinner / Gagné / Keller …) score pooled d = 0.89 below the generic baseline. Density in the wrong pedagogical family is actively counterproductive, not neutral — the orientation has a sign.

A per-turn, consistency-checked theory-of-mind brief on top of ontology guidance is null-to-negative: anagnorisis 0.83 vs 1.00. It fires correctly — it flags surface compliance — but re-encodes a move the ontology already prompts.

Signal axis · null

Modelling the concealed interior (naive)

Giving the tutor the learner's hidden state — free-text and the symbolic ToM variant alike — does not pay under the offline kill gate (§6.10). The naive version of "the signal axis" is a null; the principled version (Plan 2.0) is the standing open door.

08·

The nulls, so we don't rebuild them

What not to build again.

The repertoire's discipline cuts both ways. These are closed nulls — each disqualified for the same reason: it re-encodes signal the model already infers, or it measures the author rather than the learner. Rebuilding any of them expecting a win is the ablation-creep trap.

  • Bilateral theory-of-mind elaboration — no within-family discrimination at the pre-registered effect size §6.8.5
  • A richer learner state-schema — not just no help, a reversal §6.8.6
  • The hypothesis ledger & the learned state→action policy lever — binary gains don't survive the graded channel / the offline kill gate §6.9.7 · §6.9.8
  • Modelling the concealed interior, naively — free-text and symbolic ToM both null under the kill gate §6.10
  • The ontology ToM-feedback layer — redundant over plain ontology guidance §02 · notes/ontology
  • Reading dramatic form off the transcript — doesn't transfer to tutoring, and the effect is the author §04
  • A denser recognition lexicon — the vocabulary is a marker, not a mediator §03

A cascade of nulls is not a sad result — it is a map. It tells you exactly which kind of move to stop making, and which small win to build on instead.three instruments · 2026-06-06

Durable baseline: hidden + proof-debt; adaptive channel selection not established (§6.13.15).

Terms

Glossary.

Repertoire a controlled set of mechanisms
A set of adaptive mechanisms, each of which carries new signal, toggles cleanly for an on/off contrast, and is scored on an architecture-independent channel — the three filters that separate a real win from a confound.
ABox a per-role belief store
In description logic, the set of assertions about individuals (as opposed to the TBox of definitions). Here, per-role turn-stamped belief stores — a role's acquired ABox about another role is its theory-of-mind.
Headroom room to learn
The gap that exists only when a no-policy replay (S0) fails a held-out scene and the policy replay (S1) succeeds via the registered policy. Structural, not probabilistic: present iff a shortcut exists and lands on a decoy rather than the target.
S0 no-policy replay
A held-out scene replayed with no policy memory — the control. If S0 already solves the scene, there is no headroom.
S1 policy replay
The same held-out scene replayed with the saved policy injected — differs from S0 by exactly one thing, the policy text.
Policy memory the distilled repair
A one-paragraph distillation of the repair the tutor improvised on the training scene, injected into S1 to test whether it transfers. The one channel in the arc that reliably carries new signal.
Control-leak a contaminated control arm
When a control (no-device) arm earns recognition votes anyway — usually because the generator wrote a learner that visibly self-corrects. A hard invalidator: a lift over leak-contaminated pairs is not trustworthy.
Intersubjective orientation the active ingredient
The pedagogical stance of treating the learner as a partner whose understanding is co-constructed — shared across recognition theory and its constructivist descendants. The active ingredient behind recognition's effect; the specific Hegelian vocabulary is only its marker.
Costume the look of recognition without the substance
Text that wears the surface signs of a recognition (insight-declaration, callback to an earlier turn) without the structural recohering. The dramatic instrument cannot reliably tell a costume from the real thing.
