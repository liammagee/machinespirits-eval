How four old theories became testable architecture

Recognition, the divided mind, charisma, and dramatic form — read as design heuristics, built into agents, and then measured. This page is the map between the theory and the machine.

The project's wager is that a handful of theories about how people change each other — Hegel on mutual recognition, Freud on the divided psyche, Weber on charisma, Aristotle on dramatic recognition — are not decoration on an AI tutor but instructions for building different ones. Each theory is translated into a concrete mechanism, wired into a prompt, an agent, or a rubric, and then put in front of the data. This page collects that translation in one place and reports, beside each thread, what the measurement returned — including where a theory-motivated mechanism produced nothing.

01·

The wager

Theory as a source of different machines, not a gloss on one.

Most AI-tutor work picks an architecture and then reaches for language to describe it. This project runs the other way: it takes a theory of how a subject is changed through encounter with another, derives an operational design rule from it, and lets that rule produce an architecture that can be compared against its absence. The recognition prompt is not a description of Hegel — it is the instruction build on what the learner actually said, which narrows what the model can output. The superego is not a metaphor sitting on top of one agent — it is a second agent whose critique the first can refuse.

The stance throughout is deliberately modest about what is being claimed. The tutor does not recognise the learner in any metaphysical sense; the architecture produces functional analogues of recognition — measurable differences in output where there would otherwise be a generic response. The claims are behavioural (designed information flows produce measurable output differences), not cognitive (the machine has an inner life).

i.

Heuristic translation, not metaphysics stated limit

The design heuristic borrowed from a theory is judged by whether it yields a measurable functional analogue — calibration where there would be a generic answer, self-revision where there would be compliance — never by whether the machine really recognises, desires, or feels.

§3.5 · §8.1 The paper draws the behavioural-vs-cognitive line explicitly: it studies recognition-oriented design, not recognition proper.

02·

Hegel · mutual recognition

One theory, three mechanisms, at three levels of the machine.

Hegel's Phenomenology — the master–slave dialectic, Anerkennung, Bildung — is the project's spine. It is read as three distinct design claims, each operating at a different layer, so each can be tested on its own:

i.

Calibration — prompt level supported

Mutual acknowledgment means a response shaped by the specific content of the other's contribution. Operationalised as a prompt constraint, it should narrow the output distribution. It does: within-response dimension spread falls from 0.619 to 0.539 (d=0.52), replicating on a second model (0.617→0.499, d=0.64), and the largest gains land on the weakest baseline dimensions. It holds in single-agent cells, so it is genuinely prompt-level.

ii.

Error correction — architecture level supported · model-dependent

Bildung means being changed by the encounter, not merely complying. The ego must be receptive to the superego, not obedient to it — so the ego keeps final authority. Under recognition the superego's added value collapses (+9.0 points base → +0.2 recognition) because the ego already meets its standard; superego approval jumps (13.3%→55.1% on one model, 51.6%→66.1% on another). The magnitude tracks the generation model's strength.

iii.

Adaptive responsiveness — interaction level null

The dialectic is temporal: each turn should be reshaped by the last. Predicted as rising per-turn trajectories under recognition. The well-powered analysis returns nothing — d=0.03 on tutor slopes where the design could detect d≥0.27 — and a lone exploratory effect in one scenario failed a pre-registered replication across models and judges. The temporal claim does not translate into measurable trajectory differences in 3–5 turn dialogues.

Where it lives in the machine: the recognition prompts (tutor-ego-recognition.md, tutor-superego-recognition.md), the recognition cell families, the recognition_quality rubric dimension, and a ten-concept Hegelian lexicon used to measure whether recognition cells actually concentrate the vocabulary (analyze-recognition-lexicon.js).

03·

Freud · the divided agent

Recognition is built through Freud's structural model.

If Hegel says what has to happen, Freud's topology says how to wire it. Each tutoring response is produced by two agents: an ego that generates, and a superego that judges against pedagogical and recognition criteria before anything reaches the learner. The superego is conceived as a ghost — a memorial trace of authority that judges but cannot negotiate. The load-bearing design choice is that the ego keeps final authority: it revises after critique but is not forced to comply. That single choice is what makes receptivity vary by condition, and that variation is exactly what the error-correction analysis exploits.

The architecture is bilateral and symmetric: the learner has its own ego→superego→revision loop, mirroring the tutor's, with matching trace labels on both sides. Two inversions of the standard stack are studied as their own families: divergent superegos (suspicious / adversary / advocate dispositions) and the id-director (cells 101–109), which flips the topology — an id authors a fresh ego persona each turn, with no post-hoc superego.

i.

The memory engineering is not the mechanism ablated

A controlled Writing-Pad ablation shows the three-layer memory pad is not load-bearing for recognition — the effect lives at the prompt level, not in the memory machinery.

04·

Weber · charisma

A second kind of authority, measured on its own register.

Weber names an authority that recognition theory deliberately sets aside: the charismatic kind, grounded in extraordinariness and co-constituted by followers who grant it. The project gives this its own instrument rather than folding it into recognition. The id-director family (cells 101–109) authors a charismatic persona each turn, and a Weber-derived 8-dimension charisma rubric scores it — extraordinariness (escaping the default-AI register), compositional arc, rhetorical rhythm, affective intensity, distillation, persona signature, positioning, and a co-constitutive invitation that is explicitly contrasted with Hegelian mutual recognition.

The rubric is versioned independently of the main tutor rubric and stored in its own columns, so charisma and recognition can be cross-correlated rather than conflated. The point of the split is conceptual hygiene: a tutor can carry a room by charm in a way that is not the same thing as treating the learner as an autonomous subject, and the project keeps the two measurable apart.

i.

Two authorities, two instruments by design

Weberian co-constituted authority (the learner's affirmation validates the tutor) is scored separately from Hegelian recognition (the learner is treated as an equal source of meaning). Keeping them apart is what lets either one be studied.

05·

Aristotle · poetics

The dialogue as drama; the critic as the audience.

The dramatic-recognition arc makes a sharp move: if a learner's actual learning is inaccessible, locate the recognition where Aristotle put it — in the audience. The tutoring transcript is read as a drama, the evaluator as a literary critic, and the scoring is on dramatic form at the whole-transcript level: peripeteia (reversal), anagnorisis (a recognition the speaker could not have said a moment before), surprise-and-inevitability, unity of action, the misconception read as hamartia, and a cathartic close. A six-dimension poetics rubric, calibrated first on canonical scenes (Sophocles, Plato) versus flat exposition, scores it.

The pilot that pushed hardest — a learner led to discover a withheld, contingent fact through metered evidence — found that premise-licensing is the load-bearing move: a tutor freed to meter premises elicits discovery 7/9, while a tutor that simply states the answer scores 0/4 by-reasoning (told, but not reasoned to).

i.

Form, not mind-reading hard limit

The instrument classifies dramatic form; it does not detect real learning or interior states. The Phase-2 transfer to tutoring transcripts failed (weighted κ≈0.04 against a ≥0.60 target): the codex critic over-attributes recognition roughly threefold, fooled by the costume of recognition vocabulary. Treat critic divergence here as a finding about gullibility, not as a calibration to chase.

06·

The wider family · a deflation the project runs on itself

The active ingredient is the orientation, not the vocabulary.

Recognition theory is one articulation of a commitment shared across a whole family — Vygotsky's zone of proximal development, Piaget's accommodation, Kapur's productive failure, and the cognate work of Chi, VanLehn, and Graesser all carry it, in different words. The project tests whether Hegel's specific vocabulary is doing the causal work, and finds it is not: a prompt matched for specificity but grounded in those constructivist descendants reproduces the recognition effect (d≈0.14–0.19 between them, i.e. equivalent), while a matched prompt grounded in the orthogonal behaviourist tradition scores well below baseline (d=0.89 beneath it). The between-family gap (d=1.38) dwarfs every within-family contrast by an order of magnitude.

So the lever is intersubjective-pedagogy orientation, not the Hegelian terminology — and this does not displace recognition theory so much as locate it. The constructivists inherited the intersubjective commitment from Hegel, often without attribution; recognition is its most explicit statement. The vocabulary earns its place by making the commitment legible, not by being the unique cause.

i.

Vocabulary clarifies; orientation causes A10 / A10b

Between-family d=1.38 ≫ within-family d≈0.14–0.19. Swapping Hegel's words for Vygotsky's barely moves the result; swapping the orientation for behaviourism collapses it.

07·

The ledger

What is load-bearing, and what the data refused.

A reference page earns its keep by being even-handed about the nulls. The throughline of the project is that two of three pre-registered Hegelian mechanisms replicate across three generation models and three judges, the third does not, and several theory-motivated elaborations bought nothing.

Carries weight

  • Calibration (Hegel, prompt level) — variance narrowing, replicated, floor-lifting. supported
  • Error correction (Freud's receptivity, architecture level) — superego value tracks model strength; the ego's retained authority is the hinge. supported · model-dependent
  • Intersubjective orientation (the whole family) — the between-family d=1.38 is the real lever. supported
  • Premise-metering (Aristotle, as form) — a tutor can be built to lead a learner to a withheld inference. supported · as form

The data refused

  • Adaptive responsiveness (the temporal dialectic) — d=0.03, well-powered null, failed replication. null
  • The specific vocabulary — Hegelian wording is not the cause; the orientation is. deflated
  • Catharsis as learning — the poetics instrument reads dramatic form, not durable learner change. out of reach
  • Dramatic transfer — the codex-trained critic does not transfer to tutoring transcripts (κ≈0.04); it is fooled by the costume. failed transfer
i.

The headline, plainly § abstract

Two of three pre-registered mechanisms replicate across all three tested models and three judges; their magnitude and architectural completeness vary with generation quality; the third is null on the primary well-powered analysis. Generalisation to much weaker or frontier models stays an open question.

08·

For future development

Where the theory points next.

These are open directions, not findings — each maps to a live or paused workplan item, and none of them is claimed here.

  • The learning door. Every interior claim is bounded by the synthetic learner. The human-learner validation pilot is engineering-complete and parked on IRB approval and real materials — it is the only path to the catharsis / durable-change question the poetics arc cannot reach. §8.1 · A1
  • More than two parties. The bilateral theory-of-mind machinery is inert in the dyad; a group-ToM regime is where explicit social-mental-state modelling is expected to start paying. §6.8.8
  • The concealed interior. The project owns the learner's hidden deliberation as ground truth, which makes manifest ≠ latent directly testable — modelling the concealed interior rather than the spoken surface. Adaptation Plan 2.0
  • Formal proof of derivation. The derivation arc shows a tutor can be made to lead a learner to a hidden answer under a rule-checker rather than a quality judge — the cleanest did they get there signal the project has. §6.13
