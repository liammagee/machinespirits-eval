# Weber, Id-Charisma, and Recognition

Status: bounded workplan closeout note for `refresh-weber-id-charisma-recognition-thread`.

## Decision

No Paper 2.0 source edit is needed in this pass. The current §6.7 already states the key boundary: the charisma rubric measures Weberian co-construction of authority and extraordinary presence, while v2.2 `recognition_quality` measures Hegelian treatment of the learner as an autonomous subject. The two rubrics are correlated but not redundant, and the cell family is explicitly scoped as architectural exploration rather than evidence of real learner uptake.

## Distinctions

- Hegelian mutual recognition: the tutor recognizes the learner as an autonomous subject and shapes the pedagogy around that autonomy.
- Weberian being-recognized-as-authoritative: the tutor's authority becomes legitimate only through a call-and-response in which the learner can affirm the tutor's exceptional force.
- Generic rhetorical charisma: surface register, vividness, and fluency without evidence of either learner autonomy or legitimate authority.

## Boundary

The current evidence licenses claims about tutor-side architecture and judge-scored text quality. It does not license claims that real learners recognize the tutor's authority, internalize it, or learn more because of it. That question remains downstream of human learner validation or a future learner-uptake design.

## Future Work, Not Current Debt

If this thread reopens, the clean experiment is not another prose framing pass. It would need a learner-side uptake instrument: either real learners, or at minimum a simulated learner trace/rubric that separately scores whether the tutor is recognized as legitimate rather than merely fluent or vivid.
