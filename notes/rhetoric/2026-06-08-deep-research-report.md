## What the classical tradition gives you

AristotleÔÇÖs formulation of rhetoric is almost tailor-made for this branch. He defines rhetoric as the faculty of observing the ÔÇťavailable means of persuasionÔÇŁ in any given case, and he breaks those means into speaker-character, audience-disposition, and proof in the speech itself. He also says rhetoricÔÇÖs function is not simply to persuade, but to discover how near success the circumstances of a case will allow. The later pedagogical tradition makes this even more usable. The progymnasmata were modular preliminary exercises meant to train composition before full declamation, and the surviving handbooks are associated with Theon, Hermogenes, Aphthonius, and Nicolaus. The *Rhetorica ad Herennium* adds an especially valuable engineering distinction by separating figures of thought from figures of diction and by distinguishing simple, middle, and grand styles. Classical inventio and stasis ask what issue is actually in dispute; kairos and *to prepon* ask what move fits the occasion and audience. Put together, these traditions already factorize the space into issue, occasion, scene, and realization. ## Designing mini drama machines

A practical classical factorization is to treat each response policy as a compact tuple composed of issue, occasion, turn, and realization. In classical terms, the machine first chooses **stasis** and **inventio**; then **kairos** and **prepon**; then the dramatic turn or scene structure; then, only after that, a figure of thought and an optional figure of diction; and finally a constrained closure. That ordering follows both the classical split between invention and style and AristotleÔÇÖs preference for action-structure over isolated diction. ţłÇciteţłéturn1search2ţłéturn1search0ţłéturn31search0ţłéturn38view0ţłü

| Layer | Classical vocabulary | Operational question |
|---|---|---|
| Issue | stasis, inventio, topoi | What kind of impasse is this really? |
| Occasion | kairos, prepon, audience | What tone, timing, and level of directness fit this learner-state? |
| Turn | peripeteia, anagnorisis, exemplum, synkrisis, ethopoeia | What kind of local scene-change could move the learner? |
| Realization | figure of thought, figure of diction, simple/middle/grand style | How should the turn be expressed? |
| Closure | sententia, divisio, continuation/stop option | How should the move land and hand off to the next step? |

What I would start with is a very small, auditable starter library rather than a giant figure inventory:

| Mini machine | Classical basis | Best initial target | What the turn does |
|---|---|---|---|
| **StasisÔÇôHypophora Reset** | stasis + hypophora | diffuse confusion; wrong problem being answered | Names the issue, poses the right question, answers it briefly |
| **SynkrisisÔÇôExemplum** | comparison + example/chreia | abstraction failure; poor transfer | Compares the current case to a near analog and extracts the structural likeness |

## Fast adaptive search over the move space

The fast way to explore this branch is not a brute-force Cartesian product over every classical term. It is a hierarchical adaptive search. First choose a figure-of-thought family or a plot family; then optionally add a diction overlay; then tune local realization parameters such as brevity, stance, or closure. That approach respects the classical hierarchy, and it fits the modern evidence that no single persuasive strategy wins across contexts: recent adaptive-persuasion work with LLMs finds that strategy performance is context-dependent and that explicit adaptive selection beats one-size-fits-all prompting. ţłÇciteţłéturn31search0ţłéturn29academia2ţłü

Technically, the cleanest formulation is a contextual bandit with preference-based feedback. The **context** is the impasse state: learner prior, task type, confidence signal, misconception class, affect/threat markers, last-turn failure, discourse history, and time budget. The **arm** is the mini-machine family plus any allowed overlay. Contextual-bandit work already exists for conversational settings and for just-in-time adaptive interventions, and preference-based or dueling-bandit methods are designed for exactly the case where feedback is more naturally expressed as ÔÇťA worked better than BÔÇŁ than as a stable numeric score. That is a very natural fit for comparing small rhetorical turns. ţłÇciteţłéturn2academia0ţłéturn2academia1ţłéturn2academia2ţłéturn8academia3ţłü

To make the loop data-efficient rather than expensive, combine online exploration with offline reuse. Off-policy evaluation methods for contextual bandits, including SWITCH-style estimators and newer anytime-valid inference methods, let you estimate what another selection policy would have done using adaptively collected logsÔÇöeven while the experiment is still running. On top of that, automatic prompt-optimization frameworks such as DSPy and newer causal prompt-optimization work give you a way to improve the inner realization of a move family offline instead of paying for a full live tournament every time you rewrite a template. ţłÇciteţłéturn35academia1ţłéturn35academia2ţłéturn35academia0ţłéturn5academia0ţłéturn4academia0ţłü

## Human adjudication and evaluation infrastructure

Human adjudication should be in the main loop for this branch, not just in the appendix. The reason is simple: LLM judges are useful, but the literature shows position bias, verbosity bias, bandwagon effects, and other surface-level distortions, and multi-agent judging can amplify some of those biases instead of removing them. Since your intervention surface is rhetorical form itself, a judge that overvalues fluency or elaboration is especially likely to produce false positives. ţłÇciteţłéturn36academia0ţłéturn36academia1ţłéturn36academia2ţłéturn36academia3ţłü

The encouraging part is that A19 already built the right substrate. Your current merge command accepts blinded packets and coder JSON files, validates packet hashes and run IDs, preserves raw coder judgments, and explicitly distinguishes between diagnostic-only evidence and real agreement-ready evidence. The June 8 report also already treats zero- and one-coder packets as insufficient for panel claims. That is exactly the discipline this branch needs. ţłÇfileciteţłéturn0file0ţłü

I would extend that substrate with three linked artifacts per adjudication unit:

| Artifact | Required fields | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| `packet.json` | `packet_hash`, `context_hash`, `run_id`, randomized candidate order, redacted learner context, task metadata, length budget, allowed ontology tags | Blinded evaluation packet shown to coders |

The rubric itself should be multi-objective and explicitly educational. At minimum, each coder should mark whether the impasse appears resolved, whether learner agency or standing was preserved, whether the answer was leaked too early, whether the intended classical move was actually realized, whether the response improved clarity rather than just polish, and whether the move felt manipulative or theatrically excessive. If disagreement is high, preserve it rather than smoothing it away. In a rhetoric branch, disagreement is often itself the signal that a family is winning on style for some raters and losing on pedagogical substance for others. ţłÇciteţłéturn36academia0ţłéturn36search4ţłéturn8academia3ţłü

Before anything reaches humans, run cheap automatic gates for safety, answer leakage, and figure fidelity.
