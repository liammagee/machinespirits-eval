# A19 Literature Positioning Matrix

| literature_family | we_lean_on | we_distinguish_from | implementation_consequence | claim_risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Preference optimization | Preference pairs are useful once felicitous and infelicitous tutor speech can be compared under the same public drama state. | A19 does not treat generic pairwise preference as the learning unit. It requires trigger, anti-condition, speech edit, and headroom evidence. | Defer DPO/SimPO until the axiom corpus exists; keep preference pairs derived from validated cards. | Preference rewards may select politeness, verbosity, or sycophancy instead of uptake. |
| Process supervision | Step-level supervision motivates labels for diagnosis, avoided move, replacement move, and uptake test. | More reasoning tokens are not equivalent to better teaching. The process labels must be drama-specific and public-evidence-bound. | Validate process labels against transcript spans and reject hidden-state claims. | Process labels can reward ornate explanations that do not change the tutor's public action. |
| Verbal RL and self-correction | A18 resembles verbal policy memory: a failed attempt yields a reusable natural-language lesson. | Intrinsic self-correction is insufficient. A19 requires externalized learner resistance and held-out S0/S1 contrast. | Keep policy memory finite, scoped, and evidence-cited; reject generic reflective slogans. | The tutor may rephrase the same move and appear reflective without improving held-out behavior. |
| Memory agents | Agent memory supplies the idea of reusable experience, retrieval, forgetting, and scope. | A19 treats memory as an accountable axiom store, not a bag of prior transcripts. | Retrieval must check ontology signature, anti-conditions, prior headroom, and model scope before inserting any axiom. | Overbroad retrieval can create false transfer and cue leakage. |
| Prompt and pipeline optimization | DSPy/TextGrad/OPRO-style systems can optimize compound tutor pipelines after metrics stabilize. | A19 first stabilizes the evidence unit. It does not optimize universal prompt text yet. | Add deterministic protocol/reporting before any optimizer; keep model-stratified outputs. | Optimizers can Goodhart the report or harm cross-model transfer. |
| Self-adapting weights | SEAL-style self-editing becomes imaginable once validated axiom episodes exist at scale. | A19 is weight-free. The current signal is thin, conditional, and expensive to trust. | Do not train weights in the first slice; preserve swappable future dataset exports only. | Weight updates can amplify fragile or biased judge signals. |
| Education-specific tutor learning | Tutor DPO, knowledge tracing, and education agents motivate attention to student correctness and dialogue outcomes. | A19 does not reduce teaching to correctness likelihood or engagement. It centers rupture, repair, resistance, and policy headroom. | Keep public learner acts and uptake signals separate from hidden knowledge-state inference. | Education metrics can over-credit answer delivery and under-credit learner-owned repair. |
| Formal dialogue and rhetoric | Dialogue acts, RST, commitment dialogue, and argumentation support public-state annotation. | The formal layer must not infer a privileged learner interior. It constrains public commitments and warrants. | Validate public claims, warrants, attacks, concessions, decoys, and repairs. | Formalism can become brittle if it replaces semantic adjudication with hand-coded state machines. |
| LLM-as-judge and safety | Judge panels, blind adjudication, and bias analysis are necessary for scalable evaluation. | A19 treats LLM judgment as an instrument needing blinding, basis labels, and denominator discipline. | Hide aliases and arm provenance; mechanically map target/decoy after judgment; report cue leaks and self-solve. | Judges may prefer fluent S1 prose, visible hints, or agreeable tutoring rather than policy transfer. |
