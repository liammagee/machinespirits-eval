## Resolution

The loop resolves negatively and theoretically: A19 is now repeatedly exposing
target-granularity collapse, not transferable non-transfer repair. Under the
current blind free-text channel, learner-action repairs that withhold answer
delivery, use a diagnostic device, remove a scaffold, or require ownership tend
to be extracted as `transfer_control` whenever the committed public repair is a
learner application/check before closure.

Protocol v0.4 therefore adds `preserve_struggle` to the repair-type obligation
taxonomy. Future preserve-struggle screens must make the distinctive public
obligations visible before generation: explicitly withhold copyable completion,
remove or limit one scaffold, and require a bounded learner-owned decision. This
does not reclassify the v0.3 over-compliance screen as transfer.

Next lawful work is protocol-first, not another opportunistic card: either
design a repair family whose distinctive public obligations cannot collapse into
transfer-control, or run a stability/panel gate only after a future card actually
produces `policy_headroom` under the registered repair type.
