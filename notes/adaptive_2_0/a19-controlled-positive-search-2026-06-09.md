## Boundary

Human coding remains deferred. This note records only automated fixture
validation, real automated attempt-1 replay, bounded axiom induction, and
recursive-full S0/S1 stability screens, and v0.11 role-separated tutor/learner

## Result

No systemic S0/S1 difference was achieved under the current A19 protocol. The
first fresh attempt-1 survivor in this pass was
`over_compliance_copy_import_audit`; it admitted one bounded
`preserve_struggle` axiom, but the recursive-full stability screen was not
stable:

- `over_compliance_copy_import_audit_a`: 0/2 policy-headroom seeds,
  interpretation `no_stable_headroom`.
- `over_compliance_copy_import_audit_b`: 1/2 policy-headroom seeds,
  interpretation `mixed_unstable`.

The later v20-v21 branches found two additional attempt-1 survivors and two
additional admitted bounded axioms, but they also stopped below systemic
headroom. That stops before any Paper 2.0, atlas, sidecar, human-learning, or
broader A19 transfer claim.

Post-closeout continuation explored the non-S0-weakening options suggested by
the v10-v17 failure pattern:

- a public-warrant evidence-role family,
- a bounded-continuation dialogue shape,
- a staged learner-discovery variant,
- a taxonomy-aligned commitment-ledger variant,
- a free-text mapper fix for explicitly retracted decoy phrases, and
- compact S1 rendering of admitted axiom JSON.

Two new attempt-1 survivors were found
(`claim_evidence_role_mismatch_staged` and
`public_commitment_evidence_role_boundary`), but neither produced a stable
systemic S0/S1 difference under recursive-full S0. The search therefore remains
below claim threshold.

v0.11 then corrected a harness confound: earlier S0/S1 replay artifacts were
counterfactual transcript revisions in which one generator authored tutor,
learner, and stock-take text together. The role-separated harness gives S1
policy memory only to the tutor, gives the learner only public transcript plus
the generated tutor move, and blind-adjudicates the assembled public arms. Under
that cleaner test, `claim_evidence_role_mismatch_staged` produced no
policy-headroom seeds. `public_commitment_evidence_role_boundary_b` produced one
role-separated policy-headroom seed, but failed the added second seed as
mixed/unstable. That preserves the below-claim stop.

## Exhaustion Judgment

The over-compliance branch found a local controlled-positive candidate but not a
stable one. The productive-impasse branch repeatedly traded off answer leakage,
old-warrant misclassification, non-leakage, and recursive dyadic update; fixing
one blocker exposed another without producing an attempt-1 survivor.

The public-warrant branch got closer. A raw evidence-role mismatch blocked; a
bounded-continuation version blocked harder because the original learner already
contained too much diagnosis; a staged learner-discovery version survived
attempt-1 and admitted a bounded axiom, but stability remained mixed/unstable.
The taxonomy-aligned commitment-ledger version also survived attempt-1 and
admitted a bounded axiom, but recursive-full S0 reached the target on the first
stability seed. The compact-memory S1 renderer removed irrelevant prompt noise
but did not cause S1 to apply the policy on the staged held-out cards.

The remaining options that stay within the user's constraints are no longer
near-neighbor cards in the same branch. They require a new protocol-level
decision about the evidence unit, arm construction, critic specialization, or
model-stratified reporting. Weakening S0 and resuming human adjudication remain
out of scope for this branch.

The v0.11 role-separated branch removes the coordinated-transcript confound but
does not reverse the result. It converts the strongest remaining positive into a
single local seed that fails immediate k=2 stability.
