An adaptive tutor—or an increasingly precise simulator of one?

What the code and recent runs actually show, what remains unproven, and the shortest credible route to a tutor whose adaptation can be believed.

The direction is right; the claim is not yet earned. In three days this branch has built a serious experimental substrate: explicit interaction state, multiple policy families, profile-conditioned synthetic learners, fixed-horizon trajectory analysis, a pressure probe, frozen evidence-gate machinery, formal proof outcomes, and enough reporting to expose rather than conceal negative results. The best current result is a reproducible structure: policy rankings cross by learner profile, and simple policies remain hard to beat. The deepest current limitation is equally clear: the system has not yet validated its learner-state estimates, has not isolated instructional action from register, has not run the real four-arm field-planner gate, and has measured no human learning.

So: keep the explicit-state, guarded-policy, formal-evidence architecture. Stop treating richer fields as evidence in themselves. Make the next cycle about predictive validity, diagnostic value, meaningful pedagogical actions, immutable provenance, independent performance, and authentic learners.

What is working

Scientific instrumentation

Fixed horizons reveal differences hidden by eventual completion. Policy interleaving reduces temporal drift. Formal DAG coverage is independent of stylistic judge preference. The reports preserve rank reversals and failures.

What is real

Policy × profile interaction

Across Terra and Sonnet 5, the best turn-16 policy changes with learner profile. Bland wins the diligent profile; adaptive or adversarial policies lead the stress profiles. There is no universal winner.

What is not shown

A validated adaptive tutor

No learner-state calibration against people, no authentic transition model, no independent posttest or retention, no causal field advantage, and no generalization beyond one synthetic proof world.

What should happen next

Validate, then learn

Freeze provenance; test state prediction; separate move, task, support, and register; run the four-arm gate; log propensities; fit lean transition baselines; then cross into authentic logs and human studies.

Formally grounded

i.

The strongest architectural choice supported

Keep learner/tutor state, policy choice, LLM realization, safety authority, and outcome measurement separable. The code increasingly supports this decomposition, even though the two main runner scripts remain very large.

ii.

The strongest theoretical overreach exploratory

Velocity, curvature, attractors, and coupled fields are currently names for computed features and deterministic projections. They become scientific constructs only if they are reliable, invariant, calibrated, incrementally predictive, and intervention-sensitive.

Do not conflate Tutor-stub asks whether stance choice matters. Phase 6 asks whether move planning adds value.

i.

What field planning currently is engineering

A deterministic feature-and-prior system that scores candidate moves, applies scripts, logs projections, and can cede or take conduct authority under guards. This is a useful controller baseline.

The prior/delta tables are hand-authored. No fitted transition model demonstrates that the named fields predict how a learner will move.

ii.

What would make it a scientific field model open gate

  • Test–retest reliability and measurement invariance across worlds and model families.
  • Calibrated uncertainty for every dimension and explicit evidence provenance.
  • Held-out next-observation prediction beyond a lean mastery/confidence/difficulty state.
  • Predicted-versus-observed movement under intervention, with state-scramble controls.
  • Incremental policy value after matching action frequencies and safety authority.

The triaged tutor-stub-transition-reward-model card is therefore not a side quest; it is the bridge from geometric metaphor to empirical dynamics. Start with ridge/logistic and tree baselines, cross-fit by world/profile/model, and make the rich field earn every extra degree of freedom.

Promotion rule Even a Phase 6 pass licenses only: “field planning improves a predeclared formal failure mode without harming proof reliability.”

Code and experimental-integrity audit

Eleven things to fix before the next claim-bearing run.

  1. The Sonnet root plan is false. The saved qa-plan.json now says six core profiles, seven policies, one run, no interleaving, no pressure, and GPT-5.5—while row artifacts prove four sentinel profiles, five policies, three runs, interleaving, turn-6 pressure, and Sonnet 5. In run-tutor-stub-qa-matrix.js:419–459, --from-dir builds and writes a fresh default plan before skipping jobs. An analysis command overwrote the run manifest.
  2. Tutor-stub artifacts do not pin code. They lack Git SHA, dirty-tree fingerprint, policy/profile/config hashes, and resume lineage. Notes name a commit, but the artifact cannot prove it. Phase 6's manifest discipline should become shared infrastructure.
  3. The primary evidence is machine-local. exports/, .tutor-stub-auto-eval/, logs, and databases are ignored. Without checksum manifests and an archival destination, the evidentiary record can vanish or drift independently of the plans that cite it.
  4. Policy randomness is not replayable. sampleEngagementStanceDistribution() uses Math.random() at tutor-stub.js:4397–4422. The draw is logged, but no seed recreates the sequence. Interleaving reduces clock drift; it does not solve stochastic reproducibility.
  5. “Robust” means low spread, not good performance. At analyze-tutor-stub-auto-evals.js:628–633, a consistently bad policy is labeled robust if mean minus worst score is below .12. Add adequacy and non-inferiority gates; rename the existing property “low cross-profile dispersion.”
  6. The outcome composite is still constructed. Removing register diversity was correct. The remaining weighted composite should not be the confirmatory endpoint. Predeclare raw turn-16 proof coverage, grounded-by horizon, safety, and later transfer/retention outcomes.
  7. Learner generation and validation are entangled. Profile contracts specify target evidence/stance/agency distributions that the same model family then produces and classifies. This is a manipulation check for contract obedience, not an external validity test.
  8. There is role monoculture. In Sonnet, the tutor, learner, and analyzer are all claude-sonnet-5. Formal proof state prevents total circularity, but semantic state and profile success may reflect model self-consistency. Cross model roles or use an external, human-calibrated label channel.
  9. The safety guard is an unbalanced co-intervention. Sonnet recorded 162 candidate guard matches, repaired 253 turns, and replaced 112 of 2,111 tutor turns (5.3%) with a deterministic public-evidence fallback; Terra recorded only two matches across 1,820 turns. These are audit hits before/through repair, not 162 learner-visible leaks. The guard preserves safety, but the measured treatment is sometimes policy-plus-repair or replacement. Generic matched tokens such as “under,” “glass,” and “silver” suggest over-triggering. Log original-candidate, repaired-output, and fallback exposure by policy; tighten matching; and add a guard-frequency-yoked control.
  10. The decisive experiment is tiny and narrow. One world, four authored profiles, five policies, three draws. Coverage changes in sixths. No confidence intervals, hierarchical model, preregistered minimum effect, or independent replication set.
  11. The implementation boundary is strained. Two scripts contain more than 21k lines between them. Extract run manifests, profile contracts, policy sampling, action schema, artifact IO, and analysis adapters before another experimental dimension multiplies the coupling.
Immediate stop condition
Do not launch a claim-bearing Phase 6 or larger tutor-stub matrix until the immutable-manifest bug is fixed and regression-tested. A frozen experiment whose root plan can be rewritten during analysis is not frozen.

09·

Strategies still underexplored

The missing moves are mostly about identification, not more persona.

01 · Active diagnosis

Ask to reduce uncertainty

Choose a question for expected information gain, not immediate proof progress. Compare information-seeking, reward-greedy, and fixed-sequence policies.

02 · Difficulty-aware belief

Separate learner from item

Track mastery and ability jointly with item difficulty/discrimination. Benchmark full fields against this lean probabilistic state.

03 · Closed-loop scaffolding

Support, observe uptake, fade

Add support level, expected uptake, fade condition, responsibility owner, independent-work window, and recovery path.

04 · Outer-loop curriculum

Adapt what comes next

Select knowledge component, retrieval spacing, interleaving, example difficulty, and worked-versus-problem mode across turns and sessions.

05 · Hybrid learned policy

Learn ranking inside hard guards

Compare hand-coded planner, learned candidate ranker, hybrid ranker plus veto, strong fixed, random, and frequency-yoked policies.

06 · Calibrated hypotheses

Do not collapse to one learner story

Maintain weighted hypotheses, evidence for/against, and the next discriminating observation. Drop dimensions that fail held-out prediction.

07 · Typed longitudinal memory

Remember with expiry and contradiction

Store evidence IDs, confidence, validity, supersession, contradictions, retrieval reason, and action preconditions. Test stale-memory harm.

08 · Co-pilot before autonomy

Use human overrides as data

Recommend a move, evidence, confidence, and alternative to real tutors; measure acceptance, override, and downstream transitions.

09 · Assistance dependence

Make independence the endpoint

Measure unassisted posttest, delayed retention, near/far transfer, hint dependence, agency, and calibration—not assisted closure alone.

10 · Simulator triangulation

Use disagreement as uncertainty

Run independently constructed learner models, hidden latent states, targeted/misaligned feedback, and human-calibrated transition checks.

Several strategies have already been explored enough to lower their priority: richer internal state for its own sake, bilateral ToM without action-value evidence, more critic/deliberation layers, and style-heavy universal policies. Preserve them as conditional mechanisms for a diagnosed failure mode, not as the default architecture.

Strategic correction Move from “which interior description is richest?” to “which uncertain state predicts the next observable and changes the right action?”

Final answer to the strategic question

Yes, the project is on the right path—if “path” means explicit state, guarded control, formal evidence, and aggressive falsification. It is on the wrong path whenever it equates richer internal description with a better learner model, simulated persona fidelity with human validity, or register diversity with adaptive pedagogy. The recent negative results are not detours; they are the reason the next architecture can be smaller, more identifiable, and more credible.

The next breakthrough should not be a more elaborate interior. It should be a state estimate that predicts, a diagnostic action that informs, a scaffold that fades, and an outcome that survives when the tutor is gone.PLAN 4.0 audit · 2026-07-11

Bottom line Preserve the machine; narrow the ontology; widen the action space; harden the evidence.
