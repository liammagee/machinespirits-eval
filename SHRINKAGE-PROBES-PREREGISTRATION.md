## Limb B — within-seed replication (the drift test)

**Question:** is the smoke-to-confirmatory gap attributable to backend drift across the time window, or to sampling variance at fixed conditions? The record's one accidental datum (the flash smoke inverting at its own seeds) points at sampling variance; this makes the measurement deliberate.

**Design:** re-run the register-router smoke's EXACT six arms — same world (marrick), same config, same decay seeds **419/421/431** (reused BY DESIGN, declared), same provider (Sonnet CLI), same labels — **twice**, into fresh dirs (`register-router-smoke-rerun1`, `-rerun2`). Together with the original smoke this gives three replications of the identical 6-arm experiment, hours-to-days apart.

**Pre-stated reads (B):**
1. **Outcome-flip count:** across the three replications, how many of the six arm-slots change outcome class (grounded vs cap-death) between any two replications? **If ≥ 2 of 6 slots flip, within-seed sampling variance is established as the dominant term** — decay seeds do not pin outcomes, smoke-tier n = 3 contrasts are formally uninformative about mechanisms, and drift is unnecessary as an explanation of the §5.12.7 record. If ≤ 1 slot flips (outcomes nearly pinned), sampling variance is small at fixed seeds and the drift explanation returns to the table with a design for testing it (a matrix-window replication).
2. The smoke's arm-level pattern (router 3/3 vs baseline 1/3) either recurs in the replications or does not — recorded plainly.
3. Router fires per arm across replications (does the same seed produce the same fires?) — descriptive.

## Limb A — null-scaling controls (the selection-asymmetry test)

**Question:** the observed record is 100% *advanced* signals (nulls are never re-run), so near-universal decline is the censored-sample expectation under zero truth. Scaling KNOWN-NULL smokes breaks the censoring: under the zero-truth + symmetric-noise account, scaled nulls should move symmetrically (signs random, occasional excursions); if scaled nulls sit dead while promoted smokes always collapse, the asymmetry would be real evidence against the account.

**A1 — placebo scaling (Sonnet).** The learner-mirror-refusal smoke was a perfect null with an *inert* arm (zero fires; §6.13.18 coda): scaling it is a pure harness placebo — the arms differ by a flag that does nothing. n = 10/arm on marrick at dose 0.08, pair-interleaved, fresh primes **541, 547, 557, 563, 569, 571, 577, 587, 593, 599**; arms `baseline` vs `mirror-refusal` (`--learner-mirror-refusal on`, real trigger — expected to keep firing zero times).
**A2 — active-null scaling (codex).** The codex register-router smoke was null (1/3 vs 1/3 grounded) with an *active* arm (6 evidenced fires): scaling it measures the fate of a null-coloured active mechanism. n = 10/arm on marrick at the codex dose 0.35, pair-interleaved, fresh primes **601, 607, 613, 617, 619, 631, 641, 643, 647, 653**; arms `baseline` vs `register-router`. **Declared explicitly: the register-router outcome question is CLOSED (§6.13.19) and stays closed; A2 licenses no mechanism reading in either direction.**

**Pre-stated reads (A):**
1. For each scaled null: pooled Δ (T\*, cap+1 = 29 imputed) and one-sided U at 10/10 — reported as a *measurement of the null distribution*, not as a test of anything.
2. **Excursion check:** does either scaled null produce a signal of the size that got smokes promoted (|Δ| ≥ 1.5, or a 3-run stretch of same-direction pair-splits)? If yes, tantalizing-thread-sized signals arise from known nulls and the promoted smokes' colour was unremarkable — the zero-truth account confirmed from the other side.
3. **Sign symmetry:** the two scaled nulls' Δ signs (random under the account; both-dead-flat would be the anomaly worth noting).
4. Conduct sanity: leaks 0; A1 fires expected 0 (any fire is reported with its evidence); A2 fires evidenced as before.

## Results addendum (2026-07-07; frozen analysis unmodified; reports at `exports/classifier-dag/shrinkage-probes/{limb-b-and-a1,limb-a2}.md`)

**Execution (deviations disclosed).** Limb B ran ONE replication instead of two: the second was skipped under the operator's mid-programme directive moving all new launches to the codex CLI (an exact-conditions Sonnet replication is meaningless on another provider), and the flip criterion was already met at two replications. Limb A1 ran on CODEX rather than the frozen Sonnet under the same directive (the placebo logic is provider-independent — both arms share whatever provider runs them; disclosed, primes unchanged) and survived one external stop with a label-exact trimmed resume at 6/20. Limb A2 hit the codex quota wall at 12/20 and resumed label-exact after the window refreshed. Zero parse deaths anywhere; every failure was quota or external stop, recovered without touching completed artifacts.

**Limb B — within-seed replication: SAMPLING VARIANCE DOMINANT; drift unnecessary.** Across the two replications of the register-router smoke's six arms at identical seeds/config/provider, **2 of 6 slots flipped outcome class** (criterion ≥ 2 met): baseline-r1 aporia → grounded 23, baseline-r2 grounded 22 → aporia — one flip in each direction, the signature of symmetric noise, not of a provider that drifted one way. Router fires also varied at fixed seeds (r1 and r3: 1 → 0). Leaks 0. The pre-stated consequence: decay seeds do not pin outcomes; smoke-tier n = 3 contrasts are formally uninformative about mechanisms; the drift explanation of the §5.12.7 record is unnecessary. Nuance recorded plainly, no claim: the smoke's *aggregate* pattern (routers 3/3 grounded, baselines 1/3) recurred in the replication even as individual slots churned — at n = 6 this is unresolvable and nothing rests on it.

**Limb A — null scaling: A KNOWN NOTHING PRODUCED A PROMOTED-THREAD-SIZED SIGNAL.** A1 (the placebo — the learner-mirror-refusal flag fired **zero** times in all ten treat runs, making the arms prompt-identical): treat grounded **10/10 vs baseline 6/10**, Δ = **−2.80**, one-sided U = **80/100** — past the exact one-sided 0.05 point (≥ 73 at 10/10). **The excursion check fires: had this been a mechanism, it would have cleared a pre-registered significance bar.** A pure-luck signal larger than most of the programme's promoted exploratory colours, from an arm that mechanically does nothing. A2 (the active null — 35 evidenced router fires, leaks 0): Δ = −0.70, U = 58/100, grounded 7/10 vs 5/10 — an unremarkable wobble. Sign note: both scaled nulls landed treat-favourable (probability 1/4 under symmetric signs — unremarkable); the operative read was the excursion, and it landed.

**Consequences applied (per the freeze).** (i) *Which term dominates smoke-tier variance:* within-seed LLM sampling variance — the seeds pin only the decay schedule, and identical conditions change a drama's ending roughly a third of the time. (ii) *Does the censored record's asymmetry survive an uncensored control?* **No.** Scaled nulls move — one spectacularly upward — so regression operates in both directions and the record's apparent one-way curse was produced by scaling only the lucky tails. The zero-truth + tail-selection account of §5.12.7 is confirmed from both sides: promoted colours collapse (six specimens) and unpromoted nothings inflate (A1). (iii) *Smoke-design recommendation (standing):* smoke-tier outcome colour on these near-binary worlds is uninformative at any n small enough to be called a smoke — a known placebo cleared the 0.05 point at n = 10/arm. Smokes license only mechanism-availability reads (fires, conduct, leaks); outcome claims begin at pre-registered tier with §5.12.7 shrunken-estimate sizing, and motivating colour should be reported without arithmetic (no Δ, no U) to remove the anchor entirely.
