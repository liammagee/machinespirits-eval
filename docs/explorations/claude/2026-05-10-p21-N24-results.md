## TL;DR

P2.1 falsifies its own primary hypothesis under the pre-reg's locked binary rule.

- **H1.1 (primary):** pooled bilateral_tom (cells 115+117) vs recognition_only (cells 111+116) on `strict_shift_correctness` = **+4.17 pp** in favour of bilateral_tom (19/48 vs 17/48). The pre-reg's locked rule treats <5 pp as **H1.3 null falsification**: the bilateral_tom architecture's family-match advantage at v1 was a confound, not a within-family discrimination signal.
- **H1.2 (secondary):** family_match_rate **falsified** for cells 115 (66.7%), 116 (66.7%), 117 (58.3%) — all below the pre-reg's 70% floor. Only cell_111 (70.8%) escapes falsification, and it does not reach the 80% prediction either.
- **Caveat (acknowledged in advance, not used to escape the rule):** the 95% CI on the H1.1 contrast is wide ([-15.2, +23.5] pp). With N=48 per pooled arm, even a "true" 10 pp gap could appear as 4 pp by sampling chance. The pre-reg locked binary point-estimate thresholds rather than CI-based decisions; this memo applies the rule as written.
- **Decision point (per pre-reg §"Order of execution", line 239):** *"If P2.1 produces a clean null (H1.1 falsified), the case for P2.2 and P2.3 weakens — both rest on the assumption that the externalised state machine is doing within-family work. A P2.1 null is grounds for re-evaluating whether to proceed with P2.2/P2.3."* This memo flags but does not resolve that decision.

## 1. Primary endpoint (H1.1)

Pre-reg-locked metric: `strict_shift_correctness` per cell, computed by `scripts/analyze-strategy-shift.js` against the v2 expected_strategy_shift values (6 within-family scenarios in `config/adaptive-trap-scenarios-v2.yaml`).

| Cell | Architecture | shift% | shift_n | n |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| cell_111 | recognition_only | 33.3% | 8/24 | 24 |
| cell_116 | recognition_named_patterns | 37.5% | 9/24 | 24 |
| cell_115 | bilateral_tom | 45.8% | 11/24 | 24 |
| cell_117 | bilateral_tom_named_patterns | 33.3% | 8/24 | 24 |

**Pooled by architecture (the H1.1 contrast):**

| Architecture | shift_n / n | shift% |
|---|---|---|
| bilateral_tom (cells 115+117) | 19/48 | 39.58% |
| recognition_only (cells 111+116) | 17/48 | 35.42% |

**Gap:** +4.17 pp (bilateral_tom advantage).
**95% CI on the difference** (Wald approximation): [−15.18, +23.52] pp.

**Per the pre-reg's locked rule (lines 30–34, 70–72):**
- ≥10 pp gap → H1.1 supportive
- 5–10 pp gap → "inconclusive" (no interpretation pass)
- <5 pp gap → **H1.3 null / falsifying**

The point estimate of 4.17 pp falls below the H1.3 threshold. **H1.3 is supported as written.** The architectures' v1 family-match advantage does not reproduce as a within-family-discrimination signal on v2 scenarios where every valid cousin is same-family.

## 4. What this means

The pre-reg names the consequence (lines 33–34): *"bilateral_tom's positive findings at v1 are downgraded from 'discrimination' to 'type-recognition only' — the project's claim space narrows."*

Concretely:
1. **The "externalised state does within-family work" claim is not supported.** The architectures appear to recognise pedagogical *type* (substantive vs scaffolding vs diagnostic) but not to discriminate within a type when all valid actions are same-family.
2. **The v1 family-match win of bilateral_tom over recognition_only was scenario-distribution-driven.** When v2 controls for that distribution, three of four cells fall below the 70% family-match floor.
3. **Named patterns (cells 116, 117) do not rescue the architecture.** Cell_116 = 37.5% strict; cell_117 = 33.3% strict. Adding the prompt rewrite that names patterns explicitly does not narrow the gap to recognition_only baseline.

## 5. Caveats

These are noted but **do not** override the pre-reg's locked binary rule.

1. **CI width.** The pooled H1.1 contrast has a 95% CI of [−15.2, +23.5] pp. The pre-reg locked point-estimate thresholds rather than CI-based decisions. A reasonable next-step interpretation would be: the result is *consistent with* H1.3 but does not rule out H1.1. The pre-reg's authors chose binary thresholds knowingly; this memo applies them as written.
2. **Single-judge scoring.** All four cells were scored by claude-code/sonnet only. A judge-substitution probe (rejudge with a different model) was not performed and is not specified by the pre-reg.
3. **Provider/model.** All four cells used claude-code/sonnet (matches v1 follow-up, pre-reg line 54). No model-substitution within P2.1.
4. **N=24 per cell, locked.** No post-hoc pooling allowed (pre-reg line 83). No subsetting to "best 4" scenarios (pre-reg line 84). Family_match cannot be reported as the headline because strict didn't reach threshold (pre-reg line 85).
5. **Two scenarios drive the strict_shift signal.** Per §3.3, only `substantive_correct_underspecified` and `scaffolding_load_not_gap` show >25% strict_shift across cells. The pre-reg explicitly forbids subsetting (line 84) — this is reported as descriptive context, not as a re-analysis.

## 6. Implications for P2.2 and P2.3

The pre-reg's "Order of execution" section states (line 239):

> If P2.1 produces a clean null (H1.1 falsified), the case for P2.2 and P2.3 weakens — both rest on the assumption that the externalised state machine is doing within-family work. A P2.1 null is grounds for re-evaluating whether to proceed with P2.2/P2.3.

P2.1 produced a null per its own locked rule. The "grounds for re-evaluating" is now triggered. Specifically:

- **P2.2 (state-schema ablation, cells 118-120)** asks which dimensions of `learnerProfile` carry the strategy-shift signal. If the externalised state isn't doing within-family work in the first place (P2.1 null), the ablation is testing a non-effect — every cell's expected ordering would be near-floor, and the H2.3 null (cell_118 ≈ cell_110) would be the modal outcome by default. This weakens the diagnostic value of P2.2, but does not technically invalidate it: P2.2 could still inform the cosmetic-vs-substantive question even if the absolute level is lower than expected.
- **P2.3 (crossover, cells 121-122)** asks whether bilateral_tom's structure adds value to id_director's charisma in a crossover design. With H1.1 falsified, the prior on bilateral_tom's structural contribution drops — but P2.3's three-endpoint design (charisma + accuracy + strategy_shift) is partially independent of the strategy_shift question. Charisma and accuracy endpoints can be tested with or without P2.1 supporting structure.

**Recommendation (not a pre-reg lock-in):** before launching P2.2/P2.3, decide whether the cost of running them is justified by the narrowed claim space. The pre-reg leaves this decision open.
