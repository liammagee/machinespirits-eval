## 1. Introduction

*[To be drafted.]*

Working framing: Paper 2.0 establishes that recognition-enhanced prompts and multiagent architecture contest one default of LLM-agent conditioning --- the *self-contained ethical subject* inherited from instruction-tuning's customer-service framing (Ouyang et al. 2022) --- and produce measurable, traceable shifts in tutor behaviour. This paper investigates a second, structurally adjacent default: the agent as **appliance** (always-on, infinitely available, no internal horizon, bounded only by external constraints like token rate or session timeout). Where recognition contests the agent's default *subject-form*, finitude contests the agent's default *temporal-form*. The hypothesis is that simulating finitude structurally --- via Heideggerian *Sein-zum-Tode*, project-having (*Entwurf*) against a bounded horizon, the *Augenblick* of authentic resoluteness --- shifts the agent from being-available-for-queries to undertaking-something-bounded-with-a-learner-during-a-finite-arc. The finitude is hypothesised to be enabling, not maudlin.

See `notes/design-cell-110-being-towards-death.md` §1--2 for the full motivation and theoretical framing.
