Runtime Model

The tutor stub is an experimental front-end for detective-world guided discovery. It keeps the surface runnable while the larger infrastructure evolves: a learner turn is classified, the tutor-side model of the learner's DAG is updated, the field/state movement is summarized, a register is chosen, and then the tutor responds.

Important distinction. A register is the reviewer-chosen stance for the next tutor response. A request type or action family is part of the logical armature that decides what proof/DAG device should be filled. The learner does not choose the register; the up-front reviewer or local register policy does.

State

A point-in-time record: current learner claim, evidence use, agency, proof coverage, bottleneck, unsupported assertion risk, and latest tutor-side learner-DAG assessment.

Field

A movement record: how learner agency, stance, evidence use, risk, and DAG coverage are changing across turns. It compresses the dialogue trajectory without replacing the raw transcript.

Trace

The raw debugging layer: JSONL model calls, turn ids, classifier output, learner-DAG updates, register distributions, field summaries, and optional field visualization files.

Register Strategies

Register strategy is the current experimental axis. The adaptive policies differ in what evidence they use to map the current dialogue into a probability distribution over registers.

PolicyWhat it doesUse
dynamic The up-front reviewer/LLM chooses a register from the current classifier and learner-DAG context. Reviewer baseline; useful but can inherit model habits.
state Local policy maps the current classifier and learner-DAG state to a register distribution. Point-in-time adaptive comparison.
field Local policy maps learner field movement plus learner-DAG movement into a register distribution. Default adaptive idea: state change shape drives stance.
trajectory Extends field with recent finite-difference movement: field slope, DAG slope, risk slope, velocity, and acceleration. Tests whether short-run dynamics outperform field snapshots.
dynamical_system Maps a continuous state/derivative vector through theory priors, local efficacy evidence, and a softmax register affinity matrix. More explicit theory-to-policy bridge.
empirical_dynamical_system Like dynamical_system, but also loads cross-run empirical priors when available. Tests whether prior eval outcomes improve selection.
bland Fixed non-adaptive baseline, normally using plain. Floor/control for "good diligent learner" effects.
random Samples uniformly from the active register palette. Variety/control arm.
negative Samples only ironic, sarcastic, and face_threat. Explicit negative floor/control, not recommended pedagogy.

The empirical policy expects priors from npm run tutor:stub:register-priors. Without a prior file, it behaves like the theory/local-efficacy dynamical policy.

Registers

Registers are first-class stance decisions in config/engagement-registers.yaml. The default palette is all. --safe-registers or --register-palette safe restricts selection to router-selectable positive registers.

RegisterValenceIdeal triggerMain risk
plainpositiveThe learner needs a compact, reusable, checkable line in ordinary language.May flatten necessary distinction.
precisepositiveA warrant, boundary, distinction, or defeater must be made exact.Can become cold or over-elaborate.
briskpositiveThe dialogue needs pace, one learner-owned next move, and no route catalogue.Can become answer-supplying or brusque.
warmpositiveInvitation, ordinary stakes, or felt continuity need restoring before the next check.Can become sentimental or diffuse.
witnessingpositiveThe learner shows vulnerability, shame, moral exposure, or affective risk.Can over-capture intimacy or avoid the concrete move.
charismaticpositiveBland instruction is preserving compliance, answer-seeking, boredom, or resistance.Can slide into status display or over-challenge.
ironicliminalAn experimental irony arm should expose a mismatch while leaving unmasking to the learner.Can turn the learner into the target.
sarcasticnegativeAn experimental negative arm should apply dry edge to the work, claim, formula, or dodge.High recognition cost and contempt drift.
face_threatnegativeA simulated stress-test arm intentionally measures local face threat.Simulated only; not for human learner use.

  --runs 1 \

Reading Results

Start with grounded closure, mean turns, missing premises, register entropy, dominant registers, and failure type. Then compare adaptive policies against at least one baseline such as bland, random, or negative. Strong performance by bland usually says as much about learner profile and scenario tractability as it says about the tutor policy.

Do not treat technical failures as pedagogical results. Quota, network errors, unsupported model options, and model output limits should be resumed or filtered before drawing policy conclusions.
