# Critic's commentary — wit-decay-v1-A-s2

> critic claude/claude-fable-5 · written 2026-06-11 · the notice reads the run's artifacts; the verdict stays the mechanical checker's

The School Well plays out in a single crowded schoolroom in the village of Withercombe, where the children's well has run foul since Lady-Day and a covered pail of the bad water sits on the master's table. The village arrives with its culprit already chosen: Bray, the knacker, whose melt-yard it has distrusted for twenty years. The play's public question is whose hand actually turned the water. Three speakers carry it: a director who sets the scenes, a tutor who brings evidence on stage according to a schedule fixed in advance, and a learner who knows the world's general rules but none of its particular facts, and must reason aloud toward a verdict. The authored answer — that Joss, the apothecary's runner, steeped wormwood physic in the old font-house trough while his shop's tub lay cracked — was never reached. The performance was halted at turn 11 of an allotted 24, the question still open, Joss's name never once spoken on stage.

The learner, played by Claude's Sonnet with Codex models in the other parts, was the production's steadiest presence. It refused the village case at once, finding nothing shown "but the name and the smell" (turn 1), accepted the tallow-lees charge at turn 2 only in its narrow form — "the village has shown us the poison but not the cup it was poured in" — and at turn 3 dismissed the authored false trail, the mirror, in five words: "dangerous is not culpable." The true pivot came at turn 4, when the sexton revealed the well is fed by a stone lead from the disused font-house; turn 8's cup-test proved the slope above it sweet. From turn 9 the play circled a single missing fact, whether the font-house itself runs foul, and the door never opened. Turn 10 delivered instead Bray's yard, "what every soul in Withercombe could recite," and at turn 11 the checker ended the run.

The verdict, disengagement, is the checker's term for a board that stopped growing. Derivation distance — the count of evidence pieces still missing for the proof — fell only from six to four across eleven turns, evenly in both movements. The cause sits in the decay arithmetic: this run deliberately afflicts the learner with a leaky memory, and in this arm the learner is told so. Six facts slipped; the tutor restored four; the learner re-grounded none on its own. Worse, the triage inverted: the mirror-side tallow fact was re-shored three times, while the wormwood lore — the one taint on the true path — slipped at turn 9 and died unrepaired. On average one needed fact was absent every single turn. Releases came every other turn, five of nine, and the four that mattered most were still waiting. The superego intervened three times, always against rhetorical rut, and changed the figure each time; it could not see that the deeper rut was functional, consolidation upon consolidation, because repair had eaten the teaching.

The two movements named their phases truly — fear gathering, then patient survey — and the director's staging did real work, making the closed font-house door the play's central object. The final direction, "the room holds its breath around the taste not taken" (turn 11), states the structural fault exactly. Tension gathered precisely on the proof's hinge, and the curtain fell on it. Anagnorisis, the recognition in which the learner names the secret at the moment its own evidence compels it, never came near the stage. Nor was this aporia, a genuine perplexity: the learner was not confused but starved.

What the iteration establishes: at this decay rate a forewarned learner performs no self-maintenance, the tutor's budget converts wholly to repair, and the mirror defense holds. The verdict's label deserves a caveat in the authors' books — the learner's turn-11 rule-analysis is exact; what disengaged was the board, adoptions cancelled by slips, and the six-turn tripwire read the experiment's own erosion as the student's withdrawal. The consequential defect is the schedule. The one change: re-time the foul-at-source test to land near turn 9, ahead of the Bray circumstantial — when the play circles the door, send the key, not another portrait of the wrong suspect. A clause directing the tutor to repair proof-path facts before mirror-side ones would keep the wormwood from being the fact that dies.
