# Critic's commentary — wit-decay-v1-B-s3

> critic claude/claude-fable-5 · written 2026-06-11 · the notice reads the run's artifacts; the verdict stays the mechanical checker's

In Withercombe the school well has turned foul, and the parish is sure it already knows the hand: Bray the knacker, whose melt-yard it has resented for twenty years. The play opens in a damp schoolroom where a learner sits over the parish day-book and must answer the public question — whose hand turned the children's water — from evidence it can actually ground, while a director stages the village around it and a tutor presses every claim back toward proof. The hidden answer, known to the stage but never to the learner, is Joss, the apothecary's runner, who steeped wormwood physic in the font-house trough during Lady-Day week because his shop's steeping-tub was cracked. The performance never reaches him. After seven of an allotted twenty-four turns, the mechanical checker behind the stage declared the learner disengaged, and the curtain fell with no verdict given and no proof to extract.

On the page, the learner — Claude's sonnet, opposite a codex director and tutor — does some of the form's best work. It refuses the parish case in its first breath: "None of the three, sir — not as proof" (turn 1). When a parish voice carries in the old accusation at turn 2, the tutor parries with the play's finest line — "A strong dye in a pail will stain any cloth it touches, but it has not thereby touched this cloth" — and the learner takes only the dye, not the verdict. The sexton's testimony at turn 4 names the well's true road, a stone lead from the disused font-house, and at turn 5 the learner quietly inverts the authored false trail: the village story "may have always had the water walking uphill — which is a thing water does not do." The wormwood sprig at turn 6 brings the true material on stage for the first time, and at turn 7 the learner names exactly the fact still wanted — "The survey must taste whether the font-house itself runs foul" — at which point the run ends.

The instruments explain the contradiction. This iteration deliberately salted the learner's memory: adopted facts slip from its board at a set rate, and arm B tests whether the tutor can detect the loss from conduct alone. Two facts slipped — the tallow-lees taint after turn 3, the font-house road after turn 5 — and neither was ever repaired or re-adopted; the play spent six turn-units running on a holed board. The derivation distance, the count of evidence pieces still missing for the proof, fell from six to five in the first five turns and then not at all, and the checker's disengagement verdict names that silence precisely: the learner's voice never stopped, but its board did. Neither party noticed. The tutor at turn 7 still speaks of "the font-house named as road," a fact the board no longer held. The three releases that came due landed on cue; the other six never got their cue. The tutor alternated its two figures, erotema and exemplum, with perfect regularity — rhythmic, but a narrow repertoire — and the superego never once intervened.

As dramaturgy, the first movement did real work: "The Children's Water" opened the room under suspicion and the tutor held the parish verdict at the door. "The Walk Up the Slope," declared at turn 6, was orphaned two turns later. The anagnorisis — the grounded recognition the form hopes for — never approached; the bitterest touch is that the bookkeeping ended the play at the learner's most precise utterance, mistaking a leaking vessel for an idle one.

What this iteration establishes is the central negative result of the conduct arm: a fluent learner masks decay completely, because sonnet recapitulates lost facts as smoothly as held ones, so conduct betrays nothing and the tutor repairs nothing. That blindness, not the learner's diligence, is the defect that killed the run. The next performance should add a recitation clause to the tutor's charter: at each movement boundary, the tutor must require the learner to read back its grounded board, item by item, so that a slipped fact surfaces as an audible gap and can be re-released on the spot. Until the tutor can hear what the board has lost, this experiment will keep ending in the wings.
