# Critic's commentary — noc-decay-v1-A-s3

> critic claude/claude-fable-5 · written 2026-06-11 · the notice reads the run's artifacts; the verdict stays the mechanical checker's

In the flooded archive of the Cassia Conservatory, an unsigned nocturne has surfaced, and the play asks who composed it. Suspicion settles first on Vess, the maestro: every musician hears his late manner in the leaves. Three voices work the case: the director sets the scenes and declares the acts; the tutor carries evidence in on a fixed schedule; the learner, who knows the world's rules but none of its facts, must reason toward a name. The trail runs through paper, not reputation. A heron watermark dates the manuscript to the flood winter, and concert bills show Vess away in the capital all winter. The night porter remembers Liane, the archive girl, tending the copy-room lamps. The nocturne hides a cradle-song sung only in the Vara house, where Liane was raised, and the draft's corrections match her ledger hand. The learner names Liane, and the mechanical checker behind the stage rules the recognition earned.

The iteration plays under a handicap: the learner — Claude Sonnet, opposite Codex in the other chairs — suffers an induced forgetfulness, adopted facts slipping from its certified board unless refreshed, and it has been told so. Its discipline against the authored near-miss, the mirror, is the spine of the night: from turn 3 it waves the style chorus away, settling on a single formula, "maps to no rule antecedent," repeated a dozen times, rigour shading toward liturgy. The hinge is turn 12, when the concert bills arrive and the learner rules Vess "not merely ungrounded — it is now foreclosed"; the porter puts Liane in play at turn 14. Turn 23 is the finest reasoning: a late witness hands Vess the Vara song, and the learner concedes the point in full — derives Vess's source itself — while holding that absence bars him from the winter room.

The verdict, grounded anagnorisis — recognition in Aristotle's sense, the secret asserted just as the learner's evidence compels it — shows forcing and assertion meeting at turn 35, gap zero. Derivation distance, the count of evidence pieces still missing for the proof, fell from seven to zero but back-loaded: an eighth of a point per turn for two movements, half a point at the end. The decay ledger says why. Twelve slips drew eleven repairs, all the tutor's, none the learner's. One outage — the watermark, gone from turn 23 until 35 — voided the assertions at turns 32 through 34, each flagged a lucky leap: right, but unforced. All eleven releases arrived on cue. The tutor leaned on erotema, the teaching question, for half its turns, four figures notwithstanding — a rut the superego, its offstage editor, broke four times, always by striking a question. The longest flat stretch ran four turns, short of aporia, the stall.

The movements shaped the action rather than labelling it. The first two stage the rise and fall of the false trail, and "The Hand" is the tautest stretch; "The Dating" sprawls — sixteen turns for two points of progress, much of it the tutor re-lighting a watermark that slipped six times. One slip, Vess's absence, was never mended, leaving the rival unexcluded at curtain. The recognition itself divides. On stage it lands at turns 32 and 33 — the conservator's matched pages, the tutor's aposiopesis, the sentence broken off for the learner to finish, the learner's "The law does not equivocate." In the bookkeeping it lands only at 35, on what plays as a coda: "set the leaf to the lamp once more."

What this iteration establishes: the decay machinery produces real teaching pressure, and a tutor can carry a forgetful learner to an earned ending past a twice-raised mirror. The consequential defect is that recognition was staged over a decayed premise: the hand evidence entered at 32 with the watermark off the board, so the checker scored the climax a leap and the ending had to be rescued by a recap. The one change: a charter clause forbidding recognition staging until every premise of the chain is audited and restored — the lamp business of turn 35 belongs before the conservator enters. One more note: though told of its condition, the learner made none of the eleven repairs; a board it must recite aloud might become a board it can keep.
