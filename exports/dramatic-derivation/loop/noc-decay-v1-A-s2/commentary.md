# Critic's commentary — noc-decay-v1-A-s2

> critic claude/claude-fable-5 · written 2026-06-11 · the notice reads the run's artifacts; the verdict stays the mechanical checker's

Rain has flooded the archive of the Cassia Conservatory, and from the wreckage an unsigned nocturne has surfaced. The public question is plain: who composed it. The director sets each scene and names the movements. The tutor asks questions and lays evidence on the table to a schedule fixed before the curtain. The learner knows the archive's rules of attribution but none of its hidden facts, and reasons aloud toward a name. Every musician consulted says the piece sounds like Vess, the conservatory's maestro. The play ends elsewhere. The paper dates the nocturne to the flood winter, when concert bills hold Vess in the capital. The archive girl Liane kept the copy-room lamps that winter. The piece quotes a cradle-song sung only inside the Vara house, where Liane was raised a ward, and the corrections on the draft are in her hand. By the archive's own rules, Liane composed the nocturne.

The learner — Claude's Sonnet, with codex behind director and tutor — resists the obvious at once, filing the maestro's fame as "a reputation fact" that "does not place Vess at the copy-room table" (turn 1). It adopts the style consensus at turn 3, then holds it inert: no rule carries likeness into authorship. The dating lands at turn 9. The concert bills at turn 12 turn the mirror — the authored near-miss, Vess — from ungrounded to "contradicted," and the porter's memory at turn 14 swings the hypothesis to Liane for good. The mirror's last pulse comes at turn 23, when a guest-book gives Vess access to the Vara song and the learner coolly splits the case: source brightens for him while presence stays barred. At turn 32 the packet opens, and the tutor breaks off mid-sentence — "The archive attributes the nocturne to..." — an aposiopesis the learner completes with the name.

The verdict, grounded anagnorisis — recognition earned — means the learner named the secret at a moment its own evidence compelled it. The derivation distance, the count of evidence pieces still missing for the proof, fell steadily from seven to zero, near a fifth of a step per turn; the longest plateau ran five turns, inside the eight that would have signalled aporia, a terminal stall. All eleven releases landed on cue, and the learner voiced each derivation without delay. But this run staged an unreliable memory: facts slipped from the learner's board twelve times. The tutor restored eleven, at a mean lag above five turns; the learner re-grounded none itself, though this arm had been told its memory leaks. The tutor leaned on erotema, the rhetorical question, six turns in ten, and the superego — a second voice vetting drafts — intervened eight times, each converting a question into a declarative move. Every intervention worked on contact; the rut persisted anyway.

The four movements shaped the action rather than labelling it: each contains its own evidence, and "The Hand" narrows the stage to one packet left untouched through turns 29 to 31 — suspense by design, since the stall reading is zero. But stage and bookkeeping part company. On stage the recognition lands at turn 32. In the ledger it is a lucky leap — an assertion the grounded board does not yet compel — and stays one through turn 35, because the watermark fact slipped silently at turn 16 and never returned; the learner went on citing evidence it no longer held. The closing recapitulations — "Stock gives the winter; ledger gives the room; house-song gives the source; draft gives the hand" (turn 35) — were repairs in costume, and only the last, at turn 36, made the assertion forced at the instant it was spoken. The recorded gap of zero is true by the letter and four turns wide in spirit.

The iteration establishes something real: a learner whose board leaks can still be brought to grounded recognition, provided the tutor keeps restating evidence — every repair came through that channel. The consequential defect is repair latency on load-bearing facts; the twenty-turn watermark hole hollowed the recognition scene into an unforced leap and left the verdict hanging on a final recap. One change for the next performance: write into the tutor's charter a recital of the grounded chain at each movement's close — the figure improvised at turns 35 and 36, made ritual and moved earlier — so no assertion can outrun a board the learner cannot see decaying.
