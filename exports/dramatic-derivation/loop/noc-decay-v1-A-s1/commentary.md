# Critic's commentary — noc-decay-v1-A-s1

> critic claude/claude-fable-5 · written 2026-06-11 · the notice reads the run's artifacts; the verdict stays the mechanical checker's

When the curtain rises the Cassia Conservatory stands flooded, and out of its dripping archive comes a nocturne with no signature. The public question is the plain one: who composed it? The hall's ready answer is Vess, the celebrated master whose late manner the piece wears. Three voices share the stage. The director sets scenes and declares the movements. The tutor carries evidence to the table on a schedule fixed in advance and teaches its use. The learner knows the world's general laws of attribution — paper dates a work, presence permits authorship, a private tune betrays its source, only a composer corrects a draft — but none of its particulars, and must reason aloud. The evidence arrives one piece at a time. A heron watermark and a steward's stock-book fix the nocturne to the flood winter. Concert bills hold Vess in the capital for that whole winter. A porter remembers Liane, the archive girl, tending the copy-room lamps through it. A cradle-song hidden in the middle voice is traced to the Vara house, where Liane was raised as ward, and the draft's corrections prove to be in her hand. The learner concludes that Liane composed the nocturne, and the mechanical checker certifies the recognition as earned.

The learner's discipline is the production's early pleasure. At turn 1 it severs room from rumor — "presiding is not composing" — and at turn 3 it adopts the musicians' style verdict while observing that style appears in none of the six rules. Turn 9 is the first hinge, when the stock-book turns paper into time. Turn 12 is the second, when the alibi lands and the learner declares the Vess hypothesis "no longer merely ungrounded — it is actively blocked." The authored near-miss, the mirror, rises twice: on style at turn 3, and again at turn 23, when a patron's note gives Vess access to the Vara song; the learner grants him the source conjunct and shows in the same breath that source cannot buy presence. The ending comes at turn 32, when the hand evidence is released and the tutor breaks off — "And therefore..." — and the learner completes the sentence: "The rules close without remainder. The nocturne is Liane's."

The instruments complicate that ending. Derivation distance — the count of evidence pieces still missing for the proof — fell from seven to zero, but slowly at first, one piece per eight-turn movement through the opening half, then three in "The Cradle-Song" and two in "The Hand." The longest plateau, five turns, stayed inside the eight-turn aporia window, the stall that fails a run. All eleven releases landed on cue, and the learner voiced every available inference the turn it became derivable. But this run deliberately afflicts its Sonnet learner with a leaky memory: twelve facts slipped from its board, the codex tutor restored eleven, and the learner re-adopted none on its own. Mean repair latency was five turns; the watermark, the chain's first stone, lay dark from turn 19 to turn 36. That is why the checker flags turns 32 through 35 as lucky leaps — assertions made before the learner's grounded facts forced them — and why forcing and grounded assertion coincide only at turn 36, four turns before the cap. The tutor leaned on erotema, the guiding question, for 56 percent of turns; the superego's eight interventions changed the figure every time, a real but local correction.

The four movements shaped the action rather than labeling it, each opening a new evidentiary regime. Tension gathered at the alibi and at the guest-note, then sagged across turns 33 to 35, where the learner repeats one verdict four times into a static room while the tutor quietly re-stages the rotted foundation. The recognition landed on stage at turn 32, in dialogue; the certified anagnorisis arrived at turn 36. The panel's "gap 0" flatters that seam.

What this iteration establishes is that decay at this severity is survivable on external maintenance alone, and that a two-conjunct mirror never tempted a rule-bound learner — even in the told arm, though, the learner showed no self-repair at all. The consequential defect is repair priority blind to load-bearingness: the decorative style verdict was re-staged five times while the watermark waited seventeen turns. The one change: a charter clause requiring the tutor, before staging recognition, to walk the proof path and re-ground its facts oldest first. Turn 36's recital was exactly that sweep, played four turns late; at turn 31 it would have let the played ending and the certified one fall on the same beat.
