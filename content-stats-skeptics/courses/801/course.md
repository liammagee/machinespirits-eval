## Description

The three lectures move from the smallest unit of statistical claim — the summary statistic — up to the anatomy of a published study. Lecture one shows why the average, the most-cited number in popular discourse, is usually the least informative. Lecture two addresses the two great confusions of causal reasoning, one ancient and one recent. Lecture three offers a practical checklist for reading a study, tested on a worked example. Throughout, the emphasis is on developing habits of thought rather than memorizing formulas.
