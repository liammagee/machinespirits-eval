# Creative Writing: Short Fiction and Personal Essay

Welcome. If you have never taken a creative writing class before, good — you will not be at a disadvantage. Everyone in this room is working on the same thing: learning to read their own sentences more honestly.

## Course Overview

What will we learn?

- **Showing vs telling** — the difference between a scene and a summary, and when to use each
- **Voice and point of view** — who is telling this story, and how do we know
- **Revision as re-vision** — why most "editing" is actually avoidance
- **Giving and receiving feedback** — the workshop as a shared practice, not a verdict

## How We'll Learn

Creative writing is a craft, which means you learn it by writing, reading your writing back, rewriting, and eventually by letting someone else read it and say things that sting. Each lesson includes:

- A short craft reading with short-form examples
- A "notice this" exercise — read a passage slowly and point to the specific moves
- One or two drafting prompts (10-20 minutes of writing)
- A revision or feedback exercise

The most important habit to build: **read your own sentence as if you didn't write it.** That is 90% of being able to revise. Everything else in the course points at that habit from a different angle.

## A Note on Safety

Creative writing often surfaces personal material. Nothing in this course requires you to write about your own life, and nothing you write has to be shared. When you do share, the workshop discusses **the writing on the page**, not you. "I felt bored here" is a response to a sentence, not an attack on the author. You will learn both halves of that distinction — how to speak that way, and how to hear it.
