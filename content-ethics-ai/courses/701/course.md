## Description

The three lectures in this course correspond to three irreducible tensions in applied AI ethics. The first is that the formal definitions of fairness are mathematically incompatible with one another, so the choice among them is unavoidably political. The second is that the line between helpful nudge and manipulative design is easy to cross and difficult to locate. The third is that responsibility for algorithmic harms distributes across so many hands that it risks evaporating altogether. The aim is not to decide these questions in advance but to equip students to decide them well in context.
