# PLAN 3.0 Closing Screen: Does Recent Research Surface Adaptation Mechanisms the Synthetic Program Missed?

## 1. Short answer

No genuinely new dynamic-adaptation mechanism survived screening. Of 44 candidates swept from the 2024–2026 literature, **zero** screened as `genuinely_new`, **23** earned a single bounded probe (`variant_worth_one_probe`), **14** are variants of tested nulls, **6** are not applicable to a synthetic program (they require real human learners or non-text signal by construction), and **1** is already tested. The 23 probe-worthy survivors are not, on inspection, a list of missed mechanisms. They decompose into four characters:

- **Instrument-validity audits of the simulated learner** (fidelity metrics, sycophantic-capitulation bounds, IRT ability placement, epistemically constrained learner generators). These do not test the tutor; they quantify caveats the paper already states in prose (§7.10, §8.1, line ~160).
- **Boundary probes on the repo's established positives** — mostly transplants of the §6.13 criterial-conduct pattern to new surfaces (mastery gates, help ladders, answer-withholding, productive-failure phase gates), where the repo's own evidence (§6.13.11) predicts the outcome class: discipline on visible information, not new signal.
- **One residual mechanism family with genuine headroom now closed at bounded scale**: decision-time selection scored by an outcome or mechanical predicate rather than a critic's opinion. The Plan 3 outcome-selected best-of-K probe upgraded the cell-100-style selector to the validated §6.12.5 hard-transfer endpoint and returned a bounded null (single-K1, opinion-best-K, and outcome-selected all mean gain 0.250; outcome-minus-opinion 0.000; invalid plans 0). That closes the episode-level outcome-selector path for the synthetic program unless a reviewer specifically demands a full cell40/cell100/outcome-selected matrix.
- **Cross-session payload variants** riding the one live longer-horizon positive (ego pre-alignment, §6.6.11) — decayed-mastery injection, selection-pressured summaries, tutor-side "what worked" memory — all with strong null priors from the #3 rich-memory screen.

The governing conclusion: the field has not surfaced a within-session adaptation mechanism the synthetic program failed to cover. What it has surfaced is (a) independent corroboration of the repo's nulls, and (b) a measured critique of prompted simulated learners that the repo has partially pre-empted but never quantified.

## 2. SOTA positioning

**The field's biggest wins run through the repo's positives, not through within-session adaptivity.** LearnLM: Improving Gemini for Learning (arXiv 2024/25) — the single largest documented tutoring result (+31% expert preference over GPT-4o; 73.2% of non-tie match-ups in the 2025 learning arena) — credits pedagogy baked in as *orientation at training time* and its reports credit no within-session adaptivity module at all. Tutor CoPilot (arXiv/NBER 2024–25), the strongest human RCT (+4pp mastery, +9pp for the weakest tutors), is architecturally the repo's superego, and its gains concentrate in weak actors — the human-field twin of the substitution finding (§6.4). Kestin et al.'s Harvard RCT (Scientific Reports 2025) beat active learning with a *static* carefully designed tutor. VanLehn's synthesis (Educational Psychologist 2011) locates the ITS effect in the outer/step loop and finds the granularity hypothesis fails below step level — independent convergent evidence for the repo's within-session null.

**The one published head-to-head of a within-session adaptive mechanism against a strong static prompt, with real students, is a null.** Learning to Prompt (arXiv 2606.20138, 2026): a contextual-bandit prompt router beat static baselines in simulation (0.694 vs 0.647, p<0.001) then failed against the expert production prompt in a real A/B (19.1% vs 19.6%, N=656, n.s.). This replicates both the repo's §6.3/§6.9.8 nulls and its sim-to-real caution in a commercial deployment. Across the entire sweep, no published positive within-session adaptivity result survives a strong-prompt baseline on a credible metric; the survivors route through checkable verifier signal (Stepwise Verification and Remediation, EMNLP 2024) or human-in-the-loop expertise asymmetry (Tutor CoPilot) — the repo's positive #3 and a non-transferable asymmetry, respectively.

**The validity threats are real and matter most for the go/no-go.** Simulated Students in Tutoring Dialogues: Substance or Illusion? (ACL 2026) shows prompted simulators — the repo's class — score poorly on error realism, knowledge-acquisition consistency, and linguistic style. Simulating Students or Sycophantic Problem Solving? (arXiv 2026) finds Selective Flip Scores near zero across seven models: simulators abandon seeded misconceptions under *any* feedback. IRT calibration work (arXiv 2507.08232, 2025) finds no model-prompt pair achieves grade-calibrated ability. The repo has independently reached parts of these findings — §6.10's no-privileged-interior kill gate, §6.12.5's failed naive posttests (the in-repo SFS≈0 analogue), §6.13.7's harness-owned-state design rule ("asking a model to play forgetful measures its theatre of forgetfulness") — and §6.13.7's decay ledger proved the harness *does* reward mechanisms when signal is genuinely new (+0.49 told-arm repair), so the redundancy nulls are not artifacts of an unpayable environment. The Plan 3 ESS follow-up now puts one number on this caveat: loose roleplay over-credits tutoring by +0.020 relative to an epistemically constrained state endpoint (95% CI [0.015, 0.024]) and removes the small recognition outcome lift under the constrained endpoint (base+ESS 0.252, recognition+ESS 0.250). Fidelity, flip-selectivity, and ability placement remain broader pilot/go-memo bounds, but the "costume learner" objection now has an internal measured boundary rather than prose only.

## 4. Dismissed candidates, grouped by covering repo result

**Covered by the §6.9.8 fitted-policy / learned-adaptation kill gate** (offline OPE null, pre-registered, escalation foreclosed):
- Learned planner-executor over latent student state (Nam et al. arXiv 2025; PPDPP ICLR 2024) — the exact fitted state→action shape, run and closed; conversation-level reward additionally confounded by learners that recohere without the tutor.
- Trained pedagogical reward models, inference-time half (EduAlign arXiv 2025; BEA 2025 RMs) — reward-greedy selection is a demonstrated base-rate exploit; a 0.74-accuracy RM is a weaker selector than cell 100 already used.
- Dialogue-based knowledge tracing as state estimator (LLMKT LAK 2025) — the paper cites and adjudicates this exact lineage in §6.9.8's null.
- TRAVER turn-level trained verifier (ACL Findings 2025) — fitted verifier = §6.9.8; validator chain = cells 113/126–128, net-negative on the graded channel.
- Contextual-bandit prompt routing (arXiv 2606.20138) — fitted policy over in-context features; its own real A/B null corroborates §6.3; cite in §7.9, don't build.
- AgentPRM step-level verifiers (WWW 2026) — LLM-as-PRM is another rubric judge (stacking null); trained PRM needs human outcome labels — blocked, not exhausted, at the synthetic stage.

**Covered by §6.10 (no privileged interior) plus the ToM/profiling null family** (cells 54–68, §6.8.5–.6, ontology-ToM A/B, stall-watcher §6.13.6):
- UDP user world model with reaction anticipation (SIGIR 2025) — profile-then-plan; even the true latent adds nothing (R²_B negative); cite as the external instance in related work.
- Language Bottleneck Models (arXiv 2025) — held-out prediction pressure has no gradient on synthetic learners with no stable latent to trace.
- Sensor-free disengagement/wheel-spinning detectors (AIED 2020 line) — text-visible version is the trap suite + §6.15 stagnation instrument (nulls hold); action-trace version is circular synthetically.
- Adaptive help-seeking regulation (Roll/Aleven line) — trap suite covers the classification; hint gating is a §6.13 re-instantiation; the latent-disposition version is the §6.10 kill gate verbatim.

**Covered by the memory-pad / #3 cross-session rich-memory null** (§6.6.9 pad ablation N=252; rich−baseline −1.3):
- RMM/Mem0 reflective memory management (ACL 2025; ECAI 2025) — plumbing quality for a channel whose first powered screen was null; Mem0's own numbers show an accuracy deficit vs full context, and these transcripts fit in context.
- DeepTutor evidence-linked trace forest (arXiv 2026) — the audit half is A14 (cells 126–128, mechanism-clean but pedagogically negative); the richness half is the §6.8.6 reversal; the retrieval half is the §6.9.8 k-NN null.

**Covered by the orientation/substitution positives** (nothing left to test):
- Preference optimization for scaffolding (Sonkar EMNLP Findings 2024; SocraticLM) — trains in the behavior prompting already delivers on capable models; keep as a calibration citation.
- Tutor CoPilot + Kestin RCTs — already_tested in structure: superego-helps-weak-actors and static-orientation-wins, respectively; their residual value is the pilot measurement template and effect-size prior (~4–9pp), which should go onto the A1 workplan card.
- Wait-time / turn-yielding discipline (Rowe 1986) — the orientation is inside the recognition prompts and the rubric already rewards it; enforcement is the tested validator family; the causal half (learner responds to space) needs humans.
- Bridge typed-decision commitment guard (NAACL 2024) — the 14-action taxonomy, per-turn commitment, and consistency validator all exist (cells 110–113, 118–120, 126–128) and the self-referential guard shape is null-to-negative.

**Not applicable synthetically — human-phase channels by construction** (a simulated learner cannot produce the signal non-circularly; §6.10 is the ceiling for any synthetic imitation):
- Response-latency propensities (L@S 2026); gaze/mind-wandering (CHI 2021 line); facial/multimodal affect (2025–26 systems); keystroke/edit dynamics (IJAIED 2021 line); prosody in speech-LLMs (arXiv 2025). Disposition for all five: add cheap logging to the pilot instrumentation (pilotStore already timestamps turns; add compose-time, attempt/hint events, optional audio with consent) so the data exists for post-hoc analysis; build no adaptation on any of them now.

## 5. Recommendation

The synthetic program is effectively exhausted **on the mechanism axis**. Forty-four candidates from a deliberately adversarial sweep produced zero genuinely new adaptation mechanisms; the field's strongest results corroborate the repo's three positives and its central null, and the one deployed within-session adaptive A/B in the literature failed exactly the way the repo's cells did. What remains is not exploration but closure: quantifying the instrument's known weaknesses and discharging two or three bounded probes whose *null* outcomes are themselves the completeness evidence the pilot memo needs. This composes with, rather than reorders, the big plan: E2 stays first, A1 unblocking stays parallel, and nothing below competes with either for the P0 slots.

**Tier 1 $0 offline gates — now run where existing artifacts suffice.** The batched audit over existing logs and exports made no LLM calls and closed the hard offline gates: answer-delivery/public-hidden-label risk is 300/246,811 messages (0.1%; recognition family 39/50,686, 0.1%, below the 10% gate); help-ladder compliance after learner struggle or false mastery is 6,928/7,804 transitions (88.8%, above the 80% gate); mastery-gate phase 0 finds 38/265 assessable unsupported closure claims (14.3%, below the 15% gate); and first-error localization is available for 29/29 non-grounding detector failure arms (100%). Learner fidelity remains a proxy-only descriptive bound (8/58,686 false-mastery turns; 9,499 flip-language turns; 13,192 metacognitive turns). The fresh SFS matched-feedback corpus now closes the flip-selectivity caveat in the expected direction: targeted, mismatched, and generic feedback all produce correct flips at 1.000, yielding SFS=0.000 and false-flip=1.000. IRT is not a failed gate; it remains blocked until the A1 item bank is real rather than placeholder stubs. Artifacts: `exports/plan3-synthetic-closure-audits/report.md`; `exports/plan3-sfs-audit/sfs-matched-feedback.md`.

**Tier 2 paid probes — now run.** Outcome-selected best-of-K at episode level (#11) returned the intended closure null: upgrading from critic opinion to the validated hard-transfer endpoint did not beat either single-K1 or opinion-selected K. ESS learner 2×2 (#9) answered the strongest synthetic-learner objection in the bounded form available before human data: loose roleplay modestly inflates outcomes, while the constrained endpoint removes the apparent recognition hard-transfer lift. Everything else in the medium tier — PersonaMem boundary, TASA decay, procedural memory, PS-I gate, generative CAT — remains legitimate but optional; fold what fits into already-sanctioned work (history-grounded profiles into A7 Phase 2; CAT Phase 0 into the A1 item-bank build) and let the rest wait on pilot findings.

**Belongs in the human pilot, not the synthetic program.** All five multimodal channels (log-only instrumentation now, no mechanisms); the KT/LLMKT layer as a post-hoc analysis channel over pilot_turns; the real TASA/spaced-review test (real elapsed time, real forgetting); PS-I as the best-evidenced candidate *arm* for the pilot itself (g=0.36 on exactly the pretest/posttest instrument the pilot has); and the Tutor CoPilot measurement template and effect-size prior for the pilot power analysis. Add one line each to the A1 workplan card.

**Skip.** Everything in section 4; the MCTS probe (#13) and any Bridge-3b K-sweep unless a specific reviewer demand materializes — both are expensive dose-extensions of an already-characterized suggestive effect; and the entire training-time family (co-training, GRPO, DPO, RL simulators, trained PRMs) as a research program — it requires weight access this project does not have, and its published gain profile (lifting weak models to what strong models do by prompting) is the repo's substitution finding restated.

One sentence for the go/no-go: the sweep found nothing that says wait — it found a field converging on this repo's conclusions, hard offline gates that pass on existing artifacts, an SFS confirmation that the simulated learner's correction behavior is nonselective, a simulated-learner critique the pilot is the only real answer to, and two paid closing probes whose completed results now strengthen the same boundary rather than opening a new synthetic mechanism arc.
