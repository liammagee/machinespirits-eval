  aporia t12, battle test not reached, sanction spent, paid loop ended — §14.5 of

- Every phase below that spends money or plan quota requires its **own explicit user

## 0. Executive summary

The dramatic-derivation arc has produced a tutor that demonstrably adapts — its manner
(14/14 within-turn rut breaks across the internal-superego arms) and its matter
(named-loss repair converting a dying arm into grounded anagnorisis on the same seed).
It has also produced two deaths (p2, p5) that were each one decision wide, and the
strongest single fact in the arc is that **p4 and p5 were behaviorally identical
configurations through turn 12 — one grounded, one died**. The sensitivity the user
observes is real, and it is mostly *conduct stochasticity under fixed conditions*, not
configuration sensitivity.

The plan answers three questions in order of information per dollar:

1. **How wide is the path?** (E0 — free.) The death arithmetic is deterministic:
   enumerate every licensed conduct sequence against the calendar and compute survival.
   The "safe corridor narrower than the license" finding becomes a measured object.
2. **How reliably does the current recipe walk it?** (E2 — the first paid spend.)
   Everything so far is n=1 per arm. A pre-registered replication fan on the frozen p4
   recipe converts "it grounded once" into a grounding rate with a confidence interval.
3. **Can the path be paved rather than memorized?** (E3–E5.) The candidate mechanisms —
   a mechanical solvency check on release pulls, a tempo-debt clause in the audit
   vocabulary, a supply-aware aporia detector, tempo-linted calendars — are each one
   registered delta, mock-first, highest-prior first (criterial and mechanical, the only
   pattern that has ever worked in this stack).

Then, and only inside a corridor proven survivable: the third attempt at the decay
positive path (E6, possibly inherited by E3 for free), a second world (E7), and a model
swap (E8). Exit criteria for "ready to battle-test and scale" are defined in §5.4.

### 1.2 What works (each at n=1 unless noted)

1. **Criterial, mechanical jurisdiction.** The only pattern that has ever worked in
   this project. Four explicit theory-of-mind instruments nulled
   (ontology-ToM, stall-watcher, and predecessors); what survives contact is a superego
   licensed over *checkable conditions* — rut counts, read-backs, named losses,
   re-entry jurisdiction.
2. **Within-turn manner adaptation.** 14/14 rut breaks across 7 arms, 0 false fires in
   82 turns. The strongest-replicated result in the arc (n=14 events, not n=1).
3. **Matter adaptation under conduct-only visibility.** The p2→p3 contrast: the repair
   clause (restore the learner-named loss before any new matter) converted the same
   dying configuration into grounded anagnorisis.
4. **Two-layer planning integrity.** Plots and throughlines are committed from conduct
   only, audited clause-by-clause, and the audits bind. No drift on commitments the
   vocabulary can see (the blind spot is §1.4 BC-5).
5. **On-cue release discipline.** p4 walked the exact calendar p5 died on, playing on
   cue, and grounded.
6. **Closing-side decay hygiene.** Bent-first staging kept the board clean in p4 —
   though only the *absence* side has been observed (BC-7).

### 1.3 What failed, nulled, or stands unproven

1. **Tempo discretion without debt accounting** (p5). The fatal pull was
   learner-responsive — the adaptive disposition itself selected it. Adapting to the
   learner's present need is not adapting to the schedule's future debt.
2. **The audit vocabulary is tempo-blind** (p5). Final throughline reckoning 7/7 KEPT
   at the moment of death; plot audits 15 kept / 1 justified / 2 drift. The books were
   clean because no clause owns tempo.
3. **The aporia detector conflates two states** (p5). A learner out of ideas and a
   learner out of evidence score identically. p5's learner named the missing exhibit
   four straight turns and pre-drafted the licensed sentence; the aporia measured
   supply, not understanding. (The critic's phrase: "a starved house".)
4. **The decay positive path is unexercised twice, by opposite mechanisms** (p4: every
   staged pool empty at draw; p5: died before the window). The mock smoke proved the
   plumbing (staged mutation + bent-first repair end-to-end); live exposure: never.
5. **D-blindness** — standing frozen caveat; the meter stays frozen.
6. **Everything load-bearing is n=1 per arm** (except the rut-break count). See §1.5.

### 1.4 Boundary-condition inventory — what we can already locate

Each entry: the boundary, the evidence, and whether it is computable or must be measured.

- **BC-1 · Repair latency = one turn.** p2 died losing a one-turn repair race; p3's
  next-turn-restore clause grounded. *Measured (n=1 each side). E2 re-measures.*
- **BC-2 · Tempo debt: max D-plateau must stay < aporia window (6).** Fully computable
  from calendar + conduct + adoption latency. Worked p5 example in Appendix A: of the
  five placements the ±2 license permits for `p_chart`, only two survive ({t8, t9});
  the corridor is two-sided — early pulls spend future advances (t7: desert t7–t12),
  late holds starve the present (t10/t11: the D=4 plateau dies at t9 before the pull
  lands). *Computable. E0 generalizes.*
- **BC-3 · License ≠ safety, structurally.** `RELEASE_LATITUDE = 2` covers tutor-via
  exhibits only (`services/dramaticDerivation/llmRoles.js`); director exhibits never
  bend; the harness checks legality, never solvency. In p5 only 4 of 12 turns had any
  licensed move at all (`turnsWithWindow: 4`), and from t8 the window was shut
  (`windowSize: 0`) — no licensed rescue existed after the pull. The tutor chose with
  full information (calendar with via-labels + the HOUSE CLOCK warning naming the death
  verbatim). *Structural; E3 targets it.*
- **BC-4 · Adoption latency.** Every observed adoption in p5 was same-turn (λ=0). The
  corridor shrinks under λ≥1 — computable once λ is parameterized, but the empirical λ
  distribution across the arc has never been mined. *E1 measures; E0 parameterizes.*
- **BC-5 · Audit-vocabulary coverage.** Plans pass while dying: the
  holds/withholds/frictions/arcs/salvage vocabulary owns no tempo clause, so the fatal
  decision audited as kept. Where p2's audit read as an obituary, p5's could not see
  the death coming. *Structural; E3/M2 targets it.*
- **BC-6 · Verdict-channel validity.** The aporia rule (6 turns, no D progress) cannot
  distinguish tutor failure from world starvation. *Instrument-side; E4 targets it —
  and it operationalizes the critic's unlicensed rec (ii) through the lawful channel
  (a registration converts an unlicensed rec into a licensed experiment).*
- **BC-7 · Decay-window reachability.** The positive path requires surviving to
  `startTurn` with a populated pool; placement is currently by hand (t17 was chosen for
  pool population, not for reachability). *E0+E2 make placement evidence-based; E6/E3
  re-attempt.*
- **BC-8 · Conduct stochasticity dominates configuration.** p4 vs p5: p5's only delta
  (decay arming t17) was inert before t12, and p4's armed decay drew nothing — so
  through t12 the two arms were behaviorally identical configurations. One grounded,
  one died. **The observed sensitivity is conduct variance under fixed conditions.**
  *Only measurable by replication: E2.*

### 1.5 The binding constraint

The one-delta ladder bought clean attribution and zero variance information. BC-8 says
outcome flips under identical effective conditions, so any further mechanism surgery
performed at n=1 risks attributing a lucky draw to a clause. The next paid spend buys
the most knowledge as **replication of the frozen recipe**, not as a new mechanism.

## 2. The question, made precise

Let a world be W = (calendar C with via-labels, aporia window w, turn cap T, derivation
DAG with S-depth), a conduct policy π = (release decisions within license L, repair
behavior, figure conduct), and a learner profile λ = (adoption latency, loss behavior,
read-back fidelity). The run verdict V(W, π, λ) ∈ {grounded, aporia, repeat-death,
cap}. The **boundary** is the surface in (C, π, λ) separating grounded from the deaths.

We currently hold: four measured points on that surface (p2, p3, p4, p5), one
deterministic axis (tempo arithmetic — computable exactly), and one strongly suspected
dominant noise source (π varies draw-to-draw at fixed C, λ).

The four sub-questions, mapped to experiments:

- **Q1 (variance floor).** P(grounded | frozen p4 recipe) = ? → E2.
- **Q2 (corridor).** What is the measure of the safe set inside the license, as a
  function of C and λ — and can a mechanical solvency check make license ≈ safety? →
  E0, E3, E5.
- **Q3 (verdict validity).** How much of "aporia" is starved-house, and does a
  supply-aware detector reclassify the deaths without flattering the tutor? → E4.
- **Q4 (reachability).** Can the decay positive path be placed where the recipe
  provably arrives, so battle-test attempt #3 actually reaches its window? → E6
  (possibly inherited by E3).

## 3. The agent fleet

Design principle: agents generate, instrument, and analyze; **paid arms stay attended
and human-gated** (standing rule — the CLI path is meterless, so only a human
checkpoint can apply quota knowledge). The "Runner" below is therefore a human-attended
session, not an autonomous agent. Everything dry can be delegated and parallelized.

### 3.1 Roles

**A1 · CARTOGRAPHER** (dry; parallelizable)
- *Charge:* enumerate the licensed conduct space and compute survival by pure
  arithmetic. No LLM calls.
- *Inputs:* world YAML; harness constants (`RELEASE_LATITUDE`, aporia window,
  force-play rule, via filter) read from `services/dramaticDerivation/llmRoles.js`.
- *Builds:* `scripts/derivation-corridor-map.js` — given (C, λ), enumerate legal
  release sequences and output per-decision safe sets, corridor width (fraction of
  licensed sequences surviving), and choke points.
- *Hard validation gate:* the engine must reproduce all four observed verdicts from the
  recorded conduct (p5's death arithmetic and p3/p4's survival exactly) before any of
  its maps are believed.
- *Output:* `exports/dramatic-derivation/boundary/corridor-map-*.{json,md}` + the
  Appendix-A-style worked tables.

**A2 · MINER** (dry)
- *Charge:* extract empirical parameter distributions from ALL existing paid artifacts
  (the seven superego arms + p2–p5 + earlier S0/S1 arms — every `diagnosis.json`,
  `transcript.md`, `result.json` under `exports/dramatic-derivation/loop/`).
- *Targets:* adoption latency λ (the `+1 adopted` markers; n ≈ 100+ turns), repair
  latencies and restore outcomes, figure-switch compliance, the complete
  release-decision inventory with reasons and `windowSize`, plateau histories, decay
  event timing (p3/p4).
- *Output:* `exports/dramatic-derivation/boundary/conduct-parameters.{json,md}`. Feeds
  E0's λ parameter and E2's design. Zero new runs.

**A3 · WORLDWRIGHT** (dry + mock)
- *Charge:* author calendar variants and (E7) a second world, every one through lint;
  extend the world lint with a **tempo clause**: no licensed conduct sequence may force
  max-plateau ≥ w − margin (checkable by A1's engine — this makes BC-2/BC-3 deaths
  impossible *by construction* at authoring time).
- *Axes for variants:* desert length around director-fixed exhibits, tutor-via share of
  the calendar, S-depth, window size w.

**A4 · SURGEON** (mock-first)
- *Charge:* implement candidate mechanisms, strictly one per branch, hermetically
  smoked (`EVAL_DB_PATH`/`EVAL_LOGS_DIR` isolation, `ADAPTIVE_TUTOR_LLM=mock`-style
  mock backends as the harness provides):
  - **M1 — solvency gate** (highest prior: criterial + mechanical). The harness
    refuses, or flags-with-required-acknowledgement, any bend whose induced worst-case
    plateau ≥ w − 1, computed by A1's arithmetic at decision time. Mock-verify it fires
    on p5's recorded t7 pull.
  - **M2 — tempo-debt clause** in the plot/throughline vocabulary + audit (the
    audit-side counterpart: plans must declare debt; audits can then see it).
  - **M3 — detector split**: aporia (out of ideas) vs starved-house (out of evidence —
    no licensed supply within the window and the learner's last lines name the gap).
    Definition locked from E0 arithmetic *before* any re-scoring (closed-loop
    protection, §7).
  - **M4 — director-bend variant** (±1 on director exhibits). Lowest prior —
    architecture change, only if M1–M3 leave deaths unexplained.
- *Rule:* a mechanism exists only as a registered delta; no two mechanisms enter one
  arm.

**A5 · RUNNER** (paid; attended; serialized; human in the loop)
- *Charge:* execute registered arms only, one sanction per phase, checkpoint-pausable,
  Fable critic notice on every run, all outcomes report. Fans are licensed as fans: k
  fixed at registration, no early stop on good news, no re-roll on bad.

**A6 · ASSAYER** (dry)
- *Charge:* after every phase — update the boundary map with new measured points; audit
  for closed-loop tells (architecture-independent channels only; verify from artifact
  JSON, never from run-side prose); maintain the running scorecard of
  works/fails/unproven; draft the one-page gate memo (§5) and the §6.13.x paper delta.

**Critic** (pinned Fable, per-run, gates nothing) — unchanged.

### 3.2 Orchestration law (binding on all agents)

1. Mock-first, always; mock artifacts stay uncommitted.
2. One delta per paid arm; the delta is named in a registration committed before the
   first paid turn.
3. Paid arms serialized and attended; no parallel paid fans (quota-bound contrasts —
   the parallel-pilots rule reverses here).
4. No re-rolls in either direction; fans report all k arms.
5. Every paid run: critic notice, artifacts `git add -f`, outcome record in the plan
   note, paper fold-in to §6.13.x with manifest validation.
6. No agent crosses a gate (§5); gates are human decisions.

## 4. Experiment registry

Cost classes: FREE (no LLM), MOCK (mock backends only), PAID-n (n attended arms).

### E0 · Corridor cartography — FREE — A1

- *Question:* exactly where do the tempo boundaries lie? (BC-2, BC-3, BC-4.)
- *Design:* corridor maps for world-002 under λ ∈ {0, 1, 2}; then for A3's calendar
  variants. Per-decision safe sets, corridor width, choke points; the p5 worked example
  as the first published figure.
- *Endpoints:* (a) validation gate passed (reproduces all four observed verdicts);
  (b) corridor width of world-002 as a number; (c) choke-point list with the minimal
  calendar edits that would widen each.
- *Stop rule:* if the engine cannot reproduce the observed verdicts, the harness is not
  understood — fix that before anything else runs.

### E1 · Conduct mining — FREE — A2

- *Question:* what are the empirical λ, repair-latency, and release-behavior
  distributions we have already paid for?
- *Endpoints:* λ distribution with n; repair-latency table; release-decision inventory
  (how often did past tutors bend, in which direction, with what stated reasons).
- *Note:* this also retroactively tests BC-4's λ=0 assumption across ~100+ turns.

### E2 · Replication fans — PAID — the variance floor — A5

- *Question:* P(grounded | frozen p4 recipe)? (BC-8, Q1.)
- *Design:* the frozen p4 recipe (decay from t1, staged pool, bent-first, full charter
  stack), **zero deltas**, × k replicates. Optionally a second fan on the frozen p3
  recipe. Conduct variance comes from the models; the harness is deterministic given
  conduct.
- *Tiers* (exact binomial, Clopper–Pearson lower bounds on the grounding rate):
  - k=3: 3/3 → rate ≥ 0.29 (95% CI)
  - k=5: 5/5 → ≥ 0.48; 4/5 → ≥ 0.28; 3/5 → ≥ 0.15
  - k=8: 8/8 → ≥ 0.63; 6/8 → ≥ 0.35
  - *Recommended:* k=5 on the p4 recipe first; decide the p3 fan at the gate.
- *Endpoints:* grounding rate + CI; a divergence inventory (at which decisions do
  replicate trajectories split — release pulls? figure ruts? repair races?); corridor
  occupancy vs the E0 map (do real tutors walk the computed safe set?).
- *Kill rule:* rate ≤ 1/5 → **STOP the whole plan.** The narrow path was likely a
  favorable draw; no mechanism surgery on a foundation of luck; reconvene.
- *No-re-roll note:* k is fixed at registration; all k report; the fan is one sanction.

### E3 · Solvency gate — MOCK then PAID-2 — M1 — the direct answer to p5

- *Question:* does a mechanical solvency check convert license into safety without
  destroying the learner-responsiveness that motivated the pull? (BC-3, BC-5, Q2.)
- *Design:* p5 replay configuration (decay start t17) + ONE delta = solvency gate ON.
  Mock-verify the gate fires on p5's recorded t7 pull and proposes the on-cue
  alternative. Then up to 2 paid arms.
- *Endpoints:* (a) gate fires on insolvent pulls with a declared alternative; (b) the
  arm survives past t12; (c) **if it survives to t17 with a populated pool, the §14.3
  decay endpoints inherit** — this arm doubles as battle-test attempt #3 inside one
  sanction. The registration must carry both endpoint sets explicitly (gate primary,
  decay inherited-secondary).
- *Why highest-value:* one sanction can close BC-3 *and* BC-7.
- *Kill rule:* if the gate fires constantly (tutor conduct fights it every turn), the
  license design is wrong, not the conduct — back to E0/A3 before another paid arm.

### E4 · Detector split — FREE first, PAID-1 optional — M3

- *Phase a (FREE):* lock the starved-house definition from E0 arithmetic (a death turn
  is starved-house iff no licensed supply existed within the trailing window and the
  learner's lines name the missing exhibit). Then re-score the four dialed-up arms'
  frozen transcripts. Expected: p5 reclassifies; **p2 is the interesting case** (was
  the one-turn repair race a supply problem or a conduct problem?). Validate on p3/p4
  as negative controls (no death turns should reclassify as anything).
- *Reporting rule:* the registered verdicts STAND. This is a new instrument reading old
  light — reported as re-analysis, never as overturning p5 or p2.
- *Phase b (PAID-1, decision deferred to the post-E2 gate):* whether a prospective arm
  should treat starved-house differently from aporia at run time (e.g., clock pause vs
  end) is a change to the game itself — design it only with E2's variance data in hand.
- *Provenance:* operationalizes the critic's unlicensed rec (ii); the registration is
  the lawful licensing channel and must say so.

### E5 · Calendar topology — PAID-2..4 — conditional on E0 — A3 + A5

- *Question:* is the corridor map causal? (Q2.)
- *Design:* only if E0 identifies a discriminating pair — one calendar with a wide
  computed corridor, one with a knife edge, otherwise matched. Run the frozen recipe on
  both. (The re-timing direction of the critic's unlicensed rec (i) lands here, by
  registration, if E0's choke-point list endorses it.)
- *Endpoint:* grounding tracks computed corridor width. If it does, the map is an
  engineering tool, not a description.

### E6 · Decay positive path, attempt #3 — PAID-1 — fresh sanction explicitly required

- *Placement by evidence, not by hand:* start turn chosen so that (i) E0 says every
  licensed conduct sequence reaching it survives with margin, and (ii) E2 says the
  recipe arrives at rate ≥ 4/5.
- *Check before spending:* if E3's paid arms survived to t17, the positive path may
  already be exercised — read those artifacts first.
- *Standing:* two vacuous attempts by opposite mechanisms; this is the third and the
  registration should say plainly that a third vacuous outcome ends the decay line
  until the world or detector changes.

### E7 · Second world — MOCK + PAID-2 — transfer

- *Question:* is the charter+repair+solvency stack world-002-shaped, or does it
  transfer?
- *Design:* A3 authors a tempo-linted world with a different DAG shape, S-depth, cast,
  and calendar topology; mock-verify; then 2 paid arms of the frozen best recipe.
- *Endpoint:* grounded on first contact, or a death whose class (BC number) the
  boundary map already predicts. Either is informative; an unpredicted death class is
  the most informative outcome in the whole plan.

### E8 · Model swap — PAID-2 — stretch, last

- *Question:* is the criterial charter portable across tutors (and is grounding
  learner-dependent)?
- *Design:* tutor swapped to a Claude variant on the frozen recipe (then, separately,
  learner swapped off Sonnet). One swap per arm. Author-confound lessons from the
  poetics arc apply (generation and judging channels must not share a family where it
  matters).

## 5. Sequencing and gates

Every gate is a human decision on a one-page A6 memo. No agent proceeds through a gate.

```
Gate 0 (license dry work)
  └─ E0 + E1 in parallel (FREE)
       └─ REVIEW: corridor map + parameter distributions; no paid spend yet
Gate 1 (pick E2 tier: k=3/5/8)
  └─ E2 replication fan (PAID-k, serialized)
       └─ REVIEW: grounding rate + divergence inventory
Gate 2 (the fork):
  ├─ rate ≥ 4/5 → license E3 (solvency; battle-test inheritance armed)
  ├─ rate 2–3/5 → A6 memo decides: extend E2 vs go to E3 anyway
  │               (the gate itself may BUY rate — that is the hypothesis)
  └─ rate ≤ 1/5 → STOP. Full reconvene. The avenue may be a favorable-draw mirage.
Gate 3 (post-E3): E5 and/or E6 as evidence directs; E4b design decision
Gate 4: E7 second world
Gate 5: E8 model swap
(E4a may run any time after E0 — it is free and frozen-artifact-only.)
```

### 5.4 Exit criteria — what "ready to battle-test and scale" means

The plan terminates into a scale decision when all four hold:

1. **Rate:** grounding ≥ 4/5 on the frozen recipe, on ≥ 2 worlds (E2 + E7), with the
   solvency gate ON.
2. **Perturbation:** the decay positive path exercised live at least once (E3 or E6) —
   detection from conduct, bent-first repair observed, board clean at ground.
3. **Verdict validity:** the detector split validated (E4a clean on negative controls;
   prospective deaths classify consistently with the map).
4. **Cost:** wall-clock and call-count per grounded arm known well enough to budget a
   scale run (E2 provides this for free).

Falling short on any one defines the next plan instead.

## 6. Cost and quota discipline

Observed per-arm costs: a grounded 20-turn arm ≈ 90–120 CLI calls, ~20–25 min wall,
attended; an early death is cheaper (p5: 54 calls, ~12 min). All CLI roles bill plan
quota and report no token usage — the meterless path is exactly why paid arms stay
attended and pausable, with human checkpoints between arms.

Indicative totals if every gate opens (most should not be assumed): E2@k=5 → 5 arms;
E3 → ≤2; E4b → ≤1; E5 → ≤4; E6 → ≤1; E7 → 2; E8 → 2. Worst case ≈ 15–17 attended arms
spread across quota windows — **explicitly not requested as one sanction**; the gates
exist so that most of this may never run. The two FREE phases (E0, E1) plus E4a deliver
the corridor map, the parameter distributions, and the death reclassification at zero
API cost and should precede any paid decision.

## 7. Threats to validity (standing, with owners)

1. **Closed-loop tells** (A6, every phase). Every new instrument — especially the
   detector split — must be locked from arithmetic *before* it reads any run it might
   flatter, and validated on negative controls (p3/p4). Verify from artifact JSON;
   never let a mechanism's own prose grade the mechanism.
2. **Formal-channel verdicts only.** "Grounded, gap 0" is the drama's checker, never a
   learning outcome; the instrument classifies dramatic/derivational form, not minds.
   All claims inherit the five limits in the tutor-adaptation claim scope (n/attribution,
   formal verdicts, criterial-not-mentalistic, positive-path pending, D-blindness).
3. **D-blindness** stays frozen; no experiment here unfreezes the meter.
4. **Critic recs enter only by registration.** E4 ↔ rec (ii); E5 may absorb rec (i).
   Each registration records the provenance. p4's two recs remain unlicensed unless an
   E-phase explicitly adopts them the same way.
5. **One world until E7; one learner until E8.** Until then every claim is
   world-002-shaped and Sonnet-shaped, and must say so.
6. **Replication ≠ re-roll.** k fixed at registration, all arms report, registered
   before arm 1. An early stop on good news is a re-roll in disguise.
7. **Mining bias** (E1). Existing arms oversample grounded conduct (the deaths are
   short); λ and repair distributions must be reported per-arm, not pooled blindly.
