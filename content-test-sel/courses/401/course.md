# College Success and Social-Emotional Skills

Welcome. This course is a little different from your other courses. There is no content to memorize. There are no problems to solve that have a single answer. The course exists because research shows — consistently, across decades — that adult learners who build a handful of specific non-academic skills are more likely to finish what they started, regardless of their preparation at intake.

## Course Overview

We cover four skills:

- **Self-awareness** — noticing what you're feeling, and how it's shaping what you do
- **Self-management** — regulating stress, procrastination, and impulse in a way that lets you keep showing up
- **Social awareness** — noticing what someone else is feeling, even when you don't agree with them
- **Relationship skills** — asking for help, handling conflict, repairing after something goes wrong

We borrow from a framework called CASEL-5, which names a fifth skill (responsible decision-making). We cover that inside each of the four above rather than in a separate unit. Decision-making isn't a standalone skill; it's what emerges when the other four are working.

## How We'll Learn

This is not a lecture-and-quiz class. Each week includes:

- A short reading that names the skill and gives vocabulary
- An exercise you do on yourself — a self-scan, a feelings-inventory, a conversation practice
- A reflection, written or spoken into a note, about what you noticed
- Sometimes a paired activity where you try something on a low-stakes conversation partner

There are no exams. Assessment is self-report plus instructor observation over the course of the semester.

## A Note on Safety

You will not be asked to share anything you don't want to share. The exercises involve noticing your own state — and sometimes that will surface hard things. If something comes up that you want to talk about with a professional rather than in class, the student counseling center is a free resource for anyone enrolled. Nothing in this course requires you to work alone on material that would be better worked on with a therapist.
