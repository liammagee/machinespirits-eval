STAGE: [A blank cart diagram, four arrow labels, and a clear overlay printed "force on cart from ___" sit in a neat row under a desk lamp.]

TUTOR: [sets the four label cards beside the cart diagram and taps the cart body once]

“This cart is the object. No new arrows yet. Place each card beside the thing acting on the cart: hand, surface, Earth, table. The arrow direction can wait; first the label must name the interaction on the cart.”

LEARNER: [slides the hand card beside the drawn hand, then pauses over the surface card]

"So the card names the thing acting on the cart first, not the motion. Hand on cart, Earth on cart, surface on cart. The table card is the unclear one if the surface is already here."

TUTOR: [sets the table card partly under the surface card at the contact line, pauses, then moves it outside the diagram]

“Both cards reach the same contact. If this surface is the table, leave one contact label on the cart. Choose the card that stays, then read the cart’s labels again.”

STAGE: [The overlay is slid down so every arrow must pass through the printed phrase "force on cart from ___." The earlier arrow directions remain visible underneath.]
An earlier learner line returns to the table: "[slides the hand card beside the drawn hand, then pauses over the surface card] So the card names the thing acting on the cart first, not the motion." The pause holds while the learner decides whether that wording still stands, needs narrowing, or needs replacing.]

LEARNER: [keeps the surface card at the contact line and leaves the table card outside the diagram]

"“The card names the thing acting on the cart first, not the motion.” That still stands, but table and surface cannot both be separate labels for this same contact. So the labels are: force on cart from hand, force on cart from Earth, force on cart from surface."

TUTOR: [slides the table card to the clipboard edge and leaves the surface card alone at the contact line]

“Use that as the test now: each card must name one interaction on the cart, and a repeated contact leaves the diagram. Read the three labels again; no arrowheads yet.”

LEARNER: [touches the three remaining cards, leaving the table card at the clipboard edge]

"So the wording is still: one card names one interaction on the cart. The old frame made table and surface look like two labels, but here they seem to point to the same contact, so I should not count both. The three labels are force on cart from hand, force on cart from Earth, and force on cart from surface; arrowheads wait until after that."
