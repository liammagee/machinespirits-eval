STAGE: [The cart card is squared to the table edge, and the labels push, friction, gravity, and normal sit in a neat column beside the blank arrows.]

TUTOR: [sets the four cards in a column beside the sleeve, then taps the empty arrow nearest the cart]

"One card first. For push: what object acts on the cart?

Place that card by the acting object, not by the cart’s travel. If no object is acting, the card stays off the arrow.

Motion is not one of these cards."

LEARNER: [slides the push card toward the hand side of the diagram]

"So this is hand on cart, not just forward.
If the hand is gone, the push card stays off.
The arrow is for the interaction on the cart, not the cart rolling."

STAGE: [A blank card is placed above the labels: FORCE ON THE CART BY _____. The earlier label placement remains visible under the sleeve.]

TUTOR: [sets the blank card above the push card: FORCE ON THE CART BY _____.]

"That names it: force on the cart by the hand.

If the hand is gone, no hand-on-cart card.

Pick one other force card. Put it under the blank. Read the sentence before the arrow moves."

LEARNER: [places the Earth card under the blank]

"Force on the cart by Earth.
That puts the arrow down, not along the track.
The acting object gets named first."

TUTOR: [slides the Earth card beside the downward arrow]

"Earth on cart. Down.

That arrow is not the cart’s trip along the track. It is the interaction named by the card.

Next card: floor. Put it under the blank. Read the sentence before the arrow moves."

LEARNER: [sets the floor card under the blank]

"Force on the cart by floor.
Up from the track contact, not along the track.
Is that the support force?"
