STAGE: [A cart diagram is a laminated worksheet showing a small wheeled cart with force arrows drawn around it. The movable cards are labels for those force arrows: push, friction, gravity, and normal. In this activity a force arrow names an interaction on the cart, such as "Earth on cart" or "hand on cart"; it is not a motion arrow showing which way the cart travels.]

STAGE: [A cart diagram lies on the bench beside four arrow cards labelled push, friction, gravity, normal, with one arrow already placed in the direction the cart is supposed to roll.]

TUTOR: [slides the gravity card beside the downward arrow, then holds the normal card above the upward arrow]

"This one names Earth pulling on the cart. Not travel.

Up arrow now. Which object pushes on the cart there?"

LEARNER: "Down arrow — Earth pulling. Up arrow — the surface under it pushes. [glances at the diagram] Bench? The track? [taps the normal card against the up arrow] This one, then. Push on the cart."

STAGE: [The diagram is rotated ninety degrees on the bench so the cart now faces away from the learner; the previously placed arrow remains where it was, now pointing across the page rather than along it.]

TUTOR: [sets the track card under the cart drawing, touching the up-arrow tail; moves the bench card aside]

"Only the thing touching here pushes.

Bench or track? Name the up arrow. Then both arrows again.

Page turned; interaction stayed."

LEARNER: "Track. [slides the track card flush under the up-arrow tail, sets the bench card off the diagram] Touching — so it pushes. Down arrow — Earth pulls the cart. Up arrow — track pushes the cart."

TUTOR: [clips the Earth card to the down arrow and the track card to the up arrow]

"This placement gives the labels.

Down: Earth pulls on cart. Up: track pushes on cart.

Not travel. Write both labels. Then next sheet."

LEARNER: [uncaps pen, writes beside the down arrow]

"Earth pulls cart. [moves to the up arrow] Track pushes cart. [caps the pen, slides the finished sheet aside, reaches for the next]"
