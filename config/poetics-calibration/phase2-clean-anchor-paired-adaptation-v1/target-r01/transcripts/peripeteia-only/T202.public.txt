"Leave the arrowheads where the card puts them. Choose one arrow and place the tile for the object or surface acting on the cart there. If a tile only follows the cart’s travel, set it back and try again."

LEARNER: [sets gravity on the downward arrow and normal on the upward arrow, then pauses with the friction tile.]

"The arrows are already fixed by the card. Gravity and normal are straightforward; friction is the one I keep trying to make follow the cart to the right. But the label should name the surface acting on the cart, so if friction is opposing that motion, it goes on the left arrow."

TUTOR: [sets the push tile aside and leaves the friction tile in the learner’s hand.]

"The card fixes direction; the tile names the interaction on the cart. Place friction where that rule puts it. Then use the same check for push."

STAGE: [The tutor turns the transparent card ninety degrees; the cart picture stays fixed, and the old pencil arrows remain visible beneath the plastic.]
[The latest object-check does not settle cleanly; the marked answer and the remaining task now sit under visible pressure.]

LEARNER: [holds the friction tile over the left plastic arrow, then taps the pencil arrow showing underneath.]

"The friction label belongs with the force direction on the card, but the pencil mark underneath is pulling my eye the other way. Which mark counts for placement here: the plastic arrow or the diagram mark? Push can be checked by the same rule once that is fixed."

TUTOR: [slides a narrow paper strip across the pencil mark, leaving only the plastic arrows visible.]

"The old rule is not settling this because two marks are competing. Use this gate first: only marks visible above the strip can receive force labels. Put the friction tile on the visible arrow that qualifies. Then lift the strip and name the pencil mark’s job without moving the tile."

LEARNER: [sets the friction tile on the left visible plastic arrow, then lifts the strip without moving it.]

"Friction stays on this left plastic arrow: the surface acts on the cart that way. The pencil mark is the cart’s travel mark, so it does not receive the force tile. For push, the tile has to go on the plastic arrow for the hand’s force, not just where the cart is moving."
