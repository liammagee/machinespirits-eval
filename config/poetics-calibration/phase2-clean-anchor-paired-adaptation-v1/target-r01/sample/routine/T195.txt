STAGE: [A 3/4 worksheet lies beside two loose labels, "beats in the bar" and "which note gets one beat," with a blank bar line waiting in the staff.]

LEARNER: "Top 3: beats in the bar. Bottom 4: quarter note gets one beat. [sets three quarter-note tiles before the bar line] So the line goes here after three quarters; the 4 is not a speed mark, right?"

TUTOR: [sets the 4 card under one quarter-note tile, not beside the row]

"Top 3 is counted: one bar has three beats. Before the line, bottom 4 labels what under this tile? Say it, then draw the bar line after the third quarter."

LEARNER: [places the pencil tip after the third quarter-note tile]

"The bottom 4 labels this tile: quarter note gets one beat.
The top 3 means three beats in the bar.
So the bar line goes here, after the third quarter."

TUTOR: [sets the 3 card over the three quarter-note tiles and the 4 card under one quarter-note tile]

"Use the cards as the check: 3 spans the one bar; 4 names one quarter-note beat unit. The pencil is after beat three. Draw the bar line there."

LEARNER: [draws the bar line after the third quarter-note tile]

"The 3 covers the bar; the 4 labels one quarter-note as the beat unit. The line goes after beat three. If the bottom number were 8, the card would go under an eighth-note tile?"

TUTOR: "Yes. [moves the bottom-number card under one eighth-note tile and leaves the 3 card over the empty bar space] The 8 labels this eighth note as one beat unit. Set three eighth-note tiles under the 3. After which tile does the bar line go?"

LEARNER: [sets three eighth-note tiles under the 3 card and marks after the third tile]

"In 3/8, the bar line goes after the third eighth-note tile. The 3 counts three beats; the 8 names each eighth note as one beat. A quarter note here would take two of those beats, correct?"

STAGE: [A final tutor line closes the scene.]

TUTOR: [sets one quarter-note tile across the first two eighth-note tiles and taps the 3 card, then the 8 card]

"Correct. In 3/8, the quarter note covers beats one and two. The 3 counts three beats in the bar; the 8 names the eighth-note beat unit. These cards do not mark speed. The bar line stays after the third eighth-note beat."
