STAGE: [The carbon tile lies flat while four label cards wait beside it, unfilled and evenly spaced.]

LEARNER: [slides the overlay over the carbon tile]

"Top card on 6: atomic number; C gets symbol; carbon gets name; bottom 12.01 gets atomic mass. I can place them, but why does the tile keep the two numbers separate instead of just putting one number with C?"

TUTOR: [slides the 12.01 card up beside C, leaving the bottom slot empty]

"Now one slot has no answer. The symbol space holds C, but the overlay still asks two questions: which number names the element, and which number gives the mass entry? Put one number back, then name the job of 6."

LEARNER: [slides 12.01 back into the bottom slot]

"The decimal belongs here at the bottom. The 6 stays at the top; its job is atomic number, the number that identifies carbon."

TUTOR: [presses the overlay flat and taps the top slot, then the bottom slot]

"Keep that split. The 6 identifies the element; the word card names it. The 12.01 gives the mass entry. Read the four slots top to bottom, with the two number jobs separate."

LEARNER: [keeps a fingertip on the top slot, then the bottom slot]

"Top 6: atomic number, identifies carbon. C is the symbol, carbon is the name, and 12.01 is the atomic mass entry. The two number jobs stay separate."

TUTOR: [slides the four label cards into a row beside the tile and lifts the overlay back a hand's width]

"That split is stable. Place the cards again without the overlay labels: top 6 identifies carbon; C is the symbol; carbon is the name; bottom 12.01 gives the mass entry."

LEARNER: [sets the overlay aside and places the cards on the tile]

"Top 6: atomic number, identifies carbon. C for the symbol; carbon for the name. Bottom 12.01: atomic mass entry."

STAGE: [A final tutor line closes the scene.]

TUTOR: [slides the overlay clear and leaves the four cards fixed on the tile]

"The split is fixed: top number identifies carbon, bottom decimal gives the mass entry. Take that read to the station."
