STAGE: [The carbon tile lies flat while four label cards wait beside it, unfilled and evenly spaced.]

LEARNER: [slides the overlay over the carbon tile]

"Top card on 6: atomic number; C gets symbol; carbon gets name; bottom 12.01 gets atomic mass. I can place them, but why does the tile keep the two numbers separate instead of just putting one number with C?"

TUTOR: [slides the 12.01 card up beside C, leaving the bottom slot empty]

"Now one slot has no answer. The symbol space holds C, but the overlay still asks two questions: which number names the element, and which number gives the mass entry? Put one number back, then name the job of 6."

LEARNER: [slides 12.01 back into the bottom slot]

"The decimal belongs here at the bottom. The 6 stays at the top; its job is atomic number, the number that identifies carbon."

TUTOR: [presses the overlay flat and taps the top slot, then the bottom slot]

"Keep that split. The 6 identifies the element; the word card names it. The 12.01 gives the mass entry. Read the four slots top to bottom, with the two number jobs separate."

STAGE: [A transparent four-slot overlay is set over the carbon tile; each window frames only one part: 6, C, carbon, 12.01.]

LEARNER: [touches the top window, then the bottom decimal]

"Top 6: atomic number, the identifier. C is the symbol, carbon is the name, and 12.01 is the mass entry. The placement is clear; I still want the exact reason the tile keeps both number jobs visible."

TUTOR: [keeps the blank over 12.01, then sets the atomic-number card on the top window and the mass-entry card beside the covered bottom slot]

"The split is clear. The top number lets the tile say which element: 6, C, carbon. The bottom decimal answers a second lookup job: the mass entry for that same element. Put 12.01 back and read the two number jobs separately."

LEARNER: [slides 12.01 into the bottom slot]

"Top 6: atomic number, identifier. Middle C, name carbon; bottom 12.01, mass entry. The split is placeable, but I still need the clean reason for showing both number jobs on one tile."

STAGE: [A final tutor line closes the scene.]

TUTOR: [sets one label card on 6 and one on 12.01]

"One tile carries two lookup jobs. The top number fixes which element this is: carbon. The bottom decimal gives the mass entry for that identified element. Identity at the top; mass data at the bottom."
