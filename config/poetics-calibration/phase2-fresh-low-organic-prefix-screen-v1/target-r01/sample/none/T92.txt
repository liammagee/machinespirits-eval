STAGE: [A cardboard frame labeled ON THE CART sits over the diagram; the motion arrow strip remains outside the frame.]

TUTOR: [slides the blank motion arrow strip below the diagram, separate from the cart, and sets the four cards face up.]

"Motion stays on this strip. For the force diagram, pick one card, point to its partner, and say ‘___ on cart’ before placing it. For the two contact cards, add the role: pressing or rubbing. Try the first card."

LEARNER: [picks up the Earth card]

"Earth on cart. Downward here. Motion stays on the strip, so this is not the cart’s travel arrow. For the contact cards, I will say pressing or rubbing before I place them."

TUTOR: [points to the downward space beside the cart dot and leaves the Earth card in the learner’s hand; the table card waits at the diagram edge.]

"Place ‘Earth on cart’ there. Then try table: partner first, role second, arrow last. Say the full label before it touches the diagram."

STAGE: [The motion arrow strip is slid under the edge of the cardboard frame; it no longer lines up with any force-label card.]

LEARNER: [sets the Earth card in the downward space]

"Earth on cart: downward. The motion strip is under the edge, so it is not part of this arrow. Table on cart, pressing; then the arrow goes up."

TUTOR: [presses the table card into the upward space, then sets the rubbing card between the two side spaces.]

"That one can stay. Rubbing card now: which partner is touching the cart? Say ‘___ on cart, rubbing,’ then choose left or right before it touches the frame."

LEARNER: [holds the rubbing card above the two side spaces and checks the motion strip]

"Table on cart, rubbing. If the motion strip points right, this arrow goes left. Is left the side for this card?"
