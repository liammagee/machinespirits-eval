TUTOR: Set the step stool beside the vertebrae case for a moment. The stretching story has one thing right: reaching higher food can matter. The part to change is this: an adult giraffe stretching during life does not rewrite the traits its offspring inherit.

Use the casts as the population, not one animal. Young giraffes are not identical. Some are born with slightly longer necks because inherited variation already exists. If food is harder to reach, those longer-necked animals may survive and leave more offspring. Over many generations, the population shifts.

Quick check before the visitors arrive: point to one vertebra cast and say whether the change happened because that animal stretched, or because some offspring were born with inherited differences and selection favored them.
