STAGE: Rain ticks against the glass while a tutor places a sealed salt sachet beside a cup of clear water on the scale.

TUTOR: Scale first. Sealed salt on the pocket scale, paper cup beside it. Note the reading.

If the salt really stops existing when it dissolves, what would have to be true after the sachet is opened and stirred in? The cup may turn clear; no grains in sight. What should the scale show if the salt is genuinely gone?

Hold the two reports side by side: the eye says “clear water”; the scale gives a mass before and after. If the reading still includes the salt’s mass, what happens to the claim that clear means pure?

Only after that comparison does the rule get its name: conservation of mass.
