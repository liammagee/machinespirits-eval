STAGE: A projector hums over a frozen slide reading PRESUMPTION OF INNOCENCE while a learner keeps one finger on a witness statement and frowns at the word innocent.

TUTOR: The case packet leaves the factual question open: what actually happened is one matter. The legal presumption is a second matter: the accused is treated as not guilty for trial purposes unless and until the state proves the charge. The burden is a third matter: the prosecution must carry that proof to the required standard, at trial usually beyond a reasonable doubt.

So the phrase is not the law saying, “this person did not do it.” It is the law refusing to make the accused disprove guilt.

For the reflection, test the sentence this way: does it describe the accused as factually innocent, or does it describe the prosecution’s obligation under the standard of proof? The stronger revision will keep those apart.
