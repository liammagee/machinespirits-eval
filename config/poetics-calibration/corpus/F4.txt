A: Is the World War really incomparably vaster than any preceding one?
B: The monetary expense alone demonstrates this fact. According to Treasury Department figures, the total expenditure at the conclusion of the third year approached ninety thousand millions of dollars. Calculated at the then-estimated rate of $116,700,000 daily, direct expenses through March 1, 1918, reached approximately one hundred and ten billions. This dwarfs the combined spending of all wars from 1793 to 1914—including Napoleonic conflicts, the American Civil War, Franco-Prussian, and Russo-Japanese conflicts—which totaled less than a quarter of this amount.
A: Are we justified in naming this the "World" War?
B: By early 1918, approximately 93% of the world's population was involved. Only fifteen neutral countries remained, with combined populations of roughly 130,000,000—a quarter consisting of Dutch East India natives. Meanwhile, twenty-three belligerent nations possessed combined populations exceeding seventeen hundred millions. The "World at War" map illustrates the striking proportion of earth's surface nominally at peace versus engaged nations.
A: Exactly what was the alignment of nations at the beginning of 1918?
B: Central Powers comprised Germany, Austria, Turkey, and Bulgaria. The Entente Allies included Belgium, Brazil, China, Cuba, France, Great Britain, Greece, Italy, Japan, Liberia, Montenegro, Panama, Portugal, Roumania, Russia, San Marino, Serbia, Siam, and the United States. Additionally, Bolivia, Costa Rica, Egypt, Guatemala, Haiti, Honduras, Nicaragua, Peru, and Uruguay severed relations with Central Powers without formally declaring war.
A: How do these opposing forces compare in national power?
B: The Entente Allies and United States possessed 94.4 percent of warring nations' territory; 76.3 percent of population (excluding British Empire's Asiatic holdings); 78.5 percent of available military-age men; 66 percent of enrolled military personnel; 80.5 percent of national wealth; and 83.1 percent of national income. The Allies' combined debt represented 14.7 percent of their wealth versus the Central Powers' 28.7 percent. Annual interest charges consumed 11.8 percent of Central Powers' national income compared to 4.6 percent for Entente Allies. The Entente controlled surplus food-producing regions and metals through direct dominion or naval supremacy.
