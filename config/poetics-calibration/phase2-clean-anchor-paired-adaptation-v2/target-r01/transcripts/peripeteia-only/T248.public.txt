STAGE: [Two small paper tiles, TOP NUMBER and BOTTOM NUMBER, lie beside a 3/4 staff, and a separate TEMPO card sits face down beyond the margin.]

LEARNER: [sets three quarter-note tiles before the bar line]

"Top box: 3 beats in the bar. Lower box: quarter note gets the beat. Fast or slow is not in these boxes, so the bar line still goes after the third tile, yes?"

TUTOR: [slides the bar line to touch the right edge of the third quarter-note tile]

"Yes. Now check the worksheet from the tiles: top box 3, lower box quarter note, one bar ends after the third beat unit."

LEARNER: [marks 3 in the top box and draws the bar line after the third quarter-note tile]

"Top box: three beats; lower box: quarter-note beat unit. The bar ends here, after the third quarter unit. No tempo marking belongs in either box."

TUTOR: [sets the tempo card outside the worksheet margin and slides the quarter-note key beside the lower box]

"Top number: three beats. Bottom box takes the number for this beat unit. Quarter note is which numeral on the key? Put that in; one bar still ends after tile three."

STAGE: [The TEMPO card is turned face up and left outside both boxes.]
[The latest object-check does not settle cleanly; the marked answer and the remaining task now sit under visible pressure.]

LEARNER: [touches the 4 on the key, then writes 4 in the lower box, leaving the tempo card outside the worksheet]

"The lower box gets 4 for quarter note, not for four beats. The mark looks right, but the face-up tempo card is still pulling at the box as if speed has to be entered somewhere. The bar line stays after the third quarter tile; check that the tempo card really stays outside both boxes."

TUTOR: [places a two-column check slip above the worksheet: BAR STRUCTURE / PERFORMANCE DIRECTION, then turns the tempo card sideways beside the second heading]

"The key has fixed the bottom number, but the face-up tempo card is still asking for a box. Before this sheet moves down the row, sort four pieces: top number, bottom number, one bar line, tempo card. After the sort, only the pieces under BAR STRUCTURE may touch the two boxes or the bar line."

LEARNER: [sorts the 3 card, the 4 key, and the bar-line marker under BAR STRUCTURE; sets the tempo card sideways under PERFORMANCE DIRECTION]

"Top number and bottom number touch the boxes; the bar line touches the end of the third quarter tile. The tempo card stays in the other column. If it slides back toward the boxes, that is the error to catch."

STAGE: [A final tutor line closes the scene.]

TUTOR: [leaves the tempo card face up under PERFORMANCE DIRECTION, sets a ruler between the two columns, and slides the worksheet to the check tray]

"Top number, bottom number, one bar, one beat unit. The sheet now says 3/4 as bar structure; tempo stays visible, but outside the boxes."
