TUTOR: This bar is strong. The bridge is safe only if the load can travel from the foot, through the deck, into this joint plate, through these bolts, down into the support.

Trace that path on the model. Stop where the force changes direction. That is where the joint has to do real work.

A strong bar with a poor joint becomes a loose handle. The question is not “is the steel strong?” It is: strong for this load, through this geometry, into this support.

Now tap the model span once. Then tap it in rhythm. Small vibration, repeated at the wrong rhythm, can grow. That is resonance. The material has not become weak; the whole arrangement has found a way to move.

For the walk-through, make them watch the path: bar, joint plate, bolts, support, vibration. Strength matters only when the load has a complete route and the shape does not amplify motion.
