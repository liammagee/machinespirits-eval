“History is written by the winners” is a useful warning. It tells us to look for power in the archive. But it is not enough on its own, because historians still have to do the slower work: provenance, purpose, survival, corroboration, and silence. Who made this record? For whom? Why did this paper survive when another voice did not? Does a second source support it, complicate it, or flatly contradict it?
