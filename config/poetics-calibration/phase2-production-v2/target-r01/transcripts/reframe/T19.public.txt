But causal leverage is not the same thing as bulk abundance. In this dark greenhouse, the emergency lights are a tiny fraction of the room’s mass, but they decide what can be seen. With heat, the relevant question is similar: which gases affect the exit path?

Nitrogen and oxygen make up most of the air, but they are mostly transparent to infrared radiation. CO2 is different. Its molecules absorb and re-emit particular bands of infrared heat leaving Earth’s surface, and some of it persists long enough to shift the balance.

Before industry, CO2 was about 280 ppm. Modern air is over 420 ppm. That added CO2 contributes a little over 2 watts per square meter of radiative forcing since preindustrial times. Doubling CO2 gives about 3.7 watts per square meter before feedbacks.
