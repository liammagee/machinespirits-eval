## High Pole (genuine recognition / recognition-reversal scenes)

H1 — Plato, MENO, slave-boy geometry demonstration (Stephanus 82b–85b) — Benjamin Jowett translation — https://www.math.mcgill.ca/rags/JAC/124/meno.pdf (PDF; also available at https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/1643)
NOTE: Text extracted verbatim from the TCD/McGill PDF of the Jowett translation. Excerpt covers Socrates calling the boy, the confident wrong answer (doubling the side), the torpedo-shock moment of aporia, and the diagonal solution. Speaker labels: Soc. / Boy. / Men.

H2 — Sophocles, OEDIPUS THE KING (Oedipus Tyrannus), Theban Herdsman recognition scene — F. Storr translation (1912), public domain — https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/31 (Project Gutenberg #31, "Plays of Sophocles: Oedipus the King; Oedipus at Colonus; Antigone")
NOTE: CORRECTED 2026-05-20. The original fetch (mislabelled "Jebb / Perseus") contained "Damn you!", which is NOT Storr's or Jebb's diction — i.e. a modern in-copyright translation or a paraphrase, not the cited public-domain text. Re-sourced verbatim from Project Gutenberg #31 (Storr 1912), lines 2248–2498 (sources/H2.raw.txt), and cross-checked against the MIT Internet Classics Archive Storr text (which agrees verbatim over the overlap but is itself truncated server-side at play-line 1602, just before the confession). Verified Storr reads "A plague upon thee! Hold thy wanton tongue!". Speaker labels: Oedipus / Herdsman / Messenger / Chorus. Covers the herdsman's confession (child of Laius' house, given to be exposed) and culminates in Oedipus's recognition cry.

H3 — Plato, EUTHYPHRO, closing exchange (~Stephanus 15c to end) — Benjamin Jowett translation — https://standardebooks.org/ebooks/plato/dialogues/benjamin-jowett/text/euthyphro
NOTE: Verbatim text from Standard Ebooks edition of Jowett. Covers the circular collapse of all definitions of piety and Euthyphro's abrupt departure. Speaker labels: Soc. / Euth.

H4 — Plato, THEAETETUS, final aporia (~Stephanus 209d–210b) — Benjamin Jowett translation — https://www.gutenberg.org/cache/epub/1726/pg1726.txt (Project Gutenberg)
NOTE: Text extracted verbatim by direct curl from the Gutenberg plain-text file, lines ~7100–7155. Covers the collapse of the third definition (true opinion with definition), the midwifery conclusion, and Socrates' departure to meet Meletus. Speaker labels: SOCRATES / THEAETETUS.

H5 — Euripides, IPHIGENIA IN TAURIS (Iphigenia among the Taurians), recognition scene between Iphigenia and Orestes — Robert Potter translation (1781) — https://classics.mit.edu/Euripides/iph_taur.html (MIT Internet Classics Archive)
NOTE: Text extracted verbatim by direct curl of the MIT Classics page (HTML stripped), lines ~1300–1430. Covers Iphigenia dictating the letter, Orestes recognizing his sister, the mutual declaration, and the proof-exchange (golden ram tapestry, Pelops' spear). Speaker labels: IPHIGENIA / ORESTES / PYLADES / LEADER OF THE CHORUS. Gilbert Murray's verse translation (Gutenberg ebook 5063) was identified but NOT used: Murray died 1957; UK copyright expires 2027. Potter's 1781 translation is definitively public domain.
