STAGE: [PLACARD pinned to the board, hand-printed in marker: 'BAKERY, MONDAY MORNING — LOAF 5.40. BAKERY, MONDAY EVENING — LOAF 6.80.']

TUTOR: [The tutor writes under the pinned bakery list: “Mechanism is not acquittal.” Then draws two columns: “Supply cost” and “Wider margin.”]

"Start there. The new price bites into a fixed weekly budget. That fact stays on the table.

Now use the price list as evidence, not as a verdict.

[The tutor writes between the two columns: “PRICE RISE ALONE PROVES: the old price stopped holding.”]

That is the first claim. Only the first.

If wheat, flour, heat, or delivery rose, the bakery may face a higher cost for putting the same loaf on the counter. Then the higher price is a signal of supply pressure at the old price.

If those costs stayed flat and the gap to the baker widened, then the charge changes. That is not supply pressure. That is extraction.

[pauses, then taps the printed list]

Sort it cleanly. From this paper alone: old price failed. Not from this paper alone: greed, cost pressure, or widened margin.

Same pain at the counter. Different evidence needed."

LEARNER: "All right — the price list alone doesn't convict them. Granted. But the columns sit clean on the board, and the bakery isn't opening its wheat invoices for a Wednesday night class. Monday it was the small loaf instead of the usual — that was already the cost, before any of this sorting. So what does the distinction do for anyone at the counter, if the evidence to fill the right column never leaves the back office?"

TUTOR: "It does not lower the price tonight. That has to be said.

[The tutor writes across both columns: “A DISTINCTION DOES NOT PAY THE BILL. IT CHANGES THE DEMAND.” Traffic cuts through the open window. The chalk is set beside the pinned price list.]

Take the chalk. Two lines only.

[On the board:]

“CAN SAY FROM THIS PAPER:”

“MUST ASK BEFORE BLAME LANDS:”

First line: the loaf costs more now. Monday’s choice got smaller. The budget hit moved to the buyer. That much is public.

Second line: missing evidence. Not a verdict yet.

If the bakery says costs rose, the demand is: show the input cost. Flour, heat, delivery. One chain.

If the bakery says nothing, the demand is: show the margin. Same loaf, wider gap.

[The tutor taps the blank second line.]

That blank is not an excuse for the bakery. It is the pressure point. No invoices, no clean acquittal. No margins, no clean conviction.

Same small loaf. Same anger. The distinction keeps the demand from becoming fog."

STAGE: [PLACARD slid over the price list, hand-printed: 'CORRECT SURFACE, UNSETTLED RELATION.']
[PLACARD: The answer sounds settled, but the current object still does not pass the check.]

LEARNER: [chalk in hand, marks the second line]

"If they go silent: margin gap. If they cite cost: input chain. The sort runs — and then it points the asking back across the table at the buyer. The price list looked like a verdict on greed; the columns break that into two injuries, and one is supposed to bite without a villain. What stays unsettled is which side is now holding the question: the counter, with a smaller loaf, asked to prove the case it can't reach."

TUTOR: "The columns have stopped doing their job.

They split two possible causes. They do not settle who has to carry the unanswered question. The counter has the loss in hand. The bakery has the papers that could answer it.

[The tutor turns the three plastic chairs toward the board and tapes one label to each chair.]

“FELT AT THE COUNTER”

“HELD IN THE BACK OFFICE”

“DEMANDED BECAUSE ACCESS IS UNEQUAL”

[He lays six torn slips on the folding table: “Monday smaller loaf,” “new price list,” “flour invoice,” “energy bill,” “delivery cost,” “weekly margin record.”]

Move them.

Put each slip under one chair.

The rule is strict: harm can open the case, but private records carry the answer. When the answer is locked away, the demand is not “prove greed from the counter.” It is “open the record that only the bakery holds.”"

LEARNER: [lifts a slip]

""Monday smaller loaf" — counter. "New price list" — [hand hovers between two chairs] — pinned in the window, so counter, even though the bakery wrote it. Flour invoice, energy bill, delivery cost, margin record — those four go under the back office; nothing reaches for the third chair. Two against four, and the bare chair is the point: the counter can't sit holding a smaller loaf while four slips stay locked in a drawer — open them, or the silence is the answer."
