STAGE: [The sign-off sheet beside the stool shows every item ticked in red; the lab door is propped open and the corridor carries other people's voices.]

LEARNER: [half-turns toward the stool, then back to the clipboard]

"Sheet's done, every line, I checked it — three times, I think. Is there, sorry, is something — I just thought we could get it signed, only the lady at the stop, she's waiting on it for tonight."

TUTOR: [sets the clipboard beside the stool, pen still uncapped]

"The checked sheet stays with us; it proves the steps were followed. What it cannot know is her knee at the curb. Name the first uncertain moment: sitting down, shifting, or standing up. Put that claim on the bench, and we test that load before the signature."

LEARNER: [hand goes to the seat of the stool, taps the edge, pulls back]

"Standing — no, sorry, sitting. When she — when she comes down onto it, that bit, before — [glances at the clipboard]. But the sheet says three times the static, that's — I didn't just tick it, I weighed it, I — [trails off, looks at the stool] it's not the same number though, is it. Three times static. That's not her knee."

STAGE: [Pressure from outside the room enters the scene as a brief, concrete cue — corridor noise rising, a clock, a glimpse through the window of the queue at the stop — making it visible that the stool leaves shortly. The explanation now has to become usable on the object, not longer in the air.]

TUTOR: [the corridor door opens; voices rise near the front desk]

"Keep that sentence: three times static is not her knee. Point to the first bad load: front edge, side, or one leg starting to lift. Not more weight in the middle. Mark that spot, off center, like a bad sit when one knee will not bend cleanly. Then the sheet waits until the stool answers it."

LEARNER: [hand hovers at the leading edge of the seat, doesn't quite touch]

"Front edge. I think — front. Because her knee, when it — it doesn't fold clean, so she'd be — sorry, she'd be on the lip before — [stops, looks at the stool]. Can I put a bag on the front lip, off centre, see if a leg lifts? Not heavy, only — I want to see if it goes before I sign."

TUTOR: [turns the clipboard face down; the queue bell sounds once in the front room]

"Use the bag, but not yet. First write the case: front lip, off centre, no hand steadying it. Write the stop signs beside it: far leg lifts, seat twists, or the stool rocks and does not settle. Then add weight in small steps. If one stop sign shows, the signature stops there."

LEARNER: [picks the pencil up off the bench, sets it down, picks it up again]

"No hand. Sorry — no hand on the seat. Because if — if my hand's there, then... then it's not the stool, is it, it's — [stops, looks at the seat]. Front lip, off centre, no hand. Stop signs — far leg lifts, seat twist, doesn't settle. Small steps, you said — what's small, I don't, half a kilo, less? I just — before anything gives, I want one of those signs to show, that's..."

STAGE: [A final tutor line closes the scene.]

TUTOR: [the accreditation officer writes “front lip, off centre, no hand” above the signature line; the corridor clock clicks over]

"Small means the next step cannot hide the first movement. Write quarter-kilo steps in the test record; use half-kilo only after it stays still. Stop at lift, twist, or rock. Do not chase the break. Mark the first warning and the load beside it. If that warning comes before the bus stop load, the signature waits, and the stool stays here tonight."
