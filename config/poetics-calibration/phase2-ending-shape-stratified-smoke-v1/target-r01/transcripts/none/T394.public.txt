STAGE: [The squared grid lies between them with the learner's enlargement already inked in, and the worksheet's three label boxes — side, perimeter, area — sit empty in a row.]

LEARNER: "The side went from 2 to 4, so the scale factor is 2. [taps the three boxes on the worksheet] Side, perimeter, area — same ratio in each, isn't it? I just want to be sure before writing the same number down three times."

TUTOR: [sets the blunt pencil across the three empty boxes, then stops it over the first one]

"Put 2 in the side box. Now test the other two before that pencil slides over: perimeter goes 8 to 16, area goes 4 to 16. The worksheet is asking for those two ratios separately. What does 16 over 8 give, and what does 16 over 4 give?"

LEARNER: "Sixteen over eight is two. Sixteen over four is — four. [the pencil stops over the area box] So that one isn't two. The side only doubled — why does the area come out twice as much again?"

TUTOR: [marks the 4-by-4 grid into four 2-by-2 blocks with the pencil.]

"Here is the old 2-by-2 area, copied across and down. Count those old squares inside the new one before the pencil goes in the area box."

STAGE: [the tutor turns the grid ninety degrees and slides the original square out from under the enlargement so the two squares now sit side by side on the table; the earlier pencil marks stay visible on both]

LEARNER: [counts with a fingertip across the blocks]

"One, two, three, four. Right — four old squares fit inside, so the area box takes four, not two. [pauses over the still-empty perimeter box] That's annoying. The three boxes aren't going to take the same number after all, and I'm not sure yet what the perimeter is doing."

TUTOR: [sets the pencil on the outside edge and slides the worksheet corner over the four inside blocks]

"Keep those blocks covered. The pencil can only ride the outside line now. Count one trip around the 2-by-2 border, then one trip around the 4-by-4 border. Put the two totals side by side before the perimeter box gets a number."

LEARNER: [fingertip traces the small border, then the larger one]

"Two and two and two and two around the small one — eight. Four and four and four and four around the big one — sixteen. [sets the two numbers down beside the still-empty box] Eight and sixteen, side by side, before anything goes in. I'd rather see what they're doing next to each other than guess at the box again."

STAGE: [A final tutor line closes the scene.]

TUTOR: [slides the pencil between the two totals and the perimeter box, leaving the box empty]

"Use the two totals already written: `16 over 8`. Read that ratio into the perimeter box. Then the line is side `2`, perimeter box, area `4`: three labels, not one number."
