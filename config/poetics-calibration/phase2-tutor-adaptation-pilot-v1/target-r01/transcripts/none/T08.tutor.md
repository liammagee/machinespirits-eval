FEEDBACK: The draft is strong. It keeps the scene brisk, uses the red pen and time pressure well, and teaches by rule-testing rather than by giving a tolerance lecture. The key pedagogical move works: it separates “pattern exists” from “institutional register expected”, which directly addresses the learner’s shame without over-comforting them.

The best line is: “If there are conditions, there is grammar.” Keep that. It gives the learner a usable test, not just a slogan.

Main risk: “Red pen first, apology later” is stylish but may land too sharply for an anxious learner who already feels corrected and ashamed. Also, “where would speakers of that dialect reject it?” is excellent but slightly abstract; the learner may need one small concrete prompt to know what kind of contrast counts.

Socratically, it mostly succeeds: it asks for the learner’s actual sentence and makes them discover the rule. It should avoid adding much more explanation. The draft already has enough theory.

KEEP_OR_CHANGE: revise lightly.

Keep the structure, time pressure, distinction between descriptive and prescriptive grammar, and the demand for the real dialect form. Soften or clarify the opening edge, and add just enough scaffolding to help the learner test acceptability without turning the moment into a lecture.
