The clear look is one report: no salt grains big enough for the eye to catch. The scale is another report: the sealed cup has not lost what went into it.

So “gone” has to be tested against both reports. If the salt were gone from the cup, the reading should drop by the salt’s mass. If the reading holds, then clear cannot mean pure. It means the salt is no longer showing as pieces.

That is the conservation problem sitting on the table: not visible, but still counted by the balance.

One more test before the lights go off: if a spoonful of this clear water dries on the dish, what should be left behind if the salt is still there without showing?
