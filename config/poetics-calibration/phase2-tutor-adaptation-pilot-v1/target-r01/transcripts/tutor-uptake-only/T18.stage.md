FEEDBACK: The draft is strong on object setup and evidence: cup, salt, scale, sealed cup, reading. It begins from weighing rather than reassurance, and it frames “gone” as a claim that can be tested, which is pedagogically useful. The final question is generative because it asks the learner to compare sight against measurement instead of simply accepting an explanation.

The main risk is that it names both possible interpretations too cleanly before the learner has had to notice the conflict. “If it’s still in there, spread too thin to see” is close to giving the conceptual resolution early. The scene constraint says conservation of mass should emerge after the learner names the conflict between sight and mass; this draft nearly performs that naming for them. It also uses “disappears from sight” well, but “stops existing” may be slightly abstract for a novice unless anchored more tightly to “gone from the cup” versus “gone from view.”

KEEP_OR_CHANGE: revise lightly, with reasons. Keep the after-hours balance setup, the sealed-cup test, the practical clipped rhythm, and the pressure on the word “gone.” Change by withholding the “spread too thin to see” explanation until after the learner compares the readings. Let the scale produce the contradiction first, then let the learner separate “clear” from “pure” and “not visible” from “not there.”
