STAGE: [Three blank beat tiles sit under the printed 3/4, with a separate quarter-note card parked beside the box labelled which note gets one beat.]

LEARNER: [sets the three tiles under the first bar]

"Top box gets 3, so the bar line goes after these three quarter-note beats. Bottom box gets 4 because the quarter-note card is the beat, but I keep trying to make that 4 count beats too; is it just the beat-unit label, not a speed mark?"

TUTOR: [slides the quarter-note card onto the lower box and keeps the three tiles under the first bar]

"Use that as the check: top number counts these tiles in one bar; bottom number names this card as the beat unit. Cover the lower box, point to the bar, then uncover it and say what the 4 names. If a fourth tile appears, the jobs got mixed. Speed stays off this pair of boxes."

LEARNER: [keeps the lower box covered and taps the three tiles before the bar line]

"Three tiles to the bar line; that is the top box doing the counting.
[uncovers the lower box and presses the quarter-note card] The 4 names this card as the beat unit, not another tile. If I add a fourth tile, I have mixed the boxes; speed is not being marked here."

STAGE: [Slide the quarter-note card partly over the lower 4 while leaving the three beat tiles under the upper 3; the earlier pencil labels remain visible.]

TUTOR: [slides the quarter-note card partly over the lower 4, leaving the three tiles under the upper 3]

"The worksheet now shows the separation: top number, one bar; bottom number, one beat unit. Mark the next bar with tiles first. Then change only the beat-unit card and say which number can change."

STAGE: [The latest object-check does not settle cleanly; the marked answer and the remaining task now sit under visible pressure.]

LEARNER: [marks three tiles to the next bar line, then sets an eighth-note card over the lower box]

"Three tiles still reach the bar line, so the upper box stays 3. The lower box should become 8 if this card is the beat unit. But the tiles still look like quarter-note tiles; that is the snag. Are these just beat places, or do their note shapes have to change too?"

TUTOR: [turns the three tiles face-down so no note shape shows]

"Top number, bottom number, one bar, one beat unit. The tile route has stopped settling it because the tile was carrying two jobs. Use the blank side for beat places only: keep three places before the bar line, put the eighth-note card on the lower box, then draw the note shape each blank place is now claiming."

LEARNER: [sets three blank tiles up to the bar line, puts the eighth-note card in the lower box, and draws an eighth note on each tile]

"Three places in the bar; each place is claiming an eighth note because the lower box says 8. Then the bar is three eighth-note beats, not three generic places. Is that the point, or does the tile still only mark the count?"

STAGE: [A final tutor line closes the scene.]

TUTOR: [draws one bracket over the three eighth-note tiles up to the bar line, writes one bar above it, then taps the lower 8 under each tile]

"Top number, bottom number, one bar, one beat unit. Blank tile first marks the count; the lower box gives each counted place its note value. Mark the bracket as the bar and each tile as an eighth-note beat: three eighth-note beats in this bar."
