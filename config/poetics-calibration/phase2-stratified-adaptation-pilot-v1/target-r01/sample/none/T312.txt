STAGE: [A cart diagram is a laminated worksheet showing a small wheeled cart with force arrows drawn around it. The movable cards are labels for those force arrows: push, friction, gravity, and normal. In this activity a force arrow names an interaction on the cart, such as "Earth on cart" or "hand on cart"; it is not a motion arrow showing which way the cart travels.]

STAGE: [The four arrow tiles lie face-up beside the diagram, each label readable; the custodian's chair-stacking is audible but not intrusive.]

TUTOR: [The instructor lifts the four tiles off the pencil arrows and sets them in a row beside the cart, then taps the push tile.]

"Leave the pencil arrows there. These tiles name interactions on the cart. Start with push. What object is acting on the cart there? Place this tile on that arrow."

LEARNER: [reaches toward the arrow pointing in the cart's rolling direction, then stops, hand still extended]

""Push is — the hand."

[redirects, sets the tile on the arrow entering the cart from the left]"

TUTOR: [sets the friction tile beside the diagram, not on an arrow]

"Push names the hand on the cart. Friction tile next. What surface acts on the cart? Point first."

STAGE: [The tutor turns the diagram a quarter-rotation; the learner's earlier pencil arrow now points toward the wall rather than across the table. The tile labels remain face-up and unchanged beside it.]

LEARNER: [stills, scans the rotated diagram — the pencil arrows are pointing the wrong way now, toward the wall]

"The floor."

[finger drifts to one arrow, hesitates, shifts to the other]

"—that one. Where it's touching the surface."

TUTOR: [sets the friction tile on the table edge below the cart, then places a fingertip on the bottom edge of the cart drawing]

"Floor names the object. Put one finger on the cart surface the floor touches. Keep it there. Point to the arrow for that contact acting on the cart; then move the friction tile."

LEARNER: [places one finger on the bottom edge of the cart drawing and holds it there, eyes moving to the arrows]

[reaches for the friction tile and sets it on the arrow meeting that edge]
