### Tutor Superego (critique)
_provenance: role=tutor_superego | backend=codex | model=config-default | reasoning=xhigh | prompt=8217e9c1ab57b438 | latencyMs=19405_

FEEDBACK: The draft is strong on scene discipline: visible boards, a delivery note, concrete quantities, and the crucial distinction between mechanism and excuse. It respects the learner’s moral anger without surrendering the economic point.

The main weakness is that it mostly performs the reasoning for the learner. The evidence test is clear, but the learner is not yet made to use it. For a new learner who suspects profiteering, the tutor should slow the move from “price rise” to “signal” and make the learner compare two possible causes against public facts.

ADAPTATION_CHECKS:
- Pedagogical soundness: PASS. It teaches the difference between price movement and causal proof.
- Emotional attunement: PASS. “It does not excuse anyone” is essential and should stay.
- Socratic method: PARTIAL. The tutor names the test but does not give the learner a generative role.
  REQUIRED_REWRITE: Build a public comparison task where the learner must say what evidence would appear under “greed” versus “constraint,” using the boards, delivery note, queues, rationing, or replacement supply.
- ZPD awareness: PARTIAL. The scaffold is concrete, but “old terms no longer cleared the shelf” may be too compressed for a new learner.
  REQUIRED_REWRITE: Expose the mechanism through a concrete counterfactual: what happens to shelves or queues if 12 sacks arrive but the old 90p price remains.

KEEP_OR_CHANGE: revise lightly. Keep the anger line, the two price boards, the delivery note, and “same price board, different evidence behind it.” Change the turn so the learner has to test the cause publicly rather than simply receive the tutor’s distinction.

REQUIRED_REWRITE: Add one or two concrete public questions tied to the board: what evidence would prove margin-taking, what evidence would prove replacement-cost pressure, and who is excluded under each allocation rule.
