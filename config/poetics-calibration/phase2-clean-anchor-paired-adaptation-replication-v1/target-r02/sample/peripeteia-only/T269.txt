STAGE: [The carbon tile sits in the tray with four loose labels beside it: atomic number, symbol, name, atomic mass.]

LEARNER: [places the four label cards beside the tile]

"Top 6 gets atomic number, C is symbol, carbon is the name, and 12.01 is atomic mass, I think. The bottom decimal is the part I want checked before I copy it."

TUTOR: [slides the “atomic mass” card directly under 12.01, leaving a small gap from “carbon,” then taps the four cards in place.]

"This layout checks out. Keep the gap visible, read the four parts once, then copy: top 6 atomic number, C symbol, carbon name, bottom 12.01 atomic mass."

LEARNER: [keeps the atomic mass card under 12.01 with the gap showing.]

"The gap keeps 12.01 separate from carbon. Top 6 atomic number, C symbol, carbon name, bottom 12.01 atomic mass. I will copy that order."

TUTOR: [slides the clear tray under the bottom edge so only 12.01 and the atomic mass card sit over it.]

"Yes. One last check down the tile: top 6, C, carbon, then the tray line with 12.01. If each card points to only one part, copy that order."

STAGE: [the tray is turned ninety degrees; the four loose label cards stay in their original order beside it]
[The latest object-check does not settle cleanly; the marked answer and the remaining task now sit under visible pressure.]

LEARNER: [holds the atomic mass card close to 12.01 and moves the loose cards a little back.]

"Top 6, C, carbon, then 12.01 is clear. The tray turned across the bottom makes the loose cards look like another row. Keep only 12.01 on the tray line, then I can copy it."

TUTOR: [removes the tray, turns it upside down beside the tile, and puts the loose cards on it.]

"The tray line stopped settling this; it made a false row. Now it is only a parking place. One card comes off the tray at a time: place it on one printed part, name that match, then move it to the copy side. No card copies until it passes alone."

LEARNER: [takes the atomic number card from the upside-down tray and sets it beside the top 6.]

"Parking place only; this card has to pass alone. Atomic number points to the top 6 only, so it can move to the copy side. The other cards stay on the tray; next I test symbol against C, not copy the whole row together."

STAGE: [A final tutor line closes the scene.]

TUTOR: [sets the atomic number card on the copy side and leaves the tray upside down with the other cards parked.]

"That rule holds. Take only the symbol card next and set it beside C. If it fits alone, move it to the copy side; the other cards wait their own turn before the packet copy."
