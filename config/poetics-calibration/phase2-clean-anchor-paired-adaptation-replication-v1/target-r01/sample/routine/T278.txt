STAGE: [Three note tiles sit under the 3/4 sign; the pencil rests between the boxes labeled "beats in the bar" and "which note gets one beat."]

LEARNER: [sets the pencil under the lower box]

"Top box: three beats. Bottom box: the quarter-note tile gets one beat. The 4 still pulls toward speed for me, so I want to test it by putting the bar line after the third tile."

TUTOR: [sets the pencil as a bar line after the third quarter-note tile]

"The pencil has made one bar. Which box did the bar line test: the three-beat box or the quarter-note-tile box? Say it from the tiles; then we label top number, bottom number, one bar, one beat unit."

LEARNER: [points to the upper box]

"The bar line tested the three-beat box: three quarter-note tiles make one bar. The lower box labels the quarter-note tile as the beat unit, so here the 4 is naming that unit, not setting speed. I still want to test the pressure on the lower number: if the bottom box said 8, which tile would count as one beat?"

STAGE: [The worksheet is turned ninety degrees; the three note tiles stay aligned under the 3, while a quarter-note card is slid beside the 4.]

TUTOR: [slides an eighth-note card beside the lower box marked 8, then sets the quarter-note tile just beyond it]

"Top number, bottom number, one bar, one beat unit. Read the lower box first: with 8 here, which card is the one beat unit? Then we ask how many of that unit the quarter-note tile covers, not what speed it sets."

LEARNER: [sets a finger on the eighth-note card]

"With 8 in the lower box, this eighth-note card is one beat unit. The quarter-note tile covers two of those units, so I should count its size against that card, not treat the 8 as a speed mark. I want to place three eighth-note units in one bar and check the bar line."

TUTOR: [sets the three eighth-note cards loose beside the lower box and holds the pencil at the right edge]

"Top number, bottom number, one bar, one beat unit. In 3/8, place the pencil where the bar ends after three beat units. Then say what the pencil proves, and how many eighth-note units the quarter-note tile covers before the line."

LEARNER: [places the pencil after the third eighth-note card]

"The pencil marks one bar in 3/8: three eighth-note beat units. The quarter-note tile covers two eighth-note units, so it fills two counts and leaves one eighth-note unit left of the bar line. I want to check the label rule next: the top number counts the units named by the lower box."

STAGE: [A final tutor line closes the scene.]

TUTOR: [draws a bracket over the three eighth-note cards and writes 3 above it, then taps the 8 beside the eighth-note card]

"Top number, bottom number, one bar, one beat unit. From the cards: which box names the eighth-note unit, and which box counts three of those units before the pencil? Say the label rule once; then the worksheet is ready for the part-check."
