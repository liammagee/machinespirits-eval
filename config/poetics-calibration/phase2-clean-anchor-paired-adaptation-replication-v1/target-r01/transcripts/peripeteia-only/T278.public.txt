STAGE: [Three note tiles sit under the 3/4 sign; the pencil rests between the boxes labeled "beats in the bar" and "which note gets one beat."]

LEARNER: [sets the pencil under the lower box]

"Top box: three beats. Bottom box: the quarter-note tile gets one beat. The 4 still pulls toward speed for me, so I want to test it by putting the bar line after the third tile."

TUTOR: [sets the pencil as a bar line after the third quarter-note tile]

"The pencil has made one bar. Which box did the bar line test: the three-beat box or the quarter-note-tile box? Say it from the tiles; then we label top number, bottom number, one bar, one beat unit."

LEARNER: [points to the upper box]

"The bar line tested the three-beat box: three quarter-note tiles make one bar. The lower box labels the quarter-note tile as the beat unit, so here the 4 is naming that unit, not setting speed. I still want to test the pressure on the lower number: if the bottom box said 8, which tile would count as one beat?"

STAGE: [The worksheet is turned ninety degrees; the three note tiles stay aligned under the 3, while a quarter-note card is slid beside the 4.]

TUTOR: [slides an eighth-note card beside the lower box marked 8, then sets the quarter-note tile just beyond it]

"Top number, bottom number, one bar, one beat unit. Read the lower box first: with 8 here, which card is the one beat unit? Then we ask how many of that unit the quarter-note tile covers, not what speed it sets."

STAGE: [The latest object-check does not settle cleanly; the marked answer and the remaining task now sit under visible pressure.]

LEARNER: [marks two eighth-card spaces under the quarter-note tile]

"With 8 in the lower box, the eighth-note card is one beat unit. The quarter-note tile fills two eighth spaces, so one quarter tile counts as two beats here. That makes the bar line awkward: for 3/8 we stop after three eighth-card spaces, halfway through the next quarter tile if quarters stay on the table."

TUTOR: [sets the quarter-note tiles aside and draws a three-slot bar frame labeled 3/8, one slot per eighth card]

"The quarter tiles have stopped settling this; they are too large to be the frame now. Top number, bottom number, one bar, one beat unit: fill the three slots with eighth cards first. Then place one quarter tile across slots 1-2, and again starting at slot 3; mark which one fits inside the bar and which one must tie over the bar line."

LEARNER: [places three eighth-note cards in the 3/8 slots, lays one quarter tile across slots 1-2, then starts another at slot 3 and draws the pencil bar line after slot 3]

"The first quarter tile fits inside the bar: two eighth slots. The second one does not fit; it takes slot 3 and one more eighth slot past the pencil, so it needs a tie over the bar line. The 8 names the eighth card as the beat unit, and the 3 stops the bar after three of those units."

STAGE: [A final tutor line closes the scene.]

TUTOR: [labels the top box 3 and the bottom box 8, then rests the pencil on the bar line after the third eighth card]

"Top number, bottom number, one bar, one beat unit. These three eighth cards fill one bar, so 3 goes above; this eighth card is the beat unit, so 8 goes below. The quarter tile only shows the fit: two units inside, tied when it crosses the pencil. That is the label we take to rehearsal."
