STAGE: [A fraction card shows 3/8 with arrows pointing to the top number, bottom number, and fraction bar. The task is to label numerator, denominator, and fraction bar; it is not yet a comparison of fraction sizes.]

STAGE: [A 3/8 card lies in a clear sleeve beside three blank tabs, and a pencil rests exactly across the fraction bar.]

TUTOR: [sets the pencil across the fraction bar and leaves the three labels off the card.]

"First point, no labels: top number. Bottom number. Fraction bar.”

[turns the labels face up beside the card.]

“Now place them by position.”

[slides the denominator label back from the printed 8 to the lower tab space.]

“Not bigger number. Bottom position. Denominator.”

“Top position. Numerator. Pencil mark. Fraction bar.”

“Read the card: three eighths."

LEARNER: [keeps one finger on the lower tab and looks at the printed 8.]

"I keep wanting to put denominator on the 8, but the bottom place is the label place? Top number, numerator. Bottom number, denominator. The pencil mark is the fraction bar, so this says three eighths?"

STAGE: [The tutor slides the blank tab marked IMPORTANT away from the card and places three smaller tabs beside the card: TOP, BAR, BOTTOM.]

TUTOR: [slides BOTTOM under the lower space, leaving the printed 8 uncovered; sets BAR on the pencil line and TOP above the 3.]

"The printed 8 pulls the eye.

The 8 stays a number. The tab names the place.

Touch the tabs: TOP. BAR. BOTTOM.

Then read the card: three eighths."

LEARNER: [touches the upper tab, the pencil line, then the lower tab.]

"Top, bar, bottom. The 8 stays the number, and the lower tab is denominator? So this card says three eighths."

TUTOR: [keeps DENOMINATOR on the lower tab and leaves the printed 8 clear.]

"The lower tab names the bottom position: denominator.

The 8 stays clear as the number.

Touch top, bar, bottom once more. Then sleeve the card: three eighths."

LEARNER: [slides the sleeve partway on, then stops with one finger on the lower tab.]

"Top... bar... bottom. Denominator goes on this lower tab, and the 8 stays clear. Three eighths. Does numerator go on the top tab the same way?"
