STAGE: Under the strip light, a beaker of clear salt water sits on the balance while chairs scrape in the corridor outside.

TUTOR: Before the lab note goes down, place the beaker of water on the balance, add the spoonful of salt, and stir. The crystals may vanish from sight. What does that seem to say, and what does the balance say?

LEARNER: My first instinct is that the clear water makes the salt seem gone. If the balance reading stays higher after stirring, does that mean the salt is still in the beaker somehow, even though I cannot see it?

TUTOR: The eyes report clear water; the balance tests whether matter has left. With the same beaker still there and the reading still higher after stirring, which claim fits the observation: gone, or present but no longer visible?

STAGE: A lab assistant leans in to ask whether the balance is free in one minute. The next learner turn must be brief and choose one observation to trust first: clarity or mass.

LEARNER: The mass first, I think. If the balance stays higher, the salt is still in the beaker even when the water looks clear.

TUTOR: The crystals are no longer visible, yet the beaker still carries the added mass. How should that go into the note instead of “the salt disappeared”?

LEARNER: I would write, “The salt dissolved; the crystals were no longer visible, but the beaker still showed the added mass.” I am still unsure whether dissolved means the salt is spread through the water rather than gone.

STAGE: closes the scene.
