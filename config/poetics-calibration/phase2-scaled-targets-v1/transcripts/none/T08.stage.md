The transcript has two marks in the margin: “nonstandard” beside one line, “error?” beside another. Before either gets changed, one question matters: what pattern is the speaker following there?

A dialect is not tested by whether it matches the edited norm first. It is tested by whether its forms work systematically for its speakers. Descriptive grammar studies those patterns. Prescriptive grammar names selected rules for selected settings, like a formal transcript note or a school essay.

So start with the line on the screen: does the form recur in a patterned way, or is it just random breakdown?
