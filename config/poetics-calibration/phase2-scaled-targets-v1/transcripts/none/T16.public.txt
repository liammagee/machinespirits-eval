That is where the tidy story changes. A larger herd can strip food faster than the woodland replaces it, leaving less cover and forage, more pressure on each animal, and a sharper crash when winter, drought, or disease arrives.

Predators are one pressure in that system. Remove them, and browsing pressure, carrying capacity, and the wider food web begin to shift with the deer.

The deer count may rise first. Then those arrows down to regrowth show the delayed cost: less forage for the next season and less young cover in the woodland. Winter, drought, or disease may make the crash visible later; overbrowsing can be loading the system before that moment arrives.
