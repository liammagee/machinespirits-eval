The moral risk is real: an average becomes a stereotype when it is used as a verdict. Used carefully, it is a prompt for inquiry.

So in the debrief, keep the claims separated:

“At ward level, reassessment is often delayed.”

“In this anonymized case, reassessment was documented 35 minutes after analgesia.”

Now the audit lead is asking for the case summary. The responsible move is not “this patient got average care.” It is: does this case match the ward pattern, depart from it, or reveal a different process?

The group statistic can guide the question. The case evidence has to carry the claim.
