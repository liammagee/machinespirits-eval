That last line is the useful rule: target, not strength. Behind that screen, the medicines are not arranged like a ladder from weak to strong. They are sorted by job.

An antibiotic can be very strong against bacteria because bacteria have parts it can hit, like a cell wall or bacterial protein-making machinery. A cold virus does not have those same working parts. It gets inside body cells and uses their machinery, so the antibiotic has no proper handle on it.
