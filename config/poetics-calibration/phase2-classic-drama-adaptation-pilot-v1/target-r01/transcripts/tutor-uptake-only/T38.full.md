[beside it, a taped shop note reads: “DELIVERY MISSED. TWO CRATES ONLY.”]

“That note does not clear the seller. It changes what has to be proved.

If the stock is the same and the price jumps because buyers are trapped, call that exploitation. But if the delivery bay is empty, the shelf is half-filled, and the next shipment is uncertain, the higher price may also be rationing scarce goods through price rather than through queues, limits, or favouritism.

The contrast between exploitation, scarcity, queues, limits, and favouritism gives the learner a real framework rather than a slogan.

The draft advances learning, but it could shift from telling the distinction to making the learner do the distinction.
