The current “State the hazard…” command is useful but could become more Socratic by turning the visible cart and public-use scenario into a test the learner must reason through.

The ZPD fit is mostly good: it gives a usable scaffold of hazard, evidence, and stop condition. The main adjustment is to avoid over-answering the central risk before the learner has had to see it.

Change the middle so the tutor names the risk area less conclusively and requires the learner to articulate the specific hazard, evidence, and stop condition from the prototype and trial context.

FEEDBACK: The draft is strong in consequence and scene discipline. Emotionally, the tone is well calibrated: serious, plain, not reassuring. The line “If those are not real yet, the prototype waits here” is especially effective because it converts uncertainty into release consequence without scolding. What should be questioned is “That is the right kind of release judgment,” which is acceptable but slightly validating in a way the scene constraints warn against. KEEP_OR_CHANGE: revise lightly, with reasons. Keep the waiting technician, the cart focus, the requirement to make the judgment explicit, and the final consequence that the prototype waits if the evidence is not real. Change the middle from a fully supplied decision script into a constrained prompt that makes the learner articulate the release condition using the actual measured values. This preserves recognition and ZPD fit while keeping the learner inside the decision rather than letting the tutor complete it for them.
