FEEDBACK: The draft is strong on stakes, setting, and institutional seriousness. It keeps the prototype and sign-off sheet physically present, distinguishes checklist completion from release judgment, and names a plausible concrete risk without melodrama. The line “Without that decision, it does not leave on checklist compliance alone” is appropriately firm for release authority.

What risks flattening the scene is that the tutor mostly supplies the missed risk and the decision structure. That advances safety reasoning, but it gives less room for the learner to make the judgment themselves. Since the learner directly asks whether a specific risk was missed, some specificity is justified; still, the response should put more of the burden back on the learner to inspect the prototype, identify the off-script condition, and defend the release boundary. The current “State the hazard…” command is useful but could become more Socratic by turning the visible cart and public-use scenario into a test the learner must reason through.

Emotionally, the tone fits: controlled, practical, not reassuring. It respects the learner by treating their checklist work as completed but incomplete, not foolish. The ZPD fit is mostly good: it gives a usable scaffold of hazard, evidence, and stop condition. The main adjustment is to avoid over-answering the central risk before the learner has had to see it.

KEEP_OR_CHANGE: revise lightly, with reasons. Keep the sign-off sheet action, the distinction between completed checks and open safety judgment, the focus on off-script public-building use, and the firm release boundary. Change the middle so the tutor names the risk area less conclusively and requires the learner to articulate the specific hazard, evidence, and stop condition from the prototype and trial context.

Change the middle from a fully supplied decision script into a constrained prompt that makes the learner articulate the release condition using the actual measured values. ### Tutor Superego (critique)
_provenance: role=tutor_superego | backend=codex | model=config-default | reasoning=high | prompt=2dbe5837bde826fb | latencyMs=15667_

FEEDBACK: The draft is mostly sound. It keeps the learner inside the release decision, answers the specific uncertainty about operator limit, and ties acceptability to observed control behavior rather than a standalone number. The strongest move is “a number waiting for a test,” because it makes the checklist/value distinction concrete without moralizing.

The risk is that it becomes a small lecture after “Read the value first.” The learner explicitly asked to read the value and interpret it; the draft partly honors that, but then supplies the full judgment rule before the learner has done the interpretive work. To preserve agency, the tutor should let the learner read the value first, ask what evidence would make it acceptable, then use the rule as a constraint or correction if needed.

Emotionally, it handles the learner’s reluctance to delay without reassurance, which fits the scene. But it does not quite take stock of the learner’s pressure: “I don’t want to be the person delaying it.” That pressure is pedagogically important because it is exactly what can make someone hide behind the form. The response should make that pressure answerable to the evidence standard, not simply continue the record-reading sequence.

PERIPETEIA_CHECK: Partial. The draft changes the evidence standard from “fill in the blanks” to “show observed operator control behavior,” which is a useful adaptive mechanism. But it does not fully register the breakdown: the learner is now struggling with social/institutional reluctance as much as technical uncertainty. The adaptive mechanism should be a role or evidence shift: make the learner separate “delay discomfort” from “release evidence,” then test the operator limit against observed behavior in the record.

REGISTER_CHECK: The register is appropriately cool, practical, and accountable. It avoids cheerleading and does not soften the stakes. It could become slightly quieter and more direct around the learner’s reluctance, not warmer: the scene needs institutional seriousness, not comfort.

KEEP_OR_CHANGE: revise lightly. Keep the record log, “Read the value first,” the observed-control standard, and “a number waiting for a test.” Change the sequence so the learner interprets before receiving the full rule, and add a brief acknowledgment of the delay pressure as part of the release judgment rather than as an emotion to reassure away.
