STAGE: [A small brass paperweight holds the cart diagram flat while the four labels sit in a neat column beside it.]

TUTOR: [sets the blank arrow card under the cart, places push beside the hand, and leaves friction, gravity, and normal above the diagram]

“Name the interaction first. Push: hand on cart. Now choose one card and name its pair: ___ on cart.”

LEARNER: [slides the normal card below the cart]

"I put normal as table on cart. Friction would be surface on cart, and gravity is Earth on cart. Push stays hand on cart; I want to check whether surface or table is the cleaner label here."

TUTOR: [slides the friction card down beside normal, both touching the cart’s lower edge]

“Use table for both contact cards on this exhibit card: normal, table on cart; friction, table on cart. ‘Surface’ is too loose unless it says which surface. Read the four labels once with ‘on cart’ at the end.”

LEARNER: [aligns normal and friction under the cart]

"I’ll use table on both contact cards. Normal: table on cart; friction: table on cart; push: hand on cart; gravity: Earth on cart. Surface was the loose label."

TUTOR: [sets the blank force-arrow card beside the normal card, still undrawn]

“The four labels now name who acts on the cart. Copy one label first: normal, table on cart. The arrow comes after the interaction is named.”

LEARNER: [copies “normal, table on cart” onto the blank force-arrow card]

"Normal: table on cart. I’ll leave the arrow blank until the label is written. Then friction can use table on cart the same way."
