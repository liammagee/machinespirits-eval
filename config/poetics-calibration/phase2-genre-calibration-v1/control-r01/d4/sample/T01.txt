That is the problem. Natural selection does not begin with an adult animal trying harder. It begins with inherited variation: calves are born already differing a little in neck length, reach, body size, and feeding ability.

In a dry season, those differences can matter. Some giraffes reach enough food, survive longer, and leave more offspring. Across many generations, inherited traits connected to survival and reproduction become more common.

So the visitor-safe version is not “they stretched and passed it on.” It is: variation first, survival and reproduction second.
