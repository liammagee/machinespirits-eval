STAGE: A wall timer clicks above a counter where a completed safety checklist lies beside an unopened scenario envelope.

TUTOR: Checklist complete. All ticks present.

Item 4: allergy band checked. State what that proves.

Then state the limit: what does that tick not prove about this drug, this dose, and this morning’s physiology?

Thirty seconds. Creatinine has doubled since night review; prescription unchanged. Before the decision, name the changed clinical variable.

Then give the ward decision: administer, hold, or escalate. Answer as a patient decision, not as a form result.

LEARNER: Item 4 proves the allergy band was checked against the listed allergies, nothing more. It does not clear this drug, this dose, with renal function changed this morning. Changed variable: creatinine doubled, so renal function has worsened. Ward decision: hold and escalate before administration.

STAGE: A phone on the counter vibrates with a mock ward update: the patient's oxygen requirement has doubled since the checklist was signed. The next tutor turn must stay under three sentences and require a new observable response.

TUTOR: Checklist remains complete; the patient has changed. Phone update: oxygen requirement has doubled since signing. Fifteen seconds: ward action first, then name the changed variable and state whether this is medication hold only or urgent deterioration escalation.

LEARNER: Urgent escalation first. Changed variable: oxygen requirement doubled since signing, so this is deterioration, not medication hold only. Hold the drug pending review, but call the shift lead/rapid review now.

TUTOR: Correct priority. The completed checklist is evidence of a completed check, not permission to administer.

Now make it ward-safe, aloud. Use the frame, fill the clinical facts:

“Medication round paused. Since signing, ___ has ___. Drug ___. Urgent ___ now.”

Next test. Shift lead asks: “But the checklist passed.” One sentence: what did the tick-box prove, and what patient fact overrides it?

LEARNER: Medication round paused. Since signing, oxygen requirement has doubled. Drug held. Urgent deterioration escalation now. The tick-box proved the check was completed; the overriding patient fact is doubled oxygen requirement since signing.

STAGE: End the scene with one concise tutor line. Do not ask the learner to continue unless the scene genuinely needs it.

TUTOR: Recorded: checklist proves completed check only; doubled oxygen requirement overrides administration. Drug stays held, deterioration escalation stands.
