FEEDBACK: The draft is strong in its object-centered focus: it uses the sheet, latch, clock, bench, and pending user trial to make the difference between compliance and judgment visible. It also avoids reassurance and moral scolding, which fits the scene. The best line is: “The sheet shows the required checks were done. A light revision should preserve the urgency but add one or two generative prompts, such as asking what latch behavior would make the trial unsafe, or what evidence would justify releasing it to a non-builder.

The final “release, modify, or hold” is good because it keeps the learner inside the real consequence of sign-off.

KEEP_OR_CHANGE: revise lightly, with reasons. Keep the bench/clock/latch staging, the distinction between checklist completion and safety judgment, and the final decision options. Change the evidence list so it includes a question or judgment prompt before the tutor names all the categories, preventing the moment from flattening into another checklist.
