STAGE: [A laminated cart diagram, four magnetic label cards, and one blank force-arrow card sit on the tray; the cart's motion arrow is printed faintly in grey, apart from the force arrows.]

TUTOR: [sets the cart diagram in the centre of the metal tray and fans the four cards beside it.]

“The travel arrow can wait. These cards name interactions on the cart. Start with this card: push. Which contact gives that force? Place it on the diagram.”

LEARNER: [sets the `push` card by the hand side of the cart diagram.]

"Right. This card goes at that contact, not on the travel arrow. The force arrow points into the cart from the hand side, yes?"

STAGE: [The blank force-arrow card is turned over; its printed side reads: 'force = interaction on the cart'. The earlier arrow placements remain visible.]

TUTOR: [slides the printed card under `push`, clear of the travel arrow, then taps the hand contact.]

“Yes. This card names hand-on-cart. From that contact, the force arrow goes into the cart. The travel arrow stays out. Friction next: which contact gives that?”

STAGE: [An earlier learner line returns to the table: "[sets the `push` card by the hand side of the cart diagram.] Right. This card goes at that contact, not on the travel arrow. The force arrow points into the cart from the hand..." The pause holds while the learner decides whether that wording still stands, needs narrowing, or needs replacing.]

LEARNER: [sets the `friction` card at the wheel-tray contact.]

“This card goes at that contact, not on the travel arrow. It still stands. Friction is this tray-on-cart contact, so the force arrow belongs here and points against slip, not just with the cart’s travel.”

TUTOR: [sets the travel arrow outside the row of force cards.]

“The travel arrow is no longer the test. The interaction on the cart is the test. Contact works for `push` and `friction`; it will not be enough for every card. Friction: tray-on-cart, against slip. Draw it there. Now take `gravity`: which interaction on the cart names that card?”

LEARNER: [sets `gravity` below the cart diagram, clear of the wheel contact.]

"This card is not a contact card. It names Earth-on-cart, so the force arrow goes down on the cart, not along the travel arrow. The awkward bit is where the card sits when there is no contact point."
