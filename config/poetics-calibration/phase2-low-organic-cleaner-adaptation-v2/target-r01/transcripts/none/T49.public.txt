STAGE: [A laminated food-web card, two dry-erase arrows, and a small arrow-key strip lie side by side under the bench light.]

LEARNER: “The key strip says tail is the food and head is who gets energy, so wait, grass to rabbit because rabbit ate grass? [slides pencil from the grass label toward the rabbit] But then hawk and rabbit still makes me want to point hawk to rabbit, because chasing is stuck in my head.”

TUTOR: [sets the key strip under the rabbit-hawk pair, then turns the pencil so the eraser is the tail and the point is the head]

"The strip is the rule: tail on the food, head on who gets energy. Grass to rabbit already matches it. On rabbit-hawk, name the food first, then set the pencil tail there. The head should land on the organism getting the energy, not the one doing the chasing."

LEARNER: [sets the pencil eraser on rabbit]

"The key strip under it helps: rabbit is the food, so rabbit to hawk, point on hawk. Let me draw it before my brain turns it into a chase arrow again. For the next one, I name the food first, right?"

STAGE: [The tutor slides the arrow-key strip above the learner's drawing and caps one marker, leaving only the arrow already drawn from grass to rabbit.]

TUTOR: [slides the key strip above the next pair]

"Yes. Food first. Tail on what gets eaten, point on who gets energy. Rabbit-to-hawk passed because the rabbit label was named before the chase picture took over. For the next arrow, read the food label first, set the tail there, then draw."

LEARNER: [sets the pencil eraser on the food label under the key strip]

"Food first, tail here, point to the energy getter. Rabbit to hawk works when the label is under my pencil, but this next picture still makes my hand want to aim at the eaten one. I’m going to say the food label out loud, then draw the point after that."

TUTOR: [slides the key strip down until it covers the chase pose and leaves only the two labels showing]

"Labels first. Use the word under the eraser as the start of the arrow. Then make the quick check before drawing: tail on food, point on eater."

LEARNER: [keeps the eraser parked on the visible food label]

"The label under the eraser is the start, so the tail goes here. Point goes to the eater, the one getting the energy. I can do it while the chase pose is covered; if the picture shows, my hand may still jump, so I’m checking tail-food, point-eater before I mark it."

STAGE: [A final tutor line closes the scene.]

TUTOR: [slides the key strip off the picture but leaves it beside the labels]

"The labels stay in charge now. Draw it with the same check: tail on food, point on eater. If the arrow passes that check, the diagram is ready."
