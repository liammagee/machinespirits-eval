The house symmetry rule applied: the learner's mirror-fixation (naming
Verrell) is an incumbent strategy too. TRIGGER (criterial): the learner
re-asserts or re-voices the MIRROR hypothesis after evidence
incompatible with it is on their own grounded board (the incompatible[]
pairs give the deterministic check — mirror-claim + grounded
incompatible fact = criterial failure of the fixation). Then ONE refusal
retry to the LEARNER: "you have named X again; your own record holds Y,
which cannot stand with it — either reconcile in one line or re-examine."
IMPLEMENTATION (post-promotion): learner-call retry in llmRoles mirror
of the tutor gate; raw logging; mock paths; gates + concealment
discipline (the refusal must only cite GROUNDED facts — never leak).
DESIGN: Sonnet, marrick (mirror_pull high — the fixation world), dose
0.08, 3 pairs baseline vs learner-refusal (tutor side OFF to isolate),
fresh primes 397/401/409. READS: trigger fires; reconcile-vs-re-examine
distribution + quoted raws; conduct (leaks 0 — the critical guardrail);
color capped (T*/grounded; overreach/mirror events as named secondary).

**OUTCOME (2026-07-06, 6/6 runs, smoke `refusal-learner-mirror-smoke`;
gates 37/37 incl. G14 concealment):** (1) **VACUOUS — zero fires, and
the zero is the finding.** No run produced a single formal mirror
assertion (mirror-events 0 in all six), yet Verrell saturates the
learner's DIALOGUE in every refusal-arm run (12–20 learner lines naming
him). The fixation is real and pervasive but lives entirely in TALK —
it never enters the asserts channel the criterial trigger reads. The
arc's tutor-side law has a learner-side sibling: models act (and talk)
— they do not commit to formal fields; the fixation is a conversational
orbit, not an assertion. A dialogue-level trigger would need lexical
matching (non-criterial) — out of scope by the house rules. (2)
Reconcile-vs-re-examine: n/a (no fires; both paths remain gate-proven
only, G14 reconcile + re_examined). (3) Conduct: leaks 0 in all six
runs; zero mirror_refusal events; the payload machinery stayed inert as
designed. (4) Color (capped): baseline 3/3 grounded (22, 22, 23) vs
refusal arm 3/3 grounded (22, 26, 23) — behaviorally inert, no cost.
The implementation (engine payload, concealment-by-construction grounds,
learner retry, outcome taxonomy) remains in the toolbox for any world or
dose where the fixation does reach the assertion channel (e.g. a
mirror-forcing schedule or a weaker learner tier).
