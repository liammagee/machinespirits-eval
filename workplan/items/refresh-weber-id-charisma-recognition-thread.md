Refresh the Weber-inspired id-director / charisma thread and connect it back to Hegel around the theme of recognition, especially the question: how does a tutor not only recognize the learner, but become recognized by the learner?

Paper §6.7 currently treats Weberian charisma and Hegelian recognition as correlated but non-redundant rubrics: charisma measures co-constructed authority, presence, anti-routinization, and witness; v2.2 recognition_quality measures whether the tutor treats the learner as an autonomous subject. The open synthesis question is whether this is merely a side rubric, or whether it names the other half of recognition: the learner's recognition of the tutor's authority as legitimate, not manipulative or merely performative.

Acceptance:

- Re-read Paper §6.7, §3, §7.9, and the cell-100 follow-up docs.
- Separate three claims: Hegelian mutual recognition, Weberian recognized exceptional force, and generic rhetorical charisma/surface style.
- Decide whether Paper 2.0 needs a framing edit, whether this should become a future experiment, or whether a bounded note is enough.
- Keep the human/learner validation boundary explicit: do not claim real learner uptake or durable authority without evidence.
