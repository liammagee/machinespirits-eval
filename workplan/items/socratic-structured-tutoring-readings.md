Surfaced by the daily routine (and the 2026-06-22 reference thread). Three
arxiv-verified candidates worth checking against the human-learner pilot design:

- **Hey Chat, Can You Teach Me?** (Tio et al., arXiv:2606.11744) — separates
  curriculum sequencing, Socratic dialogue, and student-state inference over
  extended sessions.
- **Beyond the AI Tutor** (Kumar et al., arXiv:2604.02677) — measures *unassisted*
  post-test gains; multi-agent peers beat single tutors.
- **Elenchus** (Allen, arXiv:2603.06974) — prover–skeptic dialogues; the
  withhold-and-derive pattern.

This is a reading/decision item, not an experiment. Outcome is a yes/no on
whether any of these changes the pilot instrument — recorded on the A1 item.

2026-06-24 Codex: Reviewed the three structured-Socratic sources as A1 inputs. Tio et al. argues extended tutoring needs curriculum sequencing, Socratic dialogue, and student-state inference as separable responsibilities; this supports A1 logging of learner state but does not unblock content/legal gates. Kumar et al. supports social/multi-agent learning and unassisted post-test measurement; A1 already has pre/post and transfer structure, so no immediate instrument change. Allen's Elenchus is a strong withhold-and-derive / prover-skeptic precedent, but it is proof/knowledge-base oriented rather than fractions-pilot instrumentation. Decision: keep these as design precedents, no new A1 task until IRB/consent/items clear.
