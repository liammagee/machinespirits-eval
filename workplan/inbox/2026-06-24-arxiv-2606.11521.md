The verifier-as-counterexample-provider is a formal instantiation of Socratic guided discovery: the tutor withholds the answer and instead supplies the minimal disconfirming evidence that forces the learner to revise. This is precisely the mechanism the poetics rubric measures in the peripeteia dimension (a sudden reversal triggered by new information). The quantitative efficiency gains — comparing self-reflection baseline vs. external verifier feedback — offer a clean experimental template for the recognition vs. placebo cells (e.g. cells 5–6 vs. 15–18).
