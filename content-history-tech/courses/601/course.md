## Description

This course treats learning technologies as objects of historical and philosophical inquiry. Across three lectures we move from the earliest externalizations of memory, through the standardizing force of print, to the contemporary attention economy. The argument is cumulative: each new apparatus does not merely supplement the one before it but reshapes the learner's inner life. By studying the stylus alongside the recommender system, we develop the vocabulary needed to evaluate any pedagogical tool, including the ones currently being sold to us as the future of education.
